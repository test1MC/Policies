Involuntary Termination Overview

The Division VP of Human Resources or the Area Human Resource Manager must be contacted before any employee (both full-time and part-time) is discharged.

If Then

Employees are being terminated by the Company because they do not have the skills necessary to perform the job

They may be given the opportunity to resign in lieu of termination for unsatisfactory work performance.

Note: If any employee is given the opportunity to resign, and chooses to do so, the reason for termination will be entered as Resigned, Mutual Agreement.

An employee is being terminated for gross misconduct or willful unsatisfactory behavior

At the discretion of the Division VP of Human Resources and the terminating manager, the employee may be given opportunity to resign in lieu of termination if such action is deemed to be in the best interest of the Company.

An employee on an approved paid or unpaid leave of absence

Normally, he or she will not be involuntarily terminated unless the employee fails to furnish periodic medical evidence or other required documentation to substantiate the need for a continuing leave of absence, or otherwise violates the terms of the leave of absence.

Store staff must be reduced for economic reasons

Part-time employees will be eliminated before full-time employees hours are reduced or eliminated, provided the full-time employees who are left can perform the work of the part-time employees who were eliminated.

Regular full-time employee is terminated due to closing of a facility
(may or may not include relocating a store depending on the distance of the relocated store to the closed store)

The employee will be paid for earned unused vacation and or personal holidays not taken. Also, the employee will receive pro rata vacation pay and separation pay according to the schedule below, provided the employee stays with the job until no longer needed.
