H1N1 Pandemic Overview

The directives below are intended to maintain a safe workplace and encourage practices to protect the health of employees, customers, and visitors, as well as to ensure the continuity of business operations in the event of severe H1N1 pandemic conditions.

Hygiene Standards

All employees should be reminded to take appropriate hygiene steps to reduce the transmission of both seasonal and H1N1 pandemic influenza in the workplace. This includes washing hands frequently with warm, soapy water, use of alcohol based hand sanitizers, and covering mouths with tissues when sneezing or coughing

Employees Exhibiting Symptoms

  • Employees exhibiting symptoms of H1N1 (fever, cough, sore throat, runny or stuffy nose, body aches, headache, chills, fatigue, diarrhea and/or vomiting) are directed to stay at home and remain out of the workplace until symptoms subside (i.e. they are free of fever without use of fever reducing medications for a 24 hour period). 
  • Employees exhibiting any of the above symptoms in the workplace are to be sent home and directed to remain out of the workplace until symptoms subside (i.e. they are free of fever without use of fever reducing medications for a 24 hours period).  When possible, such decisions should be made by the employee’s supervisor in consultation with the site HR Manager or facility manager.
