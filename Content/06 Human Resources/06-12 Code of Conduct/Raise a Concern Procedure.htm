Raise a Concern Procedure

Step Action
1

Ask questions whenever in doubt about the right course of action. Employees who do not speak up can harm Sherwin-Williams not only by acting inappropriately, but also by keeping silent when unsure what to do, or when they see something wrong. Always speak up if:

  • You are unsure about the proper course of action and need advice
  • You believe that someone acting on behalf of Sherwin-Williams is doing - or may be about to do - something that violates our company’s standards of ethical business conduct or the law.

Note: The obligation to raise a concern about a possible violation of the Code of Conduct, Sherwin-Williams policies, or the law is one of the most important responsibilities each of us has as an employee. Failure to raise a concern can cause significant harm to the health and safety of yourself, your fellow employees, the company, customers, and the communities in which we operate. Failure to raise a concern could also result in the loss of confidence by our customers and shareholders.
2

Choose where to go for help.

Option Use For Action
Supervisor or other Sherwin-Williams employee Legal, ethical, or business conduct issue Inform a supervisor of the issue. You may also reach out to your HR representative, the Legal Department, Internal Audit, or Loss Prevention.
Ethics Point Reporting System

 

Concerns or issue that:

  • You cannot discuss with your supervisor
  • Violations of our accounting practices
  • Securities laws or legal requirements
  • Any issues that you believe should be brought to the attention of the Audit Committee of the Board of Directors

Ethics Point is an independent third party that administers our ethics helpline and web portal.

  • Ethics Point Helpline - call the Ethics Point Helpline. Ethics Point will listen and make a detailed summary of your call. See country-specific phone numbers below.
  • Ethics Point Web Portal - To file a report online, go to sherwin.ethicspoint.com.
Anonymous

Concerns that you wish to report anonymously.

The Helpline or Web portal will assign a tracking number, so that reporters who do not want to give their name can still check back to receive a response or provide more information.

Call the Helpline or access the Web Portal to raise a concern.

Note: Providing your name can often help us look into the matter (see Non-Retaliation). The company will not tolerate retaliation for raising a good faith concern under this Code.

Every effort will be made to give your call a quick response, especially when circumstances make that important. If an investigation is undertaken, we will look into the issue promptly and, whenever called for, see that corrective action is taken.
