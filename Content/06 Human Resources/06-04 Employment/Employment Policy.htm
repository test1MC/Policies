Employment Policy

The District Manager and Store Manager will decide together the optimal staffing and scheduling to maximize the use of service and expense dollars.

  1. Every store normally will be staffed with at least two full-time employees.
    1. In the event that business conditions require additional staffing, it may be necessary to reclassify full-time employees.
  2. The Company will comply with all federal and state labor laws and wage and hour laws.
  3. A store employee must be 18 years old, or the minimum age permissible by the particular state labor laws, whichever is older, to be hired.
  4. Prior to hiring a minor, store managers must obtain approval from the District Manager and Area Human Resources Manager. 
    1. A Work Permit may be required before the minor begins work. Check with the School Principal or Board of Education before hiring an applicant under age 18.  Failure to comply with state child labor laws may result in penalties.
  5. An employee must be at least 18 years of age to drive a Company vehicle.
    1. Any employee using his/her personal car for Company business must be insured at the Company's required insurance limits.
    2. An employee who makes deliveries of hazardous materials in excess of 1,000 lbs. must meet other company policy requirements.
  6. Federal labor laws prohibit any person under the age of 18 from performing hazardous operations, which include, but are not limited to: driving an industrial lift truck (including walk behind or “little Joe”), using a trash compactor, scrap paper baler, box crusher, drum crusher or any other power-driven compressor.
  7. Hour of Service include:
    1. Each hour an employee is paid, or entitled to payment, for the
          performance of work duties
    2. Each hour an employee is paid, or entitled to payment, for
           non-working hours (i.e. vacation, holiday, jury duty, military duty)
    3. Select weeks or periods where part-time employees are
          scheduled to work more than 28 hours in a week due to
          unforeseen staffing or business needs
    4. Should the average hours of service for a part-time employee
           exceed 28 hours, work hours must be reduced in subsequent
           weeks to bring the average  hours to a maximum of 28 hours.
           Refer to the Part Time Hours Worked Report on the SOURCE
           homepage under Store Report. The hours on the report are store
           specific and reflect hours worked at all stores- not just the part
           time employee's home store.

Note: Any questions regarding this policy should be referred to your District Manager and/or Area Human Resources Manager.
