Progressive Misconduct Overview

A first time violation of any of the following rules will not normally result in termination. Cumulative or repeat violations may result in termination; however, depending on the severity of the violation, an employee may be terminated on the first offense for any of the following reasons:

  • Failing to properly record any transaction
  • Failing to immediately enter a transaction into the POS device
  • Falling to report work-related accidents or injuries
  • Failing to comply with the established dress code
  • Failing to improve poor work performance where warnings and/or probation have not been effective in correcting the unsatisfactory performance
  • Selling to over limit or unapproved accounts
  • Handling any of Sherwin-Williams' assets, such as merchandise, money, or any Company property carelessly
  • Using threatening, abusive, insulting, or obscene language toward others
  • Being discourteous to customers
  • Being excessively absent and/or tardy without approval
  • Soliciting or distributing non-work related materials during working time in working areas without permission
  • Engaging in activities that constitute a conflict of interest
  • Being disruptive continuously, which results in the slowing down or the interference of production and/or performance of himself/herself or a fellow employee
  • Interrupting work for personal business, personal telephone calls, or personal visits by or to other employees or third parties to excess
  • Using a cell phone (including phones which are a component of another device such as a PDA) in areas prohibited by law while driving a Company vehicle or on Company business
  • Operating machines, tools or equipment without authorization
  • Remaining in or entering the facility during off duty hours without authorization
  • Garnishments in excess of those permitted by law
  • Non-compliance with Safety and Health Rules: Violating or failing to comply with safety rules. Refusal to follow established safety procedures or to wear required safety equipment. Engaging in any practice which endangers the health and/or safety of himself or other employees, including acts of extreme carelessness or negligence

As in the past, in determining the penalty for an infraction of any of the above, the Company will give due and fair consideration to mitigating and/or aggravating circumstances. The Company is proud of the conduct of the large majority of its employees, and if each of us will consider all our actions in light of the above, compliance with these rules will most certainly follow as a normal course. Inappropriate conduct not specifically described in these rules will be handled as warranted by the circumstances of the case.
