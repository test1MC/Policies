Mediation & Arbitration Overview

Mediation is the preferred method because it provides:

  • Trained, certified, and neutral third-party
  • Means of open communications with or without legal counsel
  • Focus on the parties on their real interests
  • Appropriate, neutral meeting place
  • Discussion of creative solutions

The Company bears the entire cost of the American Arbitration Association (AAA) and the Mediator. Similarly, neither party is required to accept the Mediator’s suggestion; however, if a resolution is agreed upon, an agreement would be signed and is binding.

If the employee and the Company do not agree to mediation or cannot resolve the dispute through mediation, the employee is required to submit the claim to arbitration, in lieu of a judicial proceeding. Although mediation is the preferred method, arbitration offers additional advantages:

  • Privacy and confidentiality
  • Speed
  • Reduced cost
  • Experienced and neutral decision makers
  • Binding resolution

Resolving a claim through arbitration rather than through the court, does not waive the employee’s or the Company’s substantive legal rights; it merely changes the forum in which those rights will be resolved. Sherwin-Williams recommends the employee retain and utilize legal counsel even though it is not required.  The employee will bear his/her own attorney’s charges and the costs of producing evidence, unless applicable law provides for such remedies and the arbitrator imposes them.
