Travel & Entertainment Expenses Policy

  1. Meals of any type cannot be paid by processing a Miscellaneous Cash Disbursement Transaction or store draft. The manager must submit payment for meals on a Travel Expense Report for approval by the District Manager.
  2. Every employee incurring an expense, which is reimbursable by the Company, must submit a Travel and Entertainment Expense Report after each completed business trip, or at least every two weeks, to expedite travel processing.
  3. Expenses not submitted according to policy may be subject to refusal for reimbursement.
  4. Credit card late fees will not be reimbursed, unless special approval from district or division management.
  5. All reports through the end of the reporting month must be submitted for reimbursement on or before the tenth of the following month. If the tenth falls on a weekend, the report is due on the last workday before the tenth. It is the employee's responsibility to complete the report and obtain approval before the due date.
  6. Management must compare required receipts with expenses listed and dates indicated.
  7. Stores are also required to follow the Global Corporate Travel & Expense policy and enter all expenses online through Concur. Please refer to the Corporate Travel & Expense Policy (rev. 1/1/18) for more information.
