Evacuation or Emergency Shutdown Procedure

Maintain constant communication with both your managers and employees throughout this weather event. Exchange home / cell phone numbers to keep in contact. Before starting these procedures, call your Regional Manager.

Note: If not available, call the Region office for the official determination on closing.

Step Action
1 Follow all local law enforcement orders relating to evacuation, road closures, etc.
2 Close POS terminals and complete a backup/day end.
3 Shut down and power off all PC’s & terminals and perform a system shutdown (spin down) of the IBM server.
4 Power off and unplug all printers, fax machines, mix machines, shakers, etc.
5 Unplug and secure the Forklift, pallet jacks, and any loose equipment in the warehouse.
6 Site windows should be lined with poly film on the inside. The film should be held in place with duct tape; X configured from corner to corner then cross (+) configured top to bottom.
7 If freezing temperatures are forecasted, make sure your thermostats are correctly set to avoid freezing pipes.
8 Important records should be boxed, covered with poly film, duct taped, and placed on warehouse shelving.
9 Some facilities are equipped with storm shutters. Stores that are leased properties should contact the landlord in advance to have the shutters installed. Company-owned stores should install the shutters in advance (if available).
10 Wrap the POS, all computers, printers, fax machines, copiers, and other similar business machines in poly film and secure with tape. If you choose to move the PC’s, move to an elevated, secure area away from windows and doors.
11 All cash and checks, including the operating fund should be counted and deposited in the bank. If this is not possible, review the details with your manager. As soon as is practical, make the bank deposit.
12 Elevate metal containers approximately 12 inches off the sales and warehouse floors. Where possible, do the same with sandpaper, masking paper, tape, etc. Secure spray equipment with poly film and duct tape on the warehouse shelving.
13 DO NOT put yourself at risk to become trapped in the branch. EVACUATE when or before the authorities advise you to evacuate the store.
14

At home, consider filling CLEAN empty milk containers with drinking water and storing them in the refrigerator, especially if you have young children.

 

Note: If you don’t have room in your refrigerator and don’t have a second refrigerator, fill the containers anyway. Warm drinking water is better than no drinking water. The more on hand, the better.

15 Ensure that you have a good flashlight and radio (battery or hand crank) and enough batteries for both devices.
16 Immediately report any damage as a result of severe weather to Corporate Risk Management @ 216-566-3068.
