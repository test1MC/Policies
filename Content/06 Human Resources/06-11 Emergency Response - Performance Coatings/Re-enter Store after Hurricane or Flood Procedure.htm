Re-enter Store after Hurricane or Flood Procedure

Step Action
1

Wait until daylight hours to make your first visit back. Partner with another employee, if possible.

Bring the following equipment:

  • Cell phone and car charger
  • Flashlight, headlamp, camera, & batteries
  • PPE & first aid kit
  • Drinking water & food (if necessary)
  • Hand sanitizer & insect repellent

Ensure you have the proper PPE:

  • Required: long pants, long sleeves, gloves, safety glasses, close toe shoes
  • Recommended: hard hat, dust mask, chemical resistant gloves, steel toe shoes, rubber over boots, high visibility vest or makings

Note: Have your driver’s license/ID and a business card on you in case law enforcement asks for it. If approached by media, refer them to Corporate Communications.

2

Ensure that the area around your site has been deemed safe by authorities before approaching it. Check the local police website and Twitter for information.

If any of the following conditions exist, do not re-enter the site:

  • Structural Damage to building
  • Live electrical or gas leak
  • Standing water
  • Significant hazardous material or product releases
  • Physical hazards (blocked egress, falling ceiling, extreme debris, or mud on floor or passages)
  • Pest infestation
  • Severe mold growth or known damage to asbestos material

Instead, ask for assistance and hire contractors, if necessary.

Note: Never drive or walk through high water as it can contain debris or harmful bacteria.

3 Before turning on the lights or activating any electrical equipment, check for leaks or moisture coming from or around switches, outlets, cords, etc. Do not attempt to touch or activate any electrical equipment that is wet or leaking.
4

Check for visible signs of storm damage such as a water line, fallen ceiling tiles, leaks, broken glass, damaged product and equipment, & mold growth.

If possible, take photographs from the outside of the building through doors and windows. Pictures and assessment notes are to be sent to your ASM/RFM, including information below:

  • Location Name/Store #: Power – yes or no
  • Water line on walls, signs of leaks, etc.: Damage (inside, outside, both, etc.)
5 Look for animals and insects that may have sought higher ground on shelves or in vehicle wheel wells.
6 Look for spilled product and contain if possible.
7 Be aware of suspicious individuals or activity near the store. Never confront anyone, but call law enforcement authorities.
8 When in doubt about any re-entry procedure, reach out and call area leadership or specific divisional leaders from the Crisis & Emergency Contacts.
