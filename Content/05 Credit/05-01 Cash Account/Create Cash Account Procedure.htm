Create Cash Account Procedure

When creating a cash account, the process MUST be either completed or voided in order for the new account to be accessed immediately.  The new account number will NOT appear in the system until the following day unless a customer lookup to Cleveland is performed, which may take up to fifteen (15) minutes to appear in the search results. For more information on preferred customer enrollment, please see Preferred Customer Program Enrollment Overview

Note: On occasion, there can be very short periods of time when the mainframe is being rebooted, usually about ten minutes on a Sunday, when a new cash account cannot be created. Use either a Manual POST Form to gather the customer information and process the sale or wait 10 to 15 minutes and try again.

Creating new account from Source (District Offices Only)

From the POS desktop, select Browser -> Access the Source homepage -> Source Operations -> Credit -> Cash Account Processing -> Enter customer details

Note: The availability of the new cash account creation screen will align with CPR edit availability (usually unavailable between 8pm an 7am EST)

Note: When creating a cash account, always be sure to review the local or headquarters database to avoid account duplication.
2

From POS desktop, select Store -> Select either Point of Sale (for POS), Customer Order Entry (for COE), Manager Functions (for PRC), or Miscellaneous (for Wallcovering) -> Press LOOKUP [F3] when prompted to enter the customer’s account number. 

3

Search the local database.  Enter the customer's last name when prompted. 

Example: If you originally entered [SMITH] and you can't find the customer's name, try entering [S].

4

Search the headquarters database.  Press LOOKUP [F3] twice more.  You will need to provide Area Code or Zip Code to search the HQ database. Press ACCEPT [F1].

Note: If the system cannot find a match on the headquarters database, begin new customer add.  If matches are found, press LOOKUP [F3] to begin the new customer addition.

5

Select a Customer Type:  DIY (DIY), Product Finishes (CC), and Employee (EMP).

6

Complete the New Cash Account Customer Add Screen. 

Note: Refrain from using any punctuation other than hyphens or ampersand [&]. Any other use of punctuation (including periods, commas, apostrophes, slashes, etc.) creates inaccuracy in the alphabetization process and name search capability in the A/R Database.

7

Enter Name.

Note: Use complete first and last names for individuals. Do not abbreviate or use nicknames. If opening a cash account in a business name, enter the entire business name in the last name field.  The business name can contain up to 23 alpha/numeric characters.

8

Enter E-mail Address

Note: Always ask for an email address so SW can inform the customer about upcoming sales, HowTo information, and special promotions. Ensure the customer that the Sherwin-Williams' policy is never to provide customers' personal information to other companies or individuals without express permission.

9

Enter Address

Note: If the BillTo address may be used as a ShipTo address, Taxware will validate the City, State, and Zip Code combination. The City name must be spelled out completely.  If you have having difficulty determining the correct Zip Code, refer to the United States Postal Service website Zip Code Locator.

10

Enter the Territory

Note: New cash accounts default to the house territory, 0100. If the default territory number is changed when the cash account is initially set up, it will be overridden to 0100 when added to the Accounts Receivable Database.

11

12

Do not change the default of No in the DCP Field.

13

Enter the Sales Volume.

Note: The Volume Tag corresponds to the annual total potential sales volume for all product types [paint, sundries, wallcovering, floorcovering]. The total potential sales volume includes both the amount the customer is likely to spend with SW and the amount the customer may spend with competitors. This potential volume will control what pricing the customer can get based on volume classes. New accounts may be set up with a Volume Class of A-C without limitation. Requests for accounts in the D and E volume class will require District Manager approval.
