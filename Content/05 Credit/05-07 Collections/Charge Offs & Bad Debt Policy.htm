Charge Offs & Bad Debt Policy

A charge-off is an account's receivable balance that has been removed from the active Store Trial Balance and expensed to the store because collection is doubtful.  Accounts are charged-off as a result of: customer death, bankruptcy, skip, or any other inability to pay.  All charge-off accounts are maintained in the bad debt ledgers at the National Credit Center in Cleveland. The Store Computer System will not allow charge activity to bad debt accounts or deleted accounts. These accounts are not reflected as part of the store's assets.

  1. All accounts in the 91+ column of the Store Trial Balance will be subject to charge-off monthly. Only the District Credit Office may make exceptions.
  2. The District Credit Manager informs the store of the accounts which are scheduled to be charged off. Bankrupt and known loss accounts are written off each month.
  3. No further credit is to be extended to a bad debt account.
  4. After a payment is received from a bad debt account, the payment will be credited to the individual store that experienced the write-off. 
  5. Once an account is set for bad debt lockout, no further company charge transaction activity can be performed.
