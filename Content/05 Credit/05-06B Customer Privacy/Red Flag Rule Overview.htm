RED FLAG RULE ANNOUNCEMENT LETTER

The Sherwin-Williams Company has implemented an Identity Theft Prevention Program designed to detect the warning signs or “red flags” of identity theft in their day-to-day operations, take steps to prevent the crime, and mitigate the damage it inflicts. By identifying red flags in advance, we will be better equipped to spot suspicious patterns when they arise and take steps to prevent a red flag from escalating into a costly episode of identity theft.  The Red Flags Rule is enforced by the Federal Trade Commission (FTC), the federal bank regulatory agencies, and the National Credit Union Administration.

To better protect our customer’s personal information and prevent identity thefts from occurring please follow these guidelines when completing these functions:

Function Guidelines

Charge Sale Processing
  • If the person(s) requesting you to charge a sale to an SW account are unknown to you, ask for identification so you can validate that they are authorized to charge to the account.
  • If it is a commercial account and you are uncertain, contact the Business Office for verification.

Opening A New Charge Account
  • Take reasonable steps to verify the person(s) and or business applying for credit is who they say they are:
    • Ask for picture identification.
    • Confirm the address.
    • In addition to a PO Box, a street address is also required. 
    • If a sales representative brings in a credit application from a customer, confirm with them that customer verification was made.
