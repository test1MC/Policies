Identity Theft Overview

If you suspect identity theft, get all the details and contact your Credit Office. Some examples of red flags that may indicate an identity theft has occurred are:

  • A customer’s mailed invoices and/or statements are returned as undeliverable. In this case, attempt to contact the customer to find out why this is occurring and, if necessary, obtain updated address information. If you are unable to contact the customer, contact your Credit Office to advise them that mail has been returned.
  • Mail sent to the customer is returned repeatedly as undeliverable although transactions continue to be processed to the account.
  • You are notified by a third party such as a consumer reporting agency, the consumer himself or herself, or a law enforcement agency that identity theft has either occurred or is a possibility. If this occurs, immediately contact your Credit Office.
  • You are notified by a customer, law enforcement agency, or another person that the store has opened a fraudulent account for a person engaged in identity theft. If this occurs, immediately contact your Credit Office.
  • Sherwin-Williams is notified of unauthorized charges or transactions in connection with a customer’s account.
  • Shortly following the notice of a change of address for an account, there is a request for the addition of authorized users on the account.
  • An applicant fails or refuses to provide personal identification when asked.
  • Documents provided to prove identification appear suspicious:
    • Documents appear to be altered or forged.
    • Photographic identification is inconsistent with the appearance of the applicant or customer.
    • An application appears to have been altered or forged or appears to have been destroyed and reassembled.
    • Personal identifying information is not consistent with personal identifying information that is on file with Sherwin-Williams.

Always report any incident or suspected incident of identity theft to your Credit Office. The Credit Office will determine what the next steps should be.
