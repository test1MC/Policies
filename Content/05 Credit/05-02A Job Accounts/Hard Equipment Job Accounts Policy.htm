Hard Equipment Job Accounts Policy

Hard equipment sales are for the purchase of costly items such as spray equipment or scaffolding. Sherwin-Williams makes extended payment plans available for hard equipment purchases. A commercial account customer whose line of credit will not cover the cost of the hard equipment purchase or who wants extended payment terms may qualify for a separate hard equipment job account.  The hard equipment account allows customers to space out their payments over a set period of time. Contact the District Credit Office/Service Center for current available plans.

  1. TAG Locations/Protective Coatings Locations:  The following policies apply to hard equipment sales NOT made against a special Hard Equipment Job Account (i.e. made against a customer’s main account with no extended payment terms):
    1. Payment Terms are Net 20th of month following purchase for commercial accounts.
    2. Accounts must be in good standing.
    3. The sale must not exceed the customer’s credit limit.
    4. The serial number must be noted on the invoice.
  2. Automotive Branches: Accounts must be current and within their assigned credit limit to be considered for financing.
  3. Hard equipment plans are available ONLY to commercial accounts.
  4. Hard equipment may NOT be sold on Painter/Contractor Job Accounts or Joint Check Accounts. 
  5. Use only the established job number for the hard equipment sale to process the sale; do not use job 01.
  6. Purchases made on a Hard Equipment Job may require a minimum down payment (amount to be determined by District Credit Office/Service Center/Division Credit Office) and specific documents that provide for title retention.
  7. The District Credit Office/Service Center/Division Credit Office must pre-approve all hard equipment sales on extended terms before the store can process the invoice and release the merchandise.
  8. A Sales Contract and Security Agreement must be complete for a sale made on a Hard Equipment Job Account. 
  9. TAG Locations/Protective Coatings Locations:  A hard copy for TAG locations of the Sales Contract and Security Agreement form is only provided by the local district credit office. Forward the original form to the District Credit Office/Service Center and retain a copy for the customer file. 
  10. Canadian Locations:  Include a postdated cheque or a signed credit card agreement form with the signed contract.
  11. Automotive Branches: The Sales Contract & Security Agreement– Automotive is available in the Forms Repository. 
  12. US Locations Only:  If directed to do so by the District Credit Office/Service Center/Division Credit Office, complete the Uniform Commercial Code Financing Statement for sales made on a Hard Equipment Job Account. The District Credit Office/Service Center/Division Credit Office will provide directions on obtaining a Uniform Commercial Code Financing Statement. Forward the original document to the District Credit Office/Service Center/Division Credit Office and retain a copy for the customer file.
  13. If required to complete the Uniform Commercial Code Financing Statement, use prime-sub 7599-099 to process the Miscellaneous Disbursement Transaction to pay for the UCC Filing.
  14. The serial and model numbers of all hard equipment items, plus a complete description, must be recorded on the invoice forms, Sales Contract and Security Agreement, and Uniform Commercial Code Financing Statement for all hard equipment plans. The customer must sign all the above documents.
