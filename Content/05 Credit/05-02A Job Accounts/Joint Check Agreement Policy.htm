Joint Check Agreement Policy

This section does not apply to Automotive Branches

  1. The customer must have a commercial account in good standing prior to establishing a joint check agreement.
  2. The District Credit Office/Service Center must approve all joint check agreements.
  3. A  Request for Special Credit Approval Form must be prepared for requesting an account.
  4. The job account established must be used for all purchases and payments for that job only. Do NOT use a job account for hard equipment purchases.
  5. A Sherwin-Williams' Joint Check Agreement Form (Joint Check Agreement Form:  W Power of Attorney-Notary/Joint Check Agreement – Canada) must be signed by all parties involved (subcontractor, general contractor, or principal contractor) and witnessed or notarized. In some cases, a letter from the General Contractor or Property Owner will be acceptable. Discuss this with the District Credit Office/Service Center.
  6. Cash payment to the contractor for their share of a joint check is not permitted. Payment must be made by store draft (0300-003), only after the amount has been discussed and approved by the District Credit Office/Service Center. The store draft should be issued to the name of the other party to which the joint check is issued.
  7. The District Credit Manager has the authority to withhold payment to a painter if doubt exists as to the worthiness of the check. After verifying the check, the District Credit Manager will contact the store with instructions for processing.
  8. Under no circumstances may the store endorse the joint check and return it to the painter, without prior approval from the District Credit Manager. Such approval may be given verbally by the District Credit Office/Service Center, but must be followed up with written approval to the store.
