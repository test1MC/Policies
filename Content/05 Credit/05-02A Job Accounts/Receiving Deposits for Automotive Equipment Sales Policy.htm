Receiving Deposits for Automotive Equipment Sales Policy

Applies ONLY to Automotive Division, United States and Canada

  1. A deposit is required for all equipment sales.
    1. Cash account – 100% of the total equipment sale (sale price, tax, and freight) must be received as a deposit upfront.
    2. Reference the chart below for information about how to handle the different account deposits:

    If

    Then

    Cash Account

    Customer must present check(s) to the branch and they must be processed via a deposit transaction. The check(s) must clear prior to ordering the merchandise from the vendor.

    Charge Account

    Deposit transactions must be processed once confirmed the customer has sufficient credit available for the sale.

    Deposit received via another form of payment (i.e. wire transfer)

    Contact General Accounting for assistance.
  2. Charge account deposits must be received from the charge account customer using the following schedule:
    1. Contact the Automotive Credit Department to verify a charge account customer has sufficient credit for the sale. If the charge customer requests financing via a lease, the lease must go through the approved leasing company, Survivor Financial Services. Refer to Booth Lease Policy
    2. 40% of the total sale as a down payment
    3. 60% of the total sale once the equipment has been shipped. The total sale includes the total selling price, taxes, and freight charges.

Note: For more information on how to process a deposit, refer to Deposit Processing Overview.
