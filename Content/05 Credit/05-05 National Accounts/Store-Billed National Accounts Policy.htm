Store-Billed National Accounts Policy

A national account customer is a customer that buys for, directs, or influences from a central office many geographically dispersed units and has a substantial overall potential for the purchase of paint, floorcovering, and wallcovering.

  1. Firms who have $500,000 in annual purchases, multiple locations, and substantial purchase potential for either paint, wallcovering, or floorcovering are prospects for national account buying agreements.
  2. Large paint contractors who do work across the country or in multiple locations may also qualify for national account status.
  3. The term "national account" is reserved solely for those companies who have been designated as such and agreed to Sherwin-Williams’ national account purchasing arrangements.
  4. Cleveland National Accounts Department makes the final decision regarding whether or not a company will be considered a national account.
    1. The headquarters of a national account approves our National Account Agreement. Credit approval for national accounts is initially established by the National Accounts Department. The credit limit is ultimately the responsibility of the District Credit Office.
    2. Stores are assigned to service the various locations of the national account.
    3. When a new national account is established, or noteworthy changes to an existing account occur, notification to all involved Sherwin-Williams locations is made.
  5. National account price record cards are maintained by the Cleveland National Accounts Department on a centralized pricing server.
  6. The highest price charged a national account will be the published National Account Price List.
  7. Exception pricing will be used for commodity type products used regularly by each account.
  8. All sales to national accounts remain on store books and are managed through the District Credit Office. The store making the sale retains 100% sales credit.
  9. Stores are provided with an alphabetical listing of national and regional accounts each January. The National and Regional Account List provides account affiliations and the respective Account Executive and their telephone number, if the account is centrally priced or receives a marketing commission.
