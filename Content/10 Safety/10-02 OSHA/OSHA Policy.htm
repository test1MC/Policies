OSHA Policy

  1. Each building must be marked with an approved Exit sign and be free of any obstacles.
  2. Illuminated exit signs, which are required by OSHA Life Safety Standards, are required to be posted over each emergency pedestrian doorway.
  3. All fire extinguishers must be located in easily accessible, well-marked places, and all employees must be trained in their use. Once a month, visually check all pressure gauges and have them professionally tested to ensure they are in good working order. Check with the local fire department for test requirements.
  4. All transfer, intermixing, or blending of flammable and combustible liquids must occur inside the Product Service Room (PSR). Specific exemptions may be granted in writing from the Division Loss Prevention on consultation with the Safety and Environmental Compliance Department.
  5. Display No Smoking signs permanently and prominently throughout the store.
    1. Tactfully ask customers to obey the “no smoking” regulation.
    2. Provide a safe area away from flammable materials where employees can smoke.
    3. Ensure smoking materials are discarded separately from other store trash.
    4. As implied, smoking, or allowing customers to smoke, anywhere on store property, including, but not limited to, sales floor, manager’s office, warehouse, restrooms, and anywhere flammable materials are stored, is subject to immediate termination.
  6. A qualified electrical inspector must check the store’s wiring whenever questions arise. Never allow store personnel to install permanent wiring.
  7. Copies of any local ordinances, which can be obtained from the local city/township office, must be kept on file at the store.
  8. The Store manager must:
    1.   Ensure that the store meets all local, OSHA, and Sherwin-Williams safety codes as presented in LiveSafe.
    2. Communicate any recommendations submitted by an outsider inspector or auditor regarding a store’s safety standards to Division Loss Prevention.
    3. Know the location of the local fire marshal and the building inspector.
    4. Be responsible for seeing that the required OSHA poster and all Sherwin-Williams safety posters are prominently displayed in the store.
    5. Inform all employees of safety codes and procedures described in the
    6. LiveSafe Administrator’s manual and that they must complete the Quarterly Safety Training.
    7. Ensure that the store has an easily accessible, approved first-aid kit.
    8. Strictly enforce all safety procedures necessary for dispensing and storing flammable materials.
    9. Immediately inform the Division Director of Loss Prevention and District Manager whenever a fire occurs.
    10. Report any cited violations, OSHA, EPAor DOT inspections to Division Loss Prevention and Stores Group Safety and Environmental Compliance Department at 216-566-1710.
