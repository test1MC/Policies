Chemical & Controlled Substance Policy

Sales of certain chemicals in the United States are controlled by the Federal Controlled Substances Act while chemicals in Canada are controlled by the Consolidated Statutes of Canada: Controlled Drugs and Substance Act Schedule VI (Sections 2,6,55, and 66) and the Precursor Control Regulations (SOR/2002-359).

  1. All US locations must implement and adhere to the following policies regarding recording and reporting sales:
    1. Recording and reporting requirements pertain to all Sherwin-Williams stores.
    2. All sales of fifty (50) gallons or more of the chemicals named above, in one transaction, must include the following information: Name and Address of Purchaser, Date, Product Type, Quantity of Product, Method of Delivery, Identification Information (Photo I.D.), Phone Number.
    3. Reporting and recording is not required if these products are included in a blend together or with other products.
    4. Sales to individuals or cash purchases must consist of at least a signature, driver's license and one other form of identification. New customers must have proper company identification and representative verification before any transaction begins.
    5. Unusually large sales of these chemicals to non-regular customers or those paying in large amounts of cash must be reported to Division Loss Prevention.
    6. Unusual orders of these chemicals requesting delivery to a new or non-regular customer should be reported to Division Loss Prevention, prior to shipment.
  2. All Canadian locations must implement and adhere to the following additional policies regarding recording and reporting sales:
    1. Recordkeeping is required for all activities involving Acetone, MEK and/or Toluene (as well as all other Class B precursors listed in Schedule VI of the Controlled Drugs and Substances Act). The below requirements only apply to pure Class B precursors and blended preparations or mixtures containing more than 30% of Class B precursors, by weight or volume. 
    2. Businesses are only required to record (1) the name of the precursor (or the brand name if a preparation or mixture), (2) the type of activity (i.e., sale, delivery, export), (3) the date of each activity, (4) the quantity sold and (5) the name and address of the purchaser, but only if the business is exporting the precursor.
    3. Records must be made available to the Canadian Department of Health at any time, upon the Department’s request.
    4. In the event of a loss or theft, stores are required to make a verbal report to the police within 24 hours of a suspected loss or theft and written report to the Canadian Office of Controlled Substances within 72 hours.
    5. An exemption certificate is required to produce, package, sell, provide, import, and/or export blended preparations or mixtures containing more than 30% Class B precursors. This certificate is maintained centrally in Sherwin-Williams Regulatory Department.
  3. All locations must implement and adhere to the following policies:
    1. Report all local health, safety, or environmental agency audits regarding chemicals or controlled substances immediately to Stores Group Safety & Environmental at storesgroupsafety@sherwin.com and Sam Skubak at sam.r.skubak@sherwin.com.
    2. Should you be audited by your local health, safety, or environmental agency and requested to produce a statement of our policy regarding the sale of controlled substances, please print and provide the Sherwin-Williams Controlled Substance Sales Policy. 
    3. This policy statement may also be shared with customers. 
    4. IMPORTANT:  Do NOT print the Policies & Procedure Manual or provide any non-Sherwin-Williams employee access to the manual.

Note: These policies are applicable when a customer purchases Acetone, Methyl Ethyl Ketone (MEK), and Toluene.
