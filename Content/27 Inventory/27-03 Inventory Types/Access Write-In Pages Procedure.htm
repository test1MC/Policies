Access Write-In Pages Procedure

Write-in pages MUST be completed on the day of inventory. The write-in pages will be uploaded to Cleveland through polling along with all the other counts recorded into the Store Computer System.

The only products that can be recorded on write-in pages are:

  • Paint Intermix
  • Phoenix Products
  • Powder Coatings
  • Spray Equipment Rental Units
  • All other products must have a valid sales number and be recorded on the Store Computer System

Step

Action

1

From the POS Desktop -> Main Menu -> Merchandise Management -> Inventory Control -> Inventory -> Record Write-Ins.

Note: You will be asked to enter your Employee Number and Password.

2

The following choices are available:

  • Current Merchandise
  • Obsolete Merchandise
  • Ready-To-Go Merchandise
  • Rental Equipment

Note: Ready-To-Go Merchandise does not apply to Automotive Division.

Important Note: You may not include a product on write-in pages simply because a sales number cannot be found or has not been established. Identify these scenarios prior to November 1st, so that the sales number can be set up.

For Automotive Branches:

  • Currently there are no Automotive product types that require the use of write in pages. If you have an item that you think needs to be included on write-in pages, consult with the Divisional Inventory Contacts (provided in your Inventory Packet). If they determine that the item should be listed as a write-in, they will provide you with an inventoriable class number. Follow the Write-In procedures and ensure that the write-in pages have been completed and entered before closing inventory on November 1st.
  • You may not include a product on write-in pages simply because a sales number has not been established yet. If a sales number has not been setup for a product, it should be manually counted on November 1, and then forwarded to your Area Sales Manager to include as post inventory Physical Inventory Adjustment form (PIA).
