Write In Landed Unit Cost Overview

  • The unit cost must be entered while recording write-ins of class controlled products.
  • The recorded cost must include the product cost and any additional costs, such as freight, handling, and other charges.
  • If the quantity on hand does not exceed the last quantity purchased, use that unit cost.
    • Example: You counted 25 units for inventory. The last time you purchased this product you got 30 units at a cost of $3.00 per unit. You would use the $3.00 per unit cost for you inventory cost.
  • If the quantity on hand exceeds the last quantity purchased use the First-In First-Out (FIFO) costing method.
    • Example: You counted 117 units for inventory. The last time you purchased this product you got 100 units at a cost of $3.95 per unit. Since your quantity on hand exceeds your last quantity purchased you will use the FIFO costing method.
