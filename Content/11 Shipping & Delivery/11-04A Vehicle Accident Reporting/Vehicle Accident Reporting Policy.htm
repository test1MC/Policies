Vehicle Accident Reporting Policy

  1. At the earliest possible moment, the driver of the vehicle transporting hazardous materials must notify the District Manager and the Division Loss Prevention Director (Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service drivers also need to contact Dispatch) if involved in an incident in which
    1. A person is killed or receives injuries requiring hospitalization
    2. There is property damage in excess of $50,000
    3. The evacuation of the public is required
    4. The closure of a facility or a major transportation artery is required
  2. A Loss, Offense and Incident Report must be completed immediately after the incident.  For more information, refer to 25-01 Loss Prevention.
  3. Notify Fleet Response per the insurance packet provided annually by Corporate Risk Management to all leased vehicle drivers.
  4. Notify Division Loss Prevention and Stores Environmental (216-566-1710) immediately if the incident involves the spill or release of hazardous materials.
  5. Emergencies only (critical injuries or significant property damage): Contact Fleet Response at 1-855-373-1763.
  6. An accident can be defined as any incident in which a vehicle is involved (whether in motion, temporarily stopped or parked) that result in property damage and/or personal injury regardless of who was hurt, what property was damaged, or who was responsible. Objects which fall off of and/or out of the store/ Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service vehicle, including paint, which cause damage to any other property also, constitutes an accident.

Accident Type

Procedure

Vehicle Repair
  • Depending on the condition of your fleet vehicle, Fleet Response will either direct you to take your vehicle to the Fleet Response designated repair facility or have it towed to one. If at all possible, you will be referred to a shop that uses Sherwin-Williams Automotive paints.
  • After Fleet Response audits the estimate, the repair facility will contact you regarding a repair appointment. Remove all company property and personal belongings from the vehicle. The repair facility is not responsible for lost or stolen property.
  • If your vehicle is not repairable, Fleet Response will handle the disposal of the damaged vehicle. For any and all questions during the repair process, contact Fleet Response at 1-855-373-1763, option 1.
  • Inspect repairs before accepting vehicle. The repair shop will bill Fleet Response. Call Fleet Response immediately if there are any problems with the repair of your vehicle.

Injury
  • Minor injuries should be reported by the driver to his/her Supervisor as soon as possible. The Supervisor will instruct the driver on proper reporting procedures.
  • When reporting a serious injury or fatality, Fleet Response will immediately contact the appropriate person at Sherwin-Williams Corporate Risk Management Department.

Subrogation
  • When another party is at fault for the incident, Fleet Response will contact the other party and/or their insurance company as Sherwin-Williams’ representative to recover the repair costs and all other expenses associated with the incident. Please cooperate fully with Fleet Response if they need any additional information while subrogating the claim. 

Rental Vehicle
  • If a rental vehicle is needed while accident repairs are being done, contact Fleet Response at 1-855-373-1763, option 1. This item is handled as a business expense and not covered under the insurance program. Fleet Response will arrange for billing through Corporate.

Glass Only
  • Glass replacement is handled by LeasePlan’s Glass Department at 1-855-373-1763, option 2.

Personal Property
  • Personal property stolen or damaged from your company vehicle should be reported to your homeowner’s insurance carrier.
