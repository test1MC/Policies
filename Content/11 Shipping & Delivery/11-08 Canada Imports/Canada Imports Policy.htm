Canada Imports  Policy

Certain information is required at the time of import and must be accurate. If any documentation is incorrect, the result can be returned shipments, shipment delays, product seizures, excessive duty charges, fines and penalties to the division. Documentation is provided by the supplier. Purchasers must communicate the appropriate resquirements to their suppliers.

  1. A properly completed Commercial Invoice is required for all import shipments to Canada.
  2. A North American Free Trade Agreement Certificate of Origin (NAFTA) is required to substantiate the claim for the preferential duty treatment under the North American Free Trade Agreement for shipments from the US or MX.
    1. In absence of the NAFTA certificate, the receiving Canadian site may be charged for import duties.
  3. Prior to placing an order with a foreign unrelated supplier, employees must provide Appendix A: Foreign Unrelated Supplier Instructions for Shipping in Canada to the supplier.
  4. Supplier must acknowledge in writing that they have read and understand the instructions.
