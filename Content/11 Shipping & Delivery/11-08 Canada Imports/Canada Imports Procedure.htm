Canada Imports Procedure

Step

Action

1

Ensure new suppliers are provided with Appendix A: Foreign Unrelated Supplier Instructions for Shipping in Canada.

Note: Supplier must acknowledge in writing that they have read and understand the instructions.

2

Inform the supplier of the following Commercial Invoice requirements:

  • SW part number (SKU [Fill rex + size code], RM #, or SMIS)
  • Manufacturers/suppliers part number (or other product identifier, if available)
  • Harmonized Tariff Schedule (HTS) Number
  • Detailed product description (Ex: use “Water Based Latex Paint” instead of “paint”)
  • Country of manufacture
  • Foreign supplier, if applicable
  • Ship To Name and Address, including Store Number
  • Purchaser’s name and address (if different from Ship To location)
  • Currency (USD or CAD)
  • Quantities (for each item)
  • Weight for all items; Weight and Volume for all liquids
  • Values (per unit price, line item total, and invoice total)

Note: For further explanation and any other data elements required by CBSA – see Appendix A - Foreign Supplier Instructions for Shipping into Canada.

3

Suppliers may provide a North American Free Trade Agreement Certificate of Origin (NAFTA), which is found in the Forms Repository, for items originating in the US, Canada or Mexico. This NAFTA Certificate of Origin must be sent along with the shipment. 

Note: A new NAFTA certificate can be provided with every shipment or a blanket NAFTA certificate can be used. Both the Commercial Invoice and the NAFTA must list accurate part numbers.

4
