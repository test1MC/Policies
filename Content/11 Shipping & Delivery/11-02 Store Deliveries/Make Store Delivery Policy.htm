Make Store Delivery Policy

The below policies and procedures are intended for drivers and employees making deliveries on behalf of the Sherwin-Williams Company:

  1. An employee must be at least 21 years of age to drive a Sherwin-Williams vehicle.
  2. Employees must complete Driver Safety Training before taking a delivery (for more information: from POS desktop, select Browser -> Access Source homepage -> LiveSafe -> Transportation & Driver Safety -> Sherwin-Williams Delivery Services & Store Driver Safety Training).
  3. Stores and/or Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service Drivers making deliveries must be aware of and adhere to all policies regarding Hazardous Materials Shipping Overview as outlined in Hazardous Materials Shipping Policy.
  4. Only store vehicle drivers with valid commercial driver’s licenses and current hazardous materials endorsements are qualified to transport hazardous materials in quantities of 1000 lbs. or more.
  5. Sherwin-Williams drivers' truck appearance and personal appearance are expected to be professional at all times.
  6. Adhere to all HR policies regarding personal dress and grooming.
  7. Trucks should be neat and clean inside and out.
  8. Any inspection, citation, traffic ticket, visit, or any correspondence from federal, state or local transportation enforcement agencies must be reported to the District Manager and the Division Loss Prevention Director immediately.
  9. Drivers in districts participating in the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service are not permitted to carry cash.
  10. Drivers in districts participating in the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service are required to utilize the GPS Navigation feature on their mobile devices.
  11. Before beginning a delivery route, the driver is required to perform a vehicle inspection covering all points noted on the Daily Vehicle Inspection Form found in the Forms Repository. Search for Form Name: “vehicle” / Form Category: Safety
  12. Before leaving a store or customer job site with a pick-up, the driver and the store employee or customer must verify the piece count and any COD amounts to be collected.
  13. Drivers are not to use phone or text while in motion. Only when the vehicle is at a full stop can they place a call or text.
  14. Smoking on or about any motor vehicle, during the operation of said vehicle, loading, unloading, or delivery of product is strictly forbidden.
  15. Never compromise your safety or the safety of others- do not drive an unsafe vehicle and do not drive unsafely. 
  16. The driver must take the following paperwork and handle accordingly when making deliveries:
    1. Two copies of the customer order Confirmation of Delivery Ticket (customer orders only when not delivered through the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service), one of which should be signed by the customer and returned to the store originating the delivery and one of which is provided to the customer.
    2. Two copies of the IST Transaction (when applicable), one of which is to be signed by the receiving store and returned to the transferring store and one which should be provided to the receiving store.
    3. Bills-of-Lading must accompany all deliveries. The person transporting the material is responsible for signing the Bill-of-Lading and accepting responsibility for the shipment [Store Truck Driver, Third Party Carrier Driver, Store Employee transporting goods, etc.]. The signed copy of the Bill-of-Lading must remain with the store, customer, or vendor requesting the pick-up.  One copy must remain with the driver while completing the route and one copy is provided to the customer as a courtesy. For more information see Bill-of-Lading Application Overview.
    4. In the rare case that a customer has requested a copy of the invoice with the shipment, process the sale transaction before the driver leaves. Provide the driver with the customer copy of the invoice and the store copy of the Confirmation of Delivery (if required). Invoicing and Delivery must take place in the same business day, if delivery does not take place, a return transaction must be processed.
  17. Drivers in stores NOT participating in the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service are also required to take a copy of the Store Delivery Schedule with them to assist in completing their route.
  18. Drivers should take the following into consideration when securing their load:
    1. Flammable liquids shall not be loaded or unloaded from any motor vehicle unless the vehicle's engine is turned off. The only exception is if the operation of a tailgate lift requires such a condition.
    2. All packaging not permanently attached to the store truck or van shall be secured against movement within the vehicle. Containers must be braced in a manner to prevent relative motion between containers. Packages must be sufficiently secured to prevent any forward, backward, or lateral movement.
    3. Palletized freight on which packages are banded, strapped or shrink-wrapped, are considered secured.
    4. Hazardous materials shall not be loaded onto a transport vehicle unless the vehicle is securely set to prevent motion of the vehicle during loading or unloading.
  19. Drivers should be aware of the following policies regarding hazardous shipments:
    1. Sherwin-Williams' Employees cannot deliver over 1000 pounds of hazardous material with their personal vehicle.
    2. When hazardous materials in quantities exceeding 1000# are delivered, the delivery vehicle must show the appropriate DOT placards on all four sides.
    3. All store vehicles used for the transportation of hazardous materials in excess of 1000# must have:
    4. "Attention Driver" labels affixed to the dashboard
    5. 10BC Fire extinguisher
    6. Disabled vehicle warning devices
    7. An Emergency Response Guidebook (2012 or later)
    8. Company Identification
    9. A U.S. DOT Identification Number
    10. Proof the vehicle passed annual DOT inspection or state equivalent
  20. Drivers participating in the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service should be aware of these additional policies:
    1. Pick-ups and deliveries must be made in the route order. Only complete stops on the route
    2. Do NOT pick up any orders from a store that are not part of your route.
    3. Call Dispatch if you have an emergency with your vehicle or are delayed so that they may re-route your stops.
    4. Drivers are required to use GPS to route deliveries. Follow the directions but be prepared to find an alternate route when your vehicle exceeds bridge allowances or when the suggested road forbids hazmat transport.
    5. It is recommended that the mobile device Route Type setting be set to Quickest Route. To verify your mobile device settings, please refer to Optimizing Route Settings on the MC-75.
    6. Leave truck keys, fuel cards and the mobile device at the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Terminal every night.
  21. Drivers participating in the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service should be aware of the additional policies regarding their mobile device:
    1. Drivers are to use the same mobile device everyday.
    2. The mobile device is NEVER to left in the alone in the truck. Always take the device with you.
    3. The mobile device should be charging whenever not in use in either the cradle at the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Terminal or the cradle within the truck.
    4. The mobile device must be returned to the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Terminal each night. If a driver returns to the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Terminal after the store has closed, the driver is to take the mobile device home for the evening and bring it back to work the next morning when the store opens.
    5. The mobile device is ONLY for business use.
  22. Any ticket incurred because of driver behavior is the responsibility of the driver, whether the violation is recorded by an officer or camera.
    1. This includes, but is not limited to the following violations: speeding, parking, stop sign/red light, school bus, failed DOT inspections, load securement, medical cards, or driver’s licenses.
    2. Drivers should never park in a fire lane, handicap spot, or emergency zone.
