Store Deliveries Policy

  1. All deliveries must be initialized through Customer Order Entry and automatically cross-referenced to a point of sale transaction. For more information, see 07-03A Customer Order Entry.
  2. You are not permitted to release merchandise on customer orders for accounts with credit warnings without prior approval from your District Credit Office.
  3. All SW store delivery and passenger vehicles must have three Post Accident Drug/Alcohol Test Consent Forms in the vehicle at all times. These are available in the Forms Repository (search “Consent to Chemical Substance Screening”).
  4. The following policy is designed to eliminate conflict and concern regarding pricing and shipping of merchandise when two or more stores, and/or Sales Representatives are involved. 
    1. Within District Boundaries:
      1. No store will ship goods into another store's territory unless the District Manager approves the transaction.
      2. Typically, the only approved exceptions will be large volume commercial, industrial maintenance or Product Finishes orders, or special blends that are shipped through the District's designated facility.
      3. Customer orders to be delivered outside a store's territory should be given to the nearest store capable of handling the order.
      4. If you are uncertain about a store's mission, or capability, refer the matter to your District Manager or Sales Manager.
    1. Outside District Boundaries:
      1. No store will ship goods into another District unless it has been agreed to by both District Managers. The Division Vice President of Sales will review questions or disagreements.
    1. Outside Division Boundaries:
      1. The Division President must approve shipments to accounts inside his Division from a store outside his Division.
  5. Stores are to use complete and correct addresses on within Customer Order Entry and follow these formatting rules:
    1. Address must be complete with all of the following fields: Street Name and Number, City, State, Zip Code.
    2. No abbreviations are permitted in the City Field.
    3. Only approved USPS abbreviations are permitted in the Street Name and State Fields. 
    4. If no street number is available for the job site, stores are to note the closest cross streets as the address.
    5. Stores should NOT include landmarks in their addresses. 
    6. Ampersands (&) are not to be used in addresses.
  6. Stores participating in the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service should be aware of the additional following policies:
    1. Districts own and are responsible for the management and on-time delivery of their delivery orders.
    2. Stores must ask their customers when their order is required and note this information on the customer order. Try to frame this information within the established times for order delivery.
    3. If the delivery requires something special such as a specific signer, drop-off point or time, or check pick-up, this MUST be included in Special Instructions or it will NOT be taken into consideration by dispatch for scheduling or be available to the drivers for review. 
    4. Usage of outside freight companies for delivery is discouraged when the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service can be used. For situations in which an outside delivery system is the best decision, approval should be obtained from the District Manager.
  7. Incoming freight and handling charges from vendors must be charged to customers
  8. Outgoing freight charges (deliveries to customers) should be charged to the customer whenever possible.
  9. It is important to know if you should charge your customer sales tax on the freight/delivery charge while processing a point-of-sale transaction.  For more information see Freight Taxability Overview.
  10. Deliveries not made through the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service are required to use the paper Confirmation of Delivery Ticket which eliminates the need to use Manual POST forms and Packing Slips for deliveries. Manual POST forms should only be used if the computer system is not working properly or as a busy-time form.
    1. Bring two copies of the Confirmation of Delivery Ticket. Customers are required to sign one copy, which must be returned to the store for retention.  The second copy is provided to the customer as a courtesy.
  11. The complete order can be canceled only if you are not able to deliver all of the merchandise and undelivered balances can be deleted from the customer order.
  12. Additional merchandise not on the original order must be added placed on a separate customer order for delivery.
  13. Sales transactions must be run within twenty-four hours of delivery, but are not to be processed until the order has been delivered and the customer signature for the delivery confirmed by either the return of the customer signed Confirmation of Delivery Ticket to the store (stores making deliveries) or viewing of the customer signature for the delivery through POS (stores using the Delivery Service for deliveries).
    1. If a customer requires their invoice at the time of delivery, it is permissible to process the sale and send the invoice copy along with the order. The customer is still required to sign either the mobile device or the Confirmation of Delivery Ticket. If, for any reason, the delivery of billed product does not take place the same day as the customer was invoiced, a return or charge credit transaction MUST be processed by the end of the business day.
  14. A cash on delivery (C.O.D.) shipment must be delivered by Sherwin-Williams' delivery truck or by a store employee.  COD payments are to be accepted as follows:
    1. The customer MUST have an account.
    2. Stores not participating in the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service are to receive funds at the time the goods are transferred to the customer.
    3. Stores participating in the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service should be aware that Delivery Service Drivers are NOT permitted to handle or carry within their vehicles any form of cash related to a sale or order.  However, drivers are permitted to accept checks.  If a COD order is required, the delivery should be placed using the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service and the payment should be handled as follows:
      1. For cash payments, a member of store management at the store requesting the delivery should make arrangements with the customer to receive the COD amount.  Payment MUST be received BEFORE the delivery is made.
      2. For check payments, the store MUST, in Special Instructions, note that the driver is to accept a check at the job site. The store needs to submit a Misc. Stop with instructions for dispatch to route the driver back to the store to drop-off the check. 
  15. All deliveries require that a Bill-of-Lading be created. Please review Bill-of-Lading Application Overview for additional information.
  16. Stores and or Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service Drivers making deliveries must be aware of and adhere to all policies regarding Hazardous Materials Shipping Overview as outlined in Hazardous Materials Shipping Policy.
  17. The customer or a signer for the customer must be present at the job site when a delivery is made.
    1. If material is going to be dropped off at a jobsite where no one is present to sign for the receipt of the material the Confirmation of Delivery must show who ordered the material and a signed Authorization and Release Form (Authorization and Release Form – AUTO) must be on file at the store (prior to delivery) stating that the customer will accept responsibility for the material without a signature.
  18. The following paperwork is required for retention in a folder entitled CONFIRMATION OF DELIVERY. At the end of each month, place the contents of the CONFIRMATION OF DELIVERY folder on the top of the monthly batching.
    1. Customer Signed Confirmation of Delivery Ticket: only applies to physical tickets for orders not delivered through the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service (orders handled by the delivery service will pass electronic proof of delivery to the POS that will be archived in the system for the appropriate retention period).
    2. Drivers Signed Bill-of-Lading/Hazardous Material Shipping papers
    3. Copy of the sales transaction
