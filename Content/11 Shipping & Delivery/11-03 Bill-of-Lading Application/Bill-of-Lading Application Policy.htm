Bill-of-Lading Application Policy

  1. BOLDOC is to be used to generate Bills-of-Lading whenever a Bill-of-Lading is required.
  2. The manual Bill-of-Lading Form and Hazardous Materials Shipping Papers will remain in the Forms Repository and should only be used in downtime scenarios. Print ten (10) copies each of the manual Bill-of-Lading Form and Hazardous Materials Shipping Papers. Store these forms in the Downtime Readiness folder for use only when SOURCE is unavailable.
  3. Manual Bills-of-Lading are ONLY permitted in exception circumstances for certain products and downtime processing: spray equipment, return of customer provided sample, transport of unsold goods to another SW location for tinting, floorcovering, wallcovering, creation of a BOL for an Approved Return IBAR for a carrier other than Con-way, Demonstration Orders, customer returns requiring pick-up of goods at the customer site, inclusion of class-numbered merchandise on order.
  4. To meet regulatory compliance, Bills-of-Lading must be created anytime product leaves the store by 3rd party carrier or CTS truck. Inclusion of all products is required so that the common carrier has accurate piece count and weight information for the shipment.
  5. Bill-of-Lading documents are always required when delivery is made via either a store delivery vehicle or employee-owned vehicle.
    1. This would include customer order deliveries, IST/ISR, IBARs (with the exception of returns handled by Con-way as they will create a BOL for the store), Store Expense transactions such as Demonstration Orders and Customer Returns requiring store personnel to pick product up at the customer job site.
  6. Bills-of-Lading are required for all shipment of product from a store to an end destination. Ex: customer delivery location, another store location, vendor.
  7. Bills-of-Lading are not required but must be made available to any customer requesting the document with their sale.
  8. Outside Buys picked up by the store from the vendor require Bills-of-Lading. While BOLDOC cannot be used for this, always request a Bill-of-Lading from the vendor to accompany the product while under your transport.
  9. A Bill-of-Lading from a Sherwin-Williams DSC or Plant will always accompany a Will Call Purchase Order. Please be sure to have this document available while transporting product.
  10. Bills-of-Lading are to be signed by the driver when picking up materials. Stores are to retain the signed copy and provide a second copy to the driver for transport.
  11. Manual Bills-of-Lading must be used when transporting spray equipment. 
  12. Classify spray equipment as follows:
    1. Airless sprayers should be classified as "Sprayers, Paint, Airless, Power- Operated NMFC Freight Code 92.5."
    2. If you are shipping spray equipment powered by a gasoline engine, use the shipping description "Engines, internal combustion, 9, UN3166."
