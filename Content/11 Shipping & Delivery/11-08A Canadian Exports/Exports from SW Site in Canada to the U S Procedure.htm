Exports from SW Site in Canada to the U.S. Procedure

This section applies to Sherwin-Williams sites that export goods from Canada

The following steps must be completed prior to each shipment. Failure to follow these steps may cause border delays, returned shipments, fines, penalties, or merchandise forfeiture.

Step

Action

Invoice Preparation

1

The shipping location must prepare and provide the invoice that meets all of the applicable requirements of the U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP).

If

Then

Invoice cannot be generated systematically from AOM or QAD

Pro Forma Invoice must be prepared, completing all 17 data elements to allow our custom brokers to accurately complete customs declaration.

Note: The Pro Forma Invoice is available in the Forms Repository and should only be used when an invoice cannot be generated by AOM or QAD. For more information, see the Guide to Using HEARs for POS.

NAFTA Certificate

1

If

Then

Products manufactured by Sherwin-Williams

The shipping sites should not need to take any action. The Trade Compliance Team in Corporate Regulatory maintains a list of all NAFTA certified products and provides this list daily to our U.S. Import Brokers.

Purchased items acquired in Canada and shipped to the U.S.

Obtain valid NAFTA certificate from the supplier to receive duty free treatment. If not obtained, then the duties will be charged to the shipping group. If a TAG store ships, then TAG division will pay the costs.

Note: A blank certificate can be found in the Forms Repository.

Declaration of Foreign Shipper

1

Items of U.S. Origin or items previously exported from the U.S. and being returned to the U.S. may be eligible for duty free treatment:

If

Then

U.S. manufactured goods
  • Clearly indicate U.S. as the Country of Origin on invoice.
  • Complete the Declaration of Foreign Shipper Form, available in the Forms Repository.

Goods previously exported from the U.S.
  • Obtain Canadian import declaration or proof of U.S. export via U.S. export declarations .
  • Complete the Declaration of Foreign Shipper Form, available in the Forms Repository.

Note: Contact tradecompliance@sherwin.com for details.

Toxic Substance Control Act (TSCA) Certification

1

If

Then

Canadian site shipping experimental products or new formulas to the U.S.

You must request a TSCA certificate from the U.S. regulatory team by emailing tradecompliance@sherwin.com prior to shipping.

Canadian site shipping products or raw materials that are purchased from suppliers in Canada
  • Prior to shipping, you must obtain written a TSCA certificate from the supplier and submit it to the Trade Compliance team via email tradecompliance@sherwin.com.
  • The Trade Compliance team will then provide a new TSCA certificate on Sherwin-Williams letterhead to accompany the shipment.

Shipping

1

Provide all required documents listed above to the designated carrier and/or driver.

2

Unless already identified on the invoice, inform the driver that our customs broker is Russell A. Farrow.
