Important Canadian Export Terms Overview

This section applies to Sherwin-Williams sites that export goods from Canada

To adhere to Sherwin-Williams internal policy as well as applicable laws and regulations, it is important to understand the following terms:

Term

Definition

Country of Origin/Manufacture

The country of origin of invoiced goods is the country in which the goods have been grown, produced, or manufactured. Each manufactured article on the invoice must have been significantly transformed in the country specified as the country of origin, to its present form ready for importing into the U.S. Certain operations—such as packaging, splitting, and sorting—may not be considered as sufficient operations to confer origin.

Declaration of Foreign Shipper

Form completed by the exporter/shipping location declaring the statement that the articles specified were exported from the United States, from a specific port and date, and that they are returned without having been advanced in value or improved in condition by any process of manufacture or other means.

NAFTA Certificate

The North American Free Trade Agreement Certificate of Origin is an agreement used to substantiate claim for the preferential duty treatment under the North American Free Trade Agreement that covers certain eligible goods manufactured in the U.S., Canada, and Mexico. A completed NAFTA certification is required to be in the hands of the U.S. Importer or the preferred broker.

Sherwin-Williams Site

Includes all Sherwin-Williams sites including but not limited to GSC plants, DSCs, Automotive branches, Product Finishes facilities, and TAG division.

TSCA

Under the Toxic Substances Control Act (TSCA), the EPA is given broad authority to issue regulations designed to gather health/safety and exposure information on, require testing of, and control exposure to individual chemical substances, chemical mixtures, and articles.  EPA’s authority under TSCA extends to all materials that are considered “chemical substances,” “mixtures,” or “articles” as defined under TSCA.
