Export Orders Policy

Violation of US export regulations can seriously jeopardize our business. Penalties for willful violations include fines and monetary penalties, imprisonment, seizures or forfeitures of goods/property, debarment from exporting defense articles and commercial products, denial of licenses, debarment from contracting with the US Government, non-U.S. SW sites can be added to the restricted parties list, and potentially damaging PR.

  1. Stores must be aware of and adhere to all policies and procedures for shipment of goods domestically as documented in Packaging & 3rd Party Shipping Overview.
  2. All orders shipped to a country other than that in which the shipping store is located must be marked as export in Customer Order Entry.
  3. If a violation is suspected, contact your Trade Compliance Department, Legal Department or Ethics Line 1800 –SWC–TRUE (1-800-792-8783) immediately for further guidance.
  4. All export shipments of defense articles require CS002 Form submission. 
    1. Shipment cannot occur until GS Customer Service review and approval process is complete.
  5. ISPM-15 compliant pallets must be used on all export shipments.
    1. Heat treated solid wood pallets are acceptable and help to reduce the spread of pests.
    2. Plastic pallets are also an option.
  6. Stores are not authorized to:
    1. Sign forwarder’s POA (send requests to Director of Trade Compliance).
    2. Sign any documents that authorize another person or company to file EEI on the behalf of the Sherwin-Williams Co.
    3. Sign any Shipping Letter of Instruction provided by a Freight Forwarder.
    4. Prepare and sign Free Trade Agreement Forms, such as NAFTA.  All requests for Free Trade Agreement Forms should be submitted to sw.reg.affairs.nafta@sherwin.com.
  7. IMO  or DG Declaration may only be completed by locations which are IMODG or IATA/ICAO trained.
  8. Stores are only authorized to complete SLI for non-licensable shipments using SW’s version of the SLI.  Stores may not complete any other version of SLI, such as forwarder’s SLI.
  9. Only approved Freight Forwarders may be used for non-routed shipments.
  10. Export documents must be received from Freight Forwarders before processing any payments to the Freight Forwarder.
  11. Customers may use their selected freight forwarder for routed shipments.
    1. Request export documents AES ITN Number from the customer or Freight Forwarder.
