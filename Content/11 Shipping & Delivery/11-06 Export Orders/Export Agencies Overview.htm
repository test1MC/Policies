Export Agencies Overview

Sherwin-Williams is required to comply with the regulations of several federal agencies in its handling of exports:

AGENCY COVERAGE CONSEQUENCES

OFAC
  • Administers and Enforces Trade Sanctions  
  • Maintains SDN and Blocked Person’s List

Penalties for Unauthorized Exports from USA to Embargo Countries and persons on the SDN

BIS

EAR – Export Administration Regulations

Fines and Penalties

  • Risk of selling to a denied party
  • Sales of nuclear grade coating to unauthorized parties
  • Some Aerospace coating are under review
  • Technology Transfer for the controlled articles

BIS

Anti-boycott regulations require US firms to refuse to participate in boycotts that the United States does not sanction
  • LC language must be reviewed
  • Reporting requirements
  • Penalties

DDTC

ITAR – International Traffic in Arms Regulations

Items specifically designed, developed, configured,adapted or modified for military application and:

  • Do not have predominant civil application
  • Do not have performance equivalent (defined by form, fit and function) to those of an article or service used for civil application

Or:

  • Are specifically designed for military application and has significant military or intelligence applicability such that DDTC determines control on USML is necessary
  • An export license or valid exemption is required for export of all defense articles, services or technology
  • File license authorization to, exports, reexport, deemed export (technical data), retransfer – within the same foreign country, and end use, end user, all parties involved
  • License tracking
  • DSCs  - Destination Control Statement
  • Fines Penalties

Department of Commerce

FTR – Foreign Trade Regulations (Census)

Collects Statistics through AES filings, which help other export agencies keep track of licensable exports

Enforces Fines Through US Customs

SEC

Administers FCPA

 

Customs & DHS

Enforces Export Controls at the ports

Enforces regulation of other agencies
