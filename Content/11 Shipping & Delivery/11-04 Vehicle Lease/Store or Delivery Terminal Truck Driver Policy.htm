Store or Delivery Terminal Truck Driver Policy

  1. Store/Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service vehicles can be driven by any employee of the store/Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service as long as the employee is assigned by the Store/Sherwin-Williams Delivery Terminal Manager, is at least 18 years of age, possesses a valid driver's license, and meets all state and federal requirements to drive the truck.
    1. Sales Reps are not authorized and/or permitted to operate a store/delivery vehicle unless they have been certified with the approved safety training or have the approved licensing (i.e. cdl) if needed to operate the larger vehicles.
  2. The qualifications of a driver (i.e. DOT qualified) to operate a truck with a gross vehicle weight of 26,001 pounds or more, or to transport placarded loads of hazardous materials are listed below. (A placarded load is defined as 1,000 pounds or more of hazardous materials).
    1. Is at least twenty-one years of age or older
    2. Possesses a valid commercial motor vehicle operator’s license
    3. Has completed a driver's road test and been issued a certificate
    4. Has successfully completed a written examination and been issued a certificate
    5. Has successfully completed an examination and been granted a Hazardous Materials license endorsement
    6. Has successfully completed a physical examination
    7. Speaks and reads English
    8. Does not any have controlled substance abuse, or motor vehicle record violations.
  3. To become a DOT qualified driver, contact the District Manager to obtain a DOT Driver Hire Packet (Order Express #3000518).
