Hazardous Materials Marking Requirements Policy

  1. Each package of flammable liquid that is being shipped as a hazardous material must be marked with the proper shipping name and the DOT identification number. No abbreviations are permitted.
    Example: "Paint UN 1263" or "Paint Related Material UN 1263".
  2. If flammable liquid containers are packaged in combination packaging (inside a UN 4G fiberboard carton), containers must be packaged with closures placed upward. The carton must be marked with the THIS END UP label or orientation arrows (Two total located away from other markings or labels and on opposing sides of the box).
  3. Flammable Liquid labels are required on containers of flammable liquids (for ground shipping purposes, these are products with flashpoints of less than 100 degrees F) as follows:

Container Size

Requirement

Gallon container in cartons

Cartons must have flammable liquid labels.

Five Gallon Pails of Flammable Liquid being shipped as single packaging

Pail must have a flammable liquid label.

Drums of Flammable Liquid

Drum must have a flammable liquid label.

Aerosol Cans
(Must be in original packaging [cartons])

Do not require labeling, but the original carton must be marked with the following wording: Consumer Commodity ORM-D and the ORM-D label.

Note: Flammable Liquid labels may be ordered through the Non Merchandise Items Catalog (Order Express #3000203).
