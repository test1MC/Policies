Common Carrier Policy

  1. Stores participating in the Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service are not to utilize common carriers for shipments without prior approval from District Management.
  2. A properly completed Carrier Agreement should be on file at your location prior to the carrier providing any delivery services for your store. Find the Carrier Agreement in the Forms Repository. Search the form name of keyword “carrier” in the Form Name Field. All available forms containing your search will appear below in the form description field. By completing and signing the Carrier Agreement, the carrier is stating their willingness to comply with the conditions and requirements set forth within the Carrier Agreement.
  3. Outgoing freight charges (deliveries to customers) should be charged to the customer whenever possible.
  4. When shipping serialized equipment:
    1. The store employee must record any serial numbers (Example: Spray Equipment) in the as a COMMENT [F9] in the order detail of customer order entry.
    2. TAG & Product Finishes Locations Only: A manual Bill-of-Lading Form must be used rather than BOLDOC. Find the Bill-of-Lading Form in the Forms Repository. Search the form name of keyword “lading” in the Form Name Field. All available forms containing your search will appear below in the form description field.
    3. Automotive Locations Only: A manual Bill-of-Lading Form must be used rather than BOLDOC. Find the Bill-of-Lading Form in the Forms Repository. Search the form name of keyword “lading” in the Form Name Field. All available forms containing your search will appear below in the form description field.
    4. Serial numbers should also be included on the Bill-of-Lading. 
  5. Merchandise shipped via common carrier requires the following paperwork:
    1. Bill-of-Lading
    2. Confirmation of Delivery
  6. Delivery driver signature is required on the Bill-of-Lading and any hazardous material shipping paperwork.  Driver signed copies must be retained within the store.
  7. Shipments that require protection from freezing and are sent via common carrier during the winter must include the statement "Protect from Freezing" on all copies of the Bill-of-Lading.
  8. Merchandise shipped via RPS (Federal Express Ground) must also be invoiced through prior to the merchandise leaving the store.
  9. Merchandise shipped via common carrier may be invoiced upon pick-up of the material by the carrier, but no later than twenty-four (24) hours after pick-up.
