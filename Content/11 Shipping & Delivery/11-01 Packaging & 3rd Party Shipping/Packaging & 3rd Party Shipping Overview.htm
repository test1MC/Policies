Packaging & 3rd Party Shipping Overview

For customer convenience, Sherwin-Williams often may deliver or ship merchandise to a specific location requested by the customer. Based on the volume of orders delivered or geographical consideration, some stores have their own delivery vehicle. Most stores handle their deliveries using their district-managed Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service, which economizes and improves the delivery process and level of customer service. For more information on Sherwin-Williams Delivery Service deliveries and store deliveries, please see Store Deliveries Overview.

Product Delivery for customers will occasionally require approved use of a third party such as a common carrier, air delivery service, or cargo service.  Regardless of the shipment method, Sherwin-Williams’ employees must adhere to all regulations and laws regarding the packaging and shipment of hazardous product. Employees should also review BOLDOC Overview.
