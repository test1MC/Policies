Hazardous Materials Shipping Overview

The description, "Paint," is the proper shipping name for paint, lacquer, enamel, stain, shellac, varnish, liquid aluminum, liquid bronze, liquid gold, liquid wood filler, and liquid lacquer base. The description, "Paint Related Material," is the proper shipping name for paint thinning, reducing, removing or drying compounds. Typical classifications of hazardous materials found in the store include:

Class

Description

Flammable liquids

Liquids with flashpoints under 141º F.

Corrosive materials

Liquids or solids that cause irreversible damage to human skin tissue, or has a severe corrosion rate on steel.

Hazardous wastes

Spent solvents or waste-flammable paints

Note: The flashpoints of products can be found on the products’ Material Safety Data Sheet (SDS), Section IV.

Flammable liquids are divided into three distinct packing groups. Sherwin-Williams never ships materials in Packing Group I, but the others are defined as follows:

Flammable liquid: Packing Groups

Packing Group

Description

II

Liquids with a flashpoint below 73 degrees Fahrenheit and with a boiling point above 100 degrees Fahrenheit;

III

Liquids with a flashpoint above 73 degrees Fahrenheit but below 141 degrees Fahrenheit.

Note: There is a special exception for domestic shipments by highway allowing liquids with a flashpoint between 100 and 141 degrees Fahrenheit to be transported as non-hazardous materials. Contact Stores Safety & Environmental at 216-566-1710 for further explanation of this exception.

To identify the materials as hazardous and to determine the correct BOL code:

  • Refer to the SDS/Data Pages Quick Links on the Source Intranet. The SDS and Data Page documents will provide you with weights, flash points, and the product’s appropriate shipping name. 
  • Access Hears using your mySherwin logon. The BOL link under Shipping Information will allow you to query for BOL Codes by product.  An X before the BOL code denotes the product is a hazardous material when shipped by ground.
