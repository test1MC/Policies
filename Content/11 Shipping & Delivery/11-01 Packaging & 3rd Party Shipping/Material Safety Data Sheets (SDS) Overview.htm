Material Safety Data Sheets (SDS) Overview

Material Safety Data Sheets (SDS) are printed documents that provide detailed information on the hazards of a hazardous chemical or material. The Occupational Health & Safety Administration (OSHA) specifies the requirements of SDSs in the workplace. SDS are available for products sold in Sherwin-Williams stores. Although the format may vary, all SDSs will have the following information:

Contents of SDS Sheets

Identification

The manufacturer name and address, emergency telephone number, the date the SDS was prepared, and a name corresponding to the one on the label.

Hazardous Ingredients

The harmful elements in a product. It tells you the Permissible Exposure Limit (PEL) or the Threshold Limit Value (TLV) and any other recommended limits. If the identity of a chemical is a trade secret, it will not be named, but the SDS will still tell you the hazards and safety measures. If the need arises, information, even on trade secret formulas, can be made available.

Physical Data

What the material looks like, smells like, how fast it evaporates, whether the vapor (fumes) rises or falls in the air, and the boiling and melting points This helps you make sure the product you have is the same as the one on the SDS. If you notice anything unusual about the product you are using, you should notify your supervisor.

Fire and Explosion Data

The temperature at which the material can ignite or explode. It also tells you how to handle these hazards.

Health Hazards

How the chemical can enter your body and the health hazards that could result from exposure. If the chemical is believed to cause cancer, that information is noted here. It also lists the symptoms of overexposure and what type of first aid to use.

Reactivity

The stability of the material and how it may react with other materials and situations.

Spill or Leak Procedures

Procedures for spills, leaks, and waste disposal. It also tells you of any special precautions for handling or storage.

Additional Sections

Other sections may be added to an SDS, which explain how to reduce harmful exposure, what personal protective equipment to use, and any special work or hygiene practices to follow.
