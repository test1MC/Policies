Hazardous Materials Shipping Policy

According to the U.S. Department of Transportation (DOT), hazardous materials are substances or materials capable of posing an unreasonable risk to health, safety, and property when transported in commerce.

  1. With few exceptions, the DOT regulations comply with international standards.  DOT, or a state’s implementing agency, regulates the transportation of hazardous materials.
  2. The DOT specifies the classification of materials, labeling, packaging, marking, placarding, documentation, vehicles, and other general requirements for transportation.
  3. Shipments of hazardous materials not in compliance with federal or state transportation requirements may be subject to fines as high as $25,000 per violation.
  4. In addition, the person who transported or who prepared the shipment for transport may face personal fines or imprisonment upon conviction of "willful" violations.
  5. As a shipper or offerer of hazardous materials for transportation, the Store Manager must ensure that the shipment complies with DOT regulations.
  6. A store must not offer or accept hazardous materials for transportation in commerce unless that material is properly classed, described, packaged, marked, labeled, in proper condition for shipment, and accompanied by shipping papers.
  7. Hazardous materials are to be transported only over public highway. Do NOT transport or ship hazardous materials by aircraft, rail or water vessels, unless authorized by Stores Safety & Environmental at 216-566-1710.
  8. The information in this section does not apply to shipments by air or cargo. Stores that do not have an employee trained and certified by Stores Safety & Environmental many not prepare for shipment or arrange for transport any hazardous materials or any other paint or paint-related products.
  9. Offering hazardous materials for transportation requires compliance with DOT standards:
    1. A Bill-of-Lading must accompany each shipment of hazardous materials regardless of quantity.
    1. In 3rd party shipments, a correctly completed Bill-of-Lading must accompany the shipment, along with the referenced Emergency Response Guidebook pages. When hazardous materials and non-hazardous materials are on the same shipment, the hazardous materials must be listed first, and identified by marking an X before the material name. If separate documents are used, the hazardous material shipping papers must appear on top of all other documents. Hazardous Materials tabs (Order Express #656-2326) should be attached to shipping papers that include hazardous materials for easy reference. 
    2. BOLDOC should be used to create a Bill-of-Lading, which will satisfy the requirement for Hazardous Materials Shipping papers.  Please see Bill-of-Lading Application Overview for details on using this application.  Three copies of the bill-of-lading are required. BOLDOC will default to print three. The copies should be distributed as follows:
    1. The first copy should be signed by the driver and must be retained in the store along with the Confirmation of Delivery Ticket and copy of the invoice.
    2. Carrier takes copies two and three. One copy must be retained by the carrier for the duration of his or her delivery run. The third copy is a courtesy copy provided for the customer.

    1. The Shipping Address must be noted.
    2. The COE# or Invoice Number must be captured on the bill-of-lading.
    3. All shipping papers shall include: the proper shipping name of the material, the hazard class, the material's identification number, the packing group, the total quantity by weight (including units), and an emergency response telephone number.
    4. The hazardous material must be described on the bill-of-lading.
    5. A Hazardous Material Shipping Form must be affixed during shipment. Find the Hazardous Material Shipping Form in the Forms Repository.
