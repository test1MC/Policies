Carpet Recovery Policy

  1. Policies within this section apply only to stores selling or shipping carpet within or into the state of California in accordance with California’s carpet stewardship law (AB 2398). They are effective as of July 1st, 2011. 
  2. Carpet may only be sold from carpet manufacturers that are in compliance with the new law.
    1. The California Department of Resources, Recycling and Recovery (CAL Recycle) will post a notice on its website (www.calrecycle.ca.gov) listing all of the manufacturers that are in compliance with the law. 
    2. This will also be monitored in Cleveland to ensure that S-W vendors are in compliance. 
  3. An assessment of $0.20 per square yard on all carpet sold and/or shipped into California must be collected from customers. 
    1. The assessment will be automatically generated on your invoices based on the SKU for affected carpet sales. 
    2. This assessment will appear on the invoice generated by the POS system as an after tax line item identified as CA Crpt Stewardship.
  4. The assessment must be included on all carpet sold and/or shipped into California including carpet, broadloom carpet, or modular carpet tiles used in commercial and residential buildings. 
    1. The assessment is not paid on padding, cushion, underlayment, or synthetic turf.
  5. If a store sells carpet to someone whose ship to address is outside of the State of California, then the carpet assessment is not to be collected. 
    1. However, a copy of an exemption form must be completed.  These may be found on www.carpetrecovery.org. 
    2. Once completed, send the exemption form to your District Manager.
  6. Failure to comply with the law may result in a penalty of up to $1,000 per day or up to $10,000 per day if the violation is found to be intentional.
