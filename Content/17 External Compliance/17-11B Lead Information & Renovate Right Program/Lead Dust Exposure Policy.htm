Lead Dust Exposure Policy

  1. Employees should identify the hazard lead may pose to any customer renovating, remodeling, or repainting a home, building, or other structure built prior to 1978 or any customer with questions concerning lead exposure.
  2. Under no circumstances should Sherwin-Williams' employees make any statements, or provide their opinion, as to the likelihood of lead on the structure.
  3. Sherwin-Williams will provide customers with the following information:
    1. Structures built prior to 1978 may have been painted with a paint containing lead.
    2. The customer should have the paint/coating on the structure tested for the presence of lead.
  4. For further information regarding the potential presence of lead, its health effects, and/or proper surface preparation and cleanup, the customer should be:
    1. Referred to the local health authority
    2. Instructed to call the National Lead Information Center at 1-800-424-LEAD.
    3. Instructed to logon to www.epa.gov/lead.
    4. Provide your customer with the EPA Renovate Right Booklet.
  5. The following brochures and booklets are outdated and should no longer be provided to contractors or customers.  Please immediately destroy any copies found:
    1. Protect Your Family Brochure.
    2. EPA Renovate Right Booklets with Sales Number 585-2512 or Sales Number 591-7596.

Automotive branches are NOT required to display the Renovate Right Counter Card nor provide the EPA Renovate Right Booklet. However, should a customer request this booklet, branches may provide.
