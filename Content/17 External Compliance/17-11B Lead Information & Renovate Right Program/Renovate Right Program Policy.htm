Renovate Right Program Policy

This section applies only to floorcovering facilities and Paint Stores selling floorcovering.

The following information outlines the obligations of Sherwin-Williams Employees under the new EPA Renovate Right Program when coordinating installation of floorcovering.

  1. All employees do not have to be EPA certified. Only certain individuals within the company must be certified:  District Managers, Sales Managers, Store Managers, Operations Managers, Sales Reps.  
  2. Employees who are not certified are not to go into the work area after it has been sealed off, even to deliver supplies.
  3. A completed Lead / Asbestos Survey Form must be kept on file at the store for all jobs. This includes buildings constructed after 1978 whether it be for bid or every day “turn” business. 
    1. The survey may be completed over the phone. Once completed, sign it and print the person’s name spoken with. Indicate the survey was completed over the phone.
    2. If the property contact refuses to sign, be sure to print their name on the form and indicate that they refused signature.
  4. Employees must identify whether the structure was built in or before 1978 and if it has undergone any significant renovations when bidding jobs to determine correct handling:

    5. If

    Then

    Built before / in 1978

    Provide the property owner with a copy of the EPA Renovate Right Booklet when they sign their contract.

    Built before / in 1978

    Building has undergone renovation and building has a certificate / report with the proper dust and lead clearance certification

    Contact District Manager for instructions.

    Built after 1978

    No special handling required.
  5. Use only a subcontractor/installer who is EPA certified for installations on buildings built in or before 1978:
    1. Certification must be confirmed with the subcontractor/installer.
    2. Certification must be documented in SOURCE when submitting a new installer request.  Contact the Credit Office or responsible Division Coordinator for updates to the IPA Account in CPR. 
  6. Sherwin-Williams is responsible for making sure the IPA Subcontract Agreement is signed and on file at the store.
  7. If visiting the jobsite, confirm that an EPA certified employee of the subcontractor/installer is overseeing the work and clean-up.
  8. Upon completion of the job, the Job Completion Certification must be signed by the installer.
  9. Report any known violations to the District Manager.
