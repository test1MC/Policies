RRP – Renovation Overview

The RRP Rule covers activities that modify an existing structure and that result in the disturbance of painted surfaces. All types of repair, remodeling, maintenance, modernization, and weatherization projects are covered, including projects performed as part of another Federal, State, or local program, if the projects meet the definition of renovation.

Renovation

Examples include (but are not limited to)

Removing, modifying or repairing painted surfaces or painted components
  • Painted doors
  • Surface restoration
  • Window repair
  • Surface preparation activity such as sanding and scraping that may generate paint dust

Removing Building components
  • Walls
  • Ceilings
  • Plumbing
  • Windows

Weatherization projects such as cutting holes in painted surfaces to
  • Install blown-in insulation
  • Gain access to attics
  • Planning thresholds to install weather stripping

Interim controls that disturb paint surfaces

 

Note: The RRP Program rule only applies to persons who perform renovations for compensation.
