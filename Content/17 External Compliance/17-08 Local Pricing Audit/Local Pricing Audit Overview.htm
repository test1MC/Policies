Local Pricing Audit Overview

Local audit departments routinely pay surprise visits to our stores to ensure that the Shelf Label pricing matches the list price of products scanned at the register. These audits are critical and protect consumer interests. Stores with violations may be subject to fines and additional audits. All states and local governments have their own regulations concerning Shelf Labels. The Store Manager should check with local authorities to become familiar with local requirements. Stores should always be ready for a pricing audit. Maintain Shelf Label accuracy by utilizing these practices:

  • Cycle Count: When performing your weekly Cycle Counts, verify that the List Price returned in the system matches the Shelf Label pricing for the product.
  • Pricing Updates: When notified of pricing updates, remove the old Shelf Labels and either display the new Shelf Labels sent to you from Headquarters or use Shelf Labels On Demand to create new Shelf Labels that reflect the price increase.
