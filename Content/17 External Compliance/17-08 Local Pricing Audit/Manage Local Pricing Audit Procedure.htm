Manage Local Pricing Audit Procedure

If the store is audited for pricing, email methods@sherwin.com immediately upon conclusion of the audit. Sherwin-Williams must be made aware of any potential problems due to local restrictions concerning Shelf Label Pricing.

Step Action

1

Email methods@sherwin.com to report that the store is being audited.

2

Always review the auditor's credentials BEFORE granting access to any information.

3

If the auditor wants to check the accuracy of the shelf labels:

  1. Ask the auditor to bring the selected products to the counter.
  2. Select INQUIRY [F4].
  3. Select Products and Prices.
  4. Scan the barcode of the product being audited.
  5. Be sure to reference the List Price (LP) in the Pricing Window.
  6. Identify any barcode that does not scan properly.

4

If the auditor wants to check the accuracy of special pricing or sales items:

  1. Process a training transaction so that no actual sales post.
  2. Select Point-of-Sale from the Main Menu.
  3. Select Enter Training Mode from the Sale/Payment Menu.  You will need the Manager Password to proceed.
  4. Select Sale from the Training Menu.
  5. Press CANCEL [F5] to bypass the Order Browse Window.
  6. Process the sale using Customer Number 1 (DIY).
  7. Scan the barcode of the product being audited.
  8. Be sure to reference the Price Returned for the product.
  9. If needing to print a journal of the transaction that was just processed, proceed to Step 5. If not, continue to Step 6.

5
  1. Press INQUIRY [F4].
  2. Select Inquiry Menu.
  3. Select POS - Point of Sale from the Inquiry Menu.
  4. Press [Enter] until reaching the Voids Field.
  5. Enter 4 - Training Transactions Only.
  6. Press ACCEPT [F1] to generate the report.
  7. When done verifying sale and special pricing for the auditor, do not forget to select Exit Training Mode from the Training Menu.

6

If the auditor requests a copy of our price discrepancy policy, please print and provide the Sherwin-Williams Price Discrepancy Policy. Do NOT print the Policy & Procedure Manual or provide any non-Sherwin-Williams employee access to it.

Some states and counties require an in-store sign posting of the Sherwin-Williams Price Discrepancy Policy, including:

  • New York State
  • Wisconsin State
  • San Diego County in California requires that this sign be posted at each scanner location.

Note: San Diego County also requires stores that fail a scanner inspection to post a Department of Agriculture, Weights, and Measures Sign (available at county website) provided by the auditor after failing the inspection. Stores should be aware of the County of San Diego Scanner Awareness Program.

7

If an auditor would like to see how scanning error transactions are handled, inform the auditor that these transactions are run through the POS and requests for additional information and reporting can be made to methods@sherwin.com. The Store Manager has NO authority to authorize local auditors to examine any records unless approval for such examination is obtained.

8

At the conclusion of the audit:

  1. The store may receive a report or be informed that a report will be mailed. Once received, fax the findings of the local auditor to Methods & Procedures at 216-515-5807. Please be sure to include a cover letter routing the fax to Methods & Procedures.
  2. Review all shelf label errors and use Shelf Labels On Demand to print and to display new shelf labels for each error found during the audit.
  3. Develop a plan to ensure that the error rate decreases.  With employees, discuss the importance of using cycle counts as an opportunity to review shelf label pricing accuracy.  Ensure that your shelf labels are updated at the beginning and end of each sale period.
