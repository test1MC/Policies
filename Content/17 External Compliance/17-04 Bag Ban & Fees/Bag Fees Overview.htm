Bag Fees Overview

In an effort to support local green initiatives, several jurisdictions have passed laws either banning the use of plastic and/or paper bags to package customer purchases or have implemented fees to be paid by the customer for the use of allowed bags.

Paper bags are available for jurisdictions where plastic has been banned but paper is still allowed.  Be careful to review the Bag Ban & Fees Chart as many of these jurisdictions allow the use of paper bags but require a fee to be charged to the customer.  Paper bags may be ordered from the DSC on the STAR Order and are only permitted in jurisdictions where plastic is banned and paper is allowed with some exceptions.  To reorder paper bags:

  • Small Bag  922-1623
  • Large Bag   922-1664

Some jurisdictions require the store to post signs or have available a public statement of Sherwin-Williams’ policy regarding usage of carryout bags.  Should this be required in the jurisdiction (consult the chart below) or if questioned by a customer or external auditor, they may provide share the following statements.  However, never print out or share the Policy & Procedure Manual with any person external to Sherwin-Williams.

If there is a new bag law in the jurisdiction, contact methods@sherwin.com for guidance before discontinuing the use of bags or charging customers for their use.  Many bans and fees apply only to specific business types.

Be aware that the local jurisdiction may audit the store’s compliance with the local ordinance. Should a local auditor visit the store, notify methods@sherwin.com immediately.
