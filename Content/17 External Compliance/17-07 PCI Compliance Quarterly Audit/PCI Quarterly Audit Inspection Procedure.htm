PCI Quarterly Audit Inspection Procedure

PCI compliance is a continuous process and stores' periodic review of their equipment is an important part of Sherwin-Williams' assessment of IT assets and business processes. The PCI Quarterly Audit must be conducted quarterly. 100% compliance from all locations utilizing POS devices is mandatory to prevent the incurrence of fines issued by the credit card brands.

Before beginning the visual inspection, become familiarized with the two charts below: Basic Terms and Peripheral Devices. These may be useful when completing the online audit survey, contacting the SCO HelpDesk for assistance, or reporting an irregularity.

Visual Inspection for stores without PAX Payment Terminals

2

Count the number of peripheral devices used for each station:

  • Potential peripherals include: keyboard, mouse, magstripe reader, scan gun, Lexmark printer, modem, and/or slip printer.
  • Typical manager stations include: keyboard and mouse.
  • Typical client (wrap-counter) stations include: keyboard, mouse, slip printer, magstripe reader, and scan gun. The primary station may also include a telephone modem.
  • Typical tint stations include: keyboard, tint dispenser, scan gun, label printer, and spectrophotometer.
  • Typical floorcovering (backroom/cut machine) stations include: label printer, keyboard, and mouse.

3

Count the actual number of cables attached to your computer. 

  • There should be one cable to each peripheral device named above, as well as a network cable and monitor cable. There may also be a modem cable. If all peripherals are attached to the POS, then there should be a maximum of 10 (ten) connected cables.
  • Be aware that cable connections may be located on the back, front, or sides of the machines. Please refer to Diagram A and Diagram B in in POS Hardware Visuals for PCI Audit Overview for references.

4

If the actual count does not match the expected count, then inventory the accessories, trace the cables to the source (Hub or computer), and record the connections.

Visual Inspection of PAX Payment Terminals

5

Inspect and count the number of cables attached to each PAX Payment Terminal. 

Each terminal should have only two cables:  a power cable and network cable.  Inspect to verify that these are the only cables and that they have not been tampered with or replaced.

6

Inspect the facing of each PAX Payment Terminal for evidence of tampering by reviewing:

  • Extension Attached to the Swiper
  • Missing or Damaged Screws
  • Missing or New Labels
  • PAX Logo no longer Visible
  • Keypad covered or overlain.  An overlay can be a small sticker that forms to the device and covers the keyboard area. Overlays may hide damage due to tampering or wires that can allow for keyboard logging.
  • Device covered or overlain. Wiggle the panel of the terminal to see if it is loose.
  • Pull on the Card Swiper to check for a Skimmer.
  • Check for cameras.  Sometimes hackers looking to get PINs put cameras above the keypad. Feel for anything protruding that could be a camera and review the area for potential recording devices. 
7

Contact the SCO HelpDesk at 216-566-2740 if requiring assistance or finding any irregularities when completing the visual inspection.
