Register for EPA Customer Training Procedure

Step

Action

1

The minimum number of trainees per session is 20 and the maximum is 25 except in the few states that have approved a higher number that is indicated below.

2

Training for the initial certification course starts at 8:00 AM and concludes at 5:00 PM. The RRP refresher course is a four hour course.

3

When trainees arrive, they will have their picture taken and complete an application.

At the conclusion of training, a simple test will be given of multiple-choice questions.

The picture, application, and test will be forwarded to training firm’s office for completion.

Approximately 4 weeks later, those who pass will be mailed a certificate with their photo attached which is good for 5 years.

4

In order for a “firm” to conduct Lead-Based paint activities and/or renovations, it must be certified by submitting an application to the EPA. The EPA charges $300.00 for firm certification and it is also good for 5 years.  This fee is separate from the class training fee and is submitted directly to the EPA.

5

During the hands-on part of the class, the EPA requires a 1 to 10 ratio of students to instructor, which means the store will need to supply S-W personnel to assist with the RRP session. A full class of 25 will require (2) S-W employees to assist. The instructor will advise them what to do. The following states require additional certified instructors in place of SW representatives:
