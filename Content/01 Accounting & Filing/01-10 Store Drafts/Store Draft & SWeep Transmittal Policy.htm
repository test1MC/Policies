Store Draft & SWeep Transmittal Policy

  1. Whenever possible, process an invoice transmittal on SWEEP instead of paying by store draft. The Store Draft process DOES NOT take the place of the SWEEP Expense Invoice Transmittal process.  For more information see Service Provider Payment Overview.
  2. Automotive Branches Only:  Automotive branches are not permitted to write branch drafts without obtaining approval from GFG General Accounting as there may be other preferred methods of payment such as SWEEP, eAccounting, etc.
    1. Contact GFG General Accounting at 216-566-3309, 216-566-2321 or216-566-3393.
    2. If the use of a branch draft is approved, obtain the approval code.
    3. Enter the approval code into the POS transaction (disbursement by draft, etc.) as a comment.
  3. You are not permitted or able to write a store draft for merchandise purchases, even if approved by your District Manager. Store Drafts cannot be coded to any merchandise prime-sub such as 0406-xxx, 0401-xxx, 0400-xxx. [Revised 05/04]
  4. Accounts Payable will not recode store drafts to merchandise prime-subs.
  5. All merchandise vendors must have established Credit Accounts and be paid through Accounts Payable. Find an alternative source if the vendor is not willing to establish credit terms with the Company.
  6. Store Drafts are intended for:
    1. Joint checks  [Does not apply to Automotive Branches]
    2. Installer payments  [Does not apply to Automotive Branches]
    3. Credit filings
    4. Cash refunds
    5. Replenishment of operating fund
    6. Complaint settlements under $100.00
  7. Any other use must be approved by your District Management prior to issuing the draft.  Written District Management approval must be on file at your location for all store draft exceptions.
  8. Anyone providing a service to Sherwin-Williams such as: window cleaning, floor cleaning and waxing must be paid by submitting the invoice on an Expense Transmittal [SWEEP] to Stores Group Account Payable.  Please include the taxpayer identification information.
    1. Corporate Payroll will not authorize store drafts for payroll related issues. All supplemental payments will be handled during the next regularly scheduled processing. Requests that cannot wait until the next processing will be issued on an operating check and mailed to the employee’s home address. The Corporate Payroll staff will approve store drafts on an exception basis to ensure compliance with wage and hour laws or accommodate casual labor needs. Contact Division Human Resources for more information.
    2. All store drafts must be processed through the store computer system at the time the draft is written. Failure to process store drafts in this manner may result in the bank rejecting these drafts and is a violation of Policy.For additional information seeDowntime Processing Procedure. Suntrust Bank charges $20.00 per draft per day for store drafts that are not processed through a point-of-sale transaction. This charge will be passed along to your store for any DRAFT that is cashed with no matching point-of sale transaction number.
    3. Issued drafts are not valid after 60 days.
      The Store Manager, Assistant Manager, and District Manager are the only persons authorized to sign store drafts.
    4. The employee signing the draft cannot issue a draft payable to himself/herself.
    5. Unused store drafts are to be kept in the safe.
    6. Store drafts should only be signed when they are issued, do not sign blank drafts.
    7. Do not loan or borrow Store Drafts from another Sherwin-Williams facility.
    8. Drafts that are lost or stolen must be reported to Division Loss Prevention and General Accounting (216 515-4660) immediately.  A Loss, Offense and Incident Report (LOI) must be prepared. For more information see, Complete an LOI Report Procedure

Note: Any draft questions and requests regarding voids, stops, and ordering can be directed to StoreDrafts@sherwin.com
