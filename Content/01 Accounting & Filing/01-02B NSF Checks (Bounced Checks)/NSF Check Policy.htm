  3. Employees must validate each check through the store computer system. If the system is down, use the “For Deposit Only” check stamp to mark the check for deposit only. Remember to include customer information on the Manual POST Form.
  4. Locations should not place NSF checks for collection.  This is automatically handled through the NSF Check Management System, which will place accounts for collection with a third party. 
  5. Locations should not send NSF letters to customers and should only take the collection steps indicated in the email instructions; however, locations are still responsible for contacting the customer by phone to solicit payment.
  6. Customers are not permitted to make partial payments on NSF amounts owed unless given prior approval by the credit office.
  7. Customers may make payment for NSF amounts at any location within their DAD.
  8. NSF checks for Payment on Account transactions must be routed directly to the Credit Office for handling.
  9. Locations are charged for NSF checks and for collection fees when placed with an agency.
  10. If the local bank requires the NSF check be picked up, then ask if the local bank can requalify the check with PNC Bank’s routing number on the back and send it through again. This routing number is included in the validation process. If the bank cannot do this, pick up the NSF check.
  11. Do not buy back NSF checks from the bank regardless of whether the NSF check was for a payment on account, cash sale, or an accommodation sale. The bank should debit the location’s account for the amount of the NSF check.
  12. After payment has been made on the NSF check, it should be returned to the customer or destroyed. All other records should be retained for seven years.
