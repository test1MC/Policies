Submit Expense Transmittals Policy

  1. All expense invoices approved by the Store Manager (equal to or less than the approved authorization level) will be automatically routed to Accounts Payable for processing. Any invoice greater than the approved authorization level will be routed to the District Manager for approval. Once the District Manager has approved the invoice, the next person in the approval chain will receive an email to continue the approval process. An approval chain of upper management is automatically built based on the dollar amount and the Division-Area-District location of the store. Once the final person approves the process, the transmittal will be submitted to Accounts Payable for processing.
  1. Non-merchandise purchases above the following authorization limits must have written District Management approval before non-merchandise products are ordered or services are rendered. Expense Invoice Transmittals above the authorization level will be automatically routed to the next level of authority. Limits should be discussed with the District Manager.   1. Invoices must be promptly entered into SWeep immediately upon receipt of the invoice to ensure timely payment.
  2. Expense Invoices will still be received by the store. The vendor should not send these to Headquarters nor should the store forward the physical invoice to Headquarters unless a copy of the invoice is requested by Accounts Payable.
  3. Utility and telephone service invoices should not be paid through SWeep.
  4. All invoices payable via SWeep must be retained in filing and retention per policy (U.S. Stores Only).
  1. Once the invoice image is attached in SWeep, stores will not be required to retain a hard copy. The store must first verify the invoice is available through SWeep prior to disposing of the invoice (locations faxing invoice documentation only).

W-9 Information:

A. A W-9 is required for any vendor that provides services to Sherwin-Williams.  If a vendor provides only material or supplies and NO service, a W-9 is not required; however, if unsure as to the nature of the transaction, please request the W-9 Form.

B. W-9 form is not required for:

    1. Foreign Vendors (ex. Canada)

    2. Customer refunds, rebates, or returns

    3. PQR (Customer refund)

C. As noted below in the Tax Department bulletin, any vendor that provides services* who does not comply with this request will be subject to 28% withholding tax for every payment made to them by Sherwin-Williams. This money will then be submitted to the IRS and is not refundable by Sherwin-Williams.  We will work with our vendors* before we do backup withholding.
