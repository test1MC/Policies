Expense Purchase Order Procedure

  1. Expense items that require a Purchase Order should be entered into the Merchandise Management System.
    or
  2. Process a SWeep Expense Invoice Transmittal to pay the expense vendor if the vendor does not require a purchase order.
  3. Use the following generic expense sales numbers:

  4. If multiple expense items (with the same prime sub) are being ordered on the same Purchase Order, remember to include the descriptor information. The descriptor will specifically identify the expense item and allow you to enter the same sales multiple times on a Purchase Order.
  5. If you cannot find a sales number for expense merchandise used in your PSR room, assign a regular sales number (don't use the above expense sales number) using category 50-66-010. After receiving the products against the purchase order, process a store expense transaction to move the merchandise to PSR Expense prime-sub 7500-202.
  6. Sales transactions using the above sales numbers will impact Paint sales margins and cost of goods sold. If you choose to sell an expense item ordered with one of the above sales numbers, you should use class number 7732 with a 99 margin. Press the COMMENT key; include the sales number and product description.
  7. Expense items that require a Purchase Order should be entered into the Merchandise Management System.

Or -

When ordering Sherwood Products, press LOOKUP and select [5. Manufacturer's Name] enter [Sherwood]. If you can't locate the product on the store's database, press LOOKUP again to search Headquarters' database for all products associated with Sherwood Products.

These Purchase Orders still need to be received like all other Purchase Orders. However, the billing process for expense items has not changed. Invoices still need to be matched to a Purchase Order, approved by the Manager and reviewed on the monthly SPR.
