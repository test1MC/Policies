Common Prime Subs Overview

The following table is a listing of the most common prime subs used and a detailed description:

Prime Sub Description

Theft (7510-001)
  • Any occurrence of theft at the store should be coded to this G/L account. Stores should only use this prime sub if a notable instance (empty wrappers) of theft has occurred. 
  • Typically, if there are missing items found during cycle counts, this should go to Shrinkage at Inventory and not be expensed.
  • An LOI should be completed for any expenses to this prime-sub.

Unaccepted Bank Card Drafts (7510-007)
  • This G/L account is used for any credit card transactions that are subsequently refused for payment by a customer when they receive their credit card bill. 
  • The disputed charges are then deducted back against Sherwin-Williams.  The most common cause for this is credit card fraud. 
  • An LOI should be completed for any expenses to this prime-sub.

Unauthorized Charges to SW Account (7510-008)
  • Any charge transactions on SW accounts that are subsequently refused for payment by a customer when they receive their SW bill.
  • The disputed charges are then deducted back against Sherwin-Williams. 
  • The most common cause for this is unauthorized persons fraudulently charging on an SW account. 
  • An LOI should be completed for any expenses to this prime-sub.

Demonstration Expense (7599-111)
  • All merchandise that is used for the purpose of customer demonstrations is expensed to this G/L account to relieve the product from inventory.
  • Non-GOM products should not be expensed to this prime-sub.

Product Samples (7300-001)
  • This prime-sub is used for any products given to the customer as a sample.
  • Non-GOM products that we are attempting to distribute to opportunity accounts should not be coded as Demonstrations, and will be expensed to this account.
  • Common charges to this account can include Finishing Touch Kits (quart of paint, brush, spackling, spackling knife, etc...) that are given to homebuilders at no cost.

Customer Complaint (7504-001)
  • This G/L account is for the settlement of customer complaints. 
  • Labor charges would typically occur if the store has to hire a contractor to repaint a house or re-install wallcovering. 
  • These charges will clear as ARJV’s, store drafts and on expense invoice transmittals.

Guarantee Policy Adjustment (7502-101)
  • This prime-sub is used to book the cost associated with fulfilling our guarantee to customers that they will be satisfied.
  • Contact your auditor prior to expensing to this prime-sub.

Services & Supplies - Other (7402-099)

Any charges that do not fit into the pre-defined general ledger accounts are coded to this prime sub.

Repair & Maintenance – Other

(7400-099)
  • Any cost associated with repairing or maintaining a fixed asset in its original working condition that does not directly add to the value of the asset.
  • Any repairs that are not for the store’s signs, heating, or air conditioning should be coded to this prime sub.

Janitorial (7402-005)
  • Expenses related to the cleaning and up-keep of the store are coded to this prime sub. 
  • Typical expenses are carpet/floor mat cleaning, window cleaning, floor cleaning/waxing and garbage removal.

Destroyed Merchandise (7599-104)
  • Any merchandise that is destroyed in the normal course of business is expensed to this prime sub to relieve the product from inventory (dented cans, leaking cans, any damaged product).

Trade Promotions (7303-104)

The costs associated with Pro Shows, trade shows, or conventions in which the store personnel host or participate in.

Paint Merchandise Aids (7303-111)
  • Displays and promotional aids for specific point of purchase displays.
  • These items can be color cards, displays, posters, signs, brochures, T-shirts, bags, and paint paddles.

NSF Checks (7510-003)
  • Customer Checks returned to the store because of insufficient funds.
  • Review to ensure any checks here are in NSF check system on Source.

POS Cash Over/Short (7510-110)
  • Cash variances from Close transactions.
  • Review for misprocessed transactions or instances of losses or gain.
  • File an LOI for any variance $75 or greater.

Over/Under Deposit Variance (7510-111)
  • Any variance in the daily deposits.
  • Review of deposits errors and review if entries to match with Cash Over/Shorts.
