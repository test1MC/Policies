A G/L Code Description
Accounts Receivable 0300 Accounts Receivable are amounts due from customers, credit card collectors, etc. of the Company, generated through sales-type transactions. Does not include special obligations instruments.
Customer Detail - Control 0300-001 These are recorded at the time of each sales-type transaction and reported via the POS device by customer account number. All receivables charges and collections are maintained in detail by Accounting Centers. Customer balances are reconciled monthly to the Cleveland Control balance of all net receivables activity on a consolidated basis.
Contra Receivables (JV Clearance) 0300-003

Records the entries made via ARJV which have not been posted against the customer receivables file. This account should be used as a temporary holding account for all transactions other than those entries generated through the POS device, including all adjustments, write-offs, etc.
Misc. Receivables (Est. Sales) 0300-004 This sub-account is used as a month-end temporary holding account offsetting estimated sales amounts for which no detail is available as to the ultimate disposition (i.e., cash, receivables, VISA, etc).
Bank Card In-Transit 0300-102

This sub-account is maintained to record credit card sales reported via the POS device daily for which the drafts have not been processed by the credit card collector and credit has not been received by the Company. Balances should represent the amount of unprocessed drafts for the last few days of any month due to time lag in transmission from the store to the respective bank at Headquarters. The accounts are charged by the POS device and credits are entered by Headquarters upon receipt of deposit advice from the banks.
