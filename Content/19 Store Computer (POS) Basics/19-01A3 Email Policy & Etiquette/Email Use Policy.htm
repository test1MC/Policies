Email Use Policy

  1. Email communications must be consistent with Company standards of ethical and proper conduct, behavior, and manners.
  2. All employees must review the Technology Use Policy in Corporate Policies Review Policy.
  3. Only one person at a time at the store location will be able to access email. There is one email address per store. The email address is (sw [your store number] @sherwin.com). All email addresses default to lower-case lettering.
  4. Incoming email will automatically appear in the Inbox. Check the email two times a day, and only check more if expecting a customer communication.
  5. Do not open a message if store employees do not know the sender, or if the subject is gibberish, objectionable, obviously non-business, etc. Delete the message.
  6. Avoid posting any Sherwin-Williams email address anywhere on the internet, and, when practical, avoid providing an address to vendors with whom the store does not have a current business relationship. Do not leave business cards in public places.
  7. Avoid providing a Sherwin-Williams’ email address on web forms and submissions on the internet. Watch for any type of check-box that enrolls the email address into a newsletter or that shares the email address with a third-party.
  8. Some sites require enrollment or request an email address for free products, free services, or contests. These sites commonly share email addresses as a method of generating revenue.
  9. Unless familiar with the senders, never reply to an email requesting a response in order to unsubscribe from an email list. Some individuals and companies use this reply as a method of validating addresses. Then, they use this information to subscribe the email address to other mailing lists. If the sender is unfamiliar, delete the email.
  10. Never forward an email that claims it is capable of tracking the forward, even if it alleges to generate revenue for a certain cause. These emails are commonly referred to as chain mail and merely help individuals gather email addresses to spam.

Note: Review Email Etiquette Overview for information on Email Policy, Procedure, Restrictions, and Etiquette.
