Operating Fund & Target Fund Policy

  1. The Store Manager must make sure that the proper operating fund is on hand at the store at all times and must take measures to replenish the operating fund should it become necessary.
  2. Any changes to the authorized store Operating Fund or point-of-sale terminal Target Fund must be approved in writing by the District Manager and retained in a Target Fund folder in Drawer Three (3) for Filing and Retention for up to six (6) years. The District Manager will also forward a copy of the approval letter to Accounting.
  3. A portion of the fund may be kept in the cash box inside the safe during the business day.
  4. The Store Manager is responsible for the Operating Fund and Target Funds.
  5. The Manager must make sure funds are allocated properly and that the operating and target amounts are always on hand.
  6. When issuing a check for replenishment of the Operating Fund, the check cannot be made out to the person signing the check.
  7. Stores must only replenish their Operating Funds using the procedures available at their location:
    1. Donor Terminal Procedure - All Stores
    2. Draft Replenishment - Automotive, Chemical Coatings, SW Paints
    3. DM Approval Replenishment - Duron, MAB Paints
  8. Duron and MAB Paints stores must have written District Manager approval on file before requesting replenishment funds from another Duron, MAB Paints, or SW Paint location.
  9. In the event of a robbery or burglary, an employee must call the District Manager and Division Loss Prevention immediately to report a loss of funds. A Loss Offense and Incident Reporting (LOI) process must be completed. For more information, see Complete an LOI Report Procedure.
