POS Operating Fund Policy

  1. The Operating Fund for each point of sale must be verified each morning to ensure the correct amount is in the drawer at the beginning of the day.
  2. Each Point of Sale terminal, containing an operating fund, must be opened every day whether you choose to use it or not.
  3. The operating fund must be included in the drawer count for each point of sale every day.
  4. If you will not be using the second or third Point of Sale for an extended period, you are permitted to decrease the operating fund to one-cent (.01).
    1. Move the funds to the main point of sale if the additional operating fund is required for store operations. Increase the operating fund on the main point of sale to account for the additional operating fund.
    2. When you need to use the additional Point of Sale, move the operating fund back by decreasing the operating fund on the main point of sale and increasing the operating fund on the second or third point of sale.
    3. Deposit the operating fund in the bank if you don't want to move the funds to the main point of sale.
  5. Write a draft to increase the operating fund when you need to start using the second or third point of sale. For more information on increasing, decreasing or replenishing the Operating Fund see Operating Fund & Target Fund Overview
  6. All terminals that run Point-of-Sale transactions must be counted.
