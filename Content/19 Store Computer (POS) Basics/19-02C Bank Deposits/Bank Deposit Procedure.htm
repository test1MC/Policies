Bank Deposit Procedure

The Drawer Count and Close Transaction must first be completed. For more information, see Close Policy. When closing the main POS terminal, a single bank deposit slip must be filled out for all of the terminals.

Step

Action

1

Fill out the bank deposit slip by including the date, currency, coin, total check dollar amount, and the total deposit amount.

Note: The bank deposit check log lists all checks processed for the POS terminal; however, it is not required to list each check separately on the bank deposit slip, just enter the total dollar amount. 

2

Compare the deposit amount and date on the bank deposit slip (printed by the bank) to the actual deposit amount and date of the POS Close Transaction.

If

Then

The bank’s deposit amount and date match the actual deposit amount and date of the POS Close Transaction

A member of store management other than the person who made the deposit must initial the bank validated deposit slip

The bank’s deposit amount and date does NOT match the actual deposit amount and date of the POS Close Transaction

Ask the bank branch representative to review the Night Deposit Log to verify the information. If the representative confirms a bank error, request a written explanation and staple it to the deposit slip

Note: Weekend deposits might have the following Monday’s date.

3

 

Attach the validated deposit slip to the corresponding store copy of the Close Transaction slip and Bank Deposit Check Log.

Note: For more information about retention requirements, refer to Filing & Retention Batch Box Overview.

Only if you are unable to deposit the exact amount of cash and coin shown on the close transaction, follow the procedure below:

Step

Action

1

Make the cash and coin deposit as close to the POS deposit amount as possible. 

2

Place a note in the cash drawer so the opening employee knows about the deposit adjustment.

Note: The cash drawer will be short during the Close Transaction as a result of the deposit adjustment from the night before.

Note: The bank charges at least $5.00 for each adjustment made to the account.

4

Enter the following information on the front of the bank deposit bag:

  • Bank name
  • Store number
  • Date
  • Prepared by
  • Cash, Check, and Total amount

5

Tear off the deposit bag’s numbered receipt and staple it to the Close Transaction slip.

6

Paperclip the folded Check Deposit Log, checks, and two copies of the bank deposit slip together and place in the bottom compartment of the deposit bag.

7

Place the currency and coin in the top compartment of the deposit bag. To seal, carefully remove the protective liner from the adhesive area then fold and press the adhesive flap firmly over both sides of the bag.

Note: Do not put the deposit in an envelope before placing it in the bank deposit bag because banks will charge up to $5 per deposit to open the envelope. 

8

An authorized employee must take the deposit to the bank immediately after closing.
