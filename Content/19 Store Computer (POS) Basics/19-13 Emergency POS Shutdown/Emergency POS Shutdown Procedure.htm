Emergency POS Shutdown Procedure

The procedure below should be used for shutting down POS machines in the event of evacuation, emergency, severe weather, or natural disaster.

Step

Action

This procedure should only be followed as long as it is safe and practical. In such situations, evacuate when or before civil authorities advise to evacuate the store, preventing the chances of getting trapped in the store.

1

Power off and unplug all printers, fax machines, mix machines, shakers, etc.

2

Unplug and secure the forklift, pallet jacks, and any loose equipment in the warehouse.

Emergency POS Shutdown for Severe Weather

1

Windows should be lined with poly film on the inside. The film should be held in place with duct tape, X configured from corner to corner then cross (+) configured top to bottom.

2

Important records should be boxed, covered with poly film, duct taped and placed on warehouse shelving.

For information on important records, see Filing & Retention Overview.

3

Some stores are equipped with storm shutters. Stores that are leased properties should contact the landlord in advance to have the shutters installed. Company-owned stores should install the shutters in advance (if available). 

For information on storm shutters, please contact the Division Real Estate Manager.

4

Wrap the POS, all computers, printers, fax machines, copiers, and other similar business machines in poly film and secure with tape.

Note: If moving the PC’s, move to an elevated, secured area away from windows and doors. 

5

All cash and checks, including the operating fund should be counted and deposited in the bank. If this is not possible, the Manager must advise their District / Area Manager that they will maintain custody of deposit funds until the deposit can be made. Provide the District / Area Manager with the exact amount of cash, checks, and the operating fund held by the manager.

6

Elevate metal containers approximately 12 inches off the sales and warehouse floors. Do the same with sandpaper, masking paper, tape, etc. Secure spray equipment with poly film and duct tape on the warehouse shelving.

7

Any damage to the Branch or Branch vehicles should be reported immediately to Corporate Risk Management (risk.management@sherwin.com).
