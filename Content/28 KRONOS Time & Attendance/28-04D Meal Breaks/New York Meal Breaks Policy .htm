New York Meal Breaks Policy

  1. Employees who work shifts of more than six hours which extend over the noon day meal period [11AM – 2PM] are entitled to a 30-minute meal break between 11AM and 2PM.
  2. Employees who work shifts of more than six hours between 1PM and 6AM must be allowed a 45-minute meal break midway through the shift.
  3. Employees who start working  before 11AM and continue later than 7PM are allowed an additional meal break of 20 minutes between 5PM and 7PM.
  4. Exceptions:
If Then

You want to have a shorter time for the meal periods.
  • A permit usually must be obtained and posted.
  • A lunch period less than 30 minutes usually does not require a permit if there is no indication of hardship to employees.

A meal period of less than 20 minutes will be permitted only in special or unusual cases.

After investigation and issuance of a special permit.

Only one employee is on a shift or is the only one of a specific occupation and eats while working, no violation will be found if the employee voluntarily consents to the arrangement.

The Department of Employment Policies and Labor Relations must be consulted prior to the utilization of any of these exceptions.
