California Meal Breaks Policy

This section applies to only non-exempt employees locations in California.

  1. Whether and how many meal breaks an employee is entitled to depends on the shift duration. Meal breaks must be started as listed below. Under certain circumstances, an employee may elect to waive his/her meal break.
  2. The decision to waive a meal break must be made voluntarily by an employee. A manager may not direct, coerce, or encourage an employee to waive a meal break. 
  3. In the event an employee fails to waive a meal break (where permitted) and fails to take the meal break on time and completely, he/she will be awarded a California Meal Break Hour equal to one hour of regular pay per day.
