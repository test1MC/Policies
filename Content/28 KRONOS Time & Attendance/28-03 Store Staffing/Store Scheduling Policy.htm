Store Scheduling Policy

  1. Store Managers are required to create and post bi-weekly staffing schedules through the Kronos application. 
  2. Staffing schedules are to be based on the target hours established by the District Manager. Store managers should ensure that target hours are not exceeded in creation of the schedule.
  3. Schedules should be made to ensure appropriate staffing and management levels throughout the week. Managers should schedule manager and assistant manager hours first, full-time employee hours next, and part-time employee hours last.  Weekly work expectations by position are:
    4. Position Hours

    Store Manager

    Minimum 48 Hours

    Assistant Manager

    Maximum 44 Hours (unless approved for unscheduled overtime)

    Full-Time Employee

    Maximum 40 Hours (unless approved for overtime)

    Part-Time Employee

    Maximum 28 Hours
  4. Staffing schedules must reflect any known employee vacation time, scheduled leaves, or planned time working outside of the store, such as sales calls, deliveries, meetings, and training sessions.
  5. Shift labels added to an employee’s schedule should be work-related reminders for the employee’s day or week-;these must be appropriate and will be reviewed.
  6. If changes must be made to the Kronos-generated schedule, adjust the hours for the remainder of the week accordingly within target hours.
  7. Once finalized in Kronos, staffing schedules must be posted in an area of the store where employees routinely view posters and other printed material, and provided to each employee no later than two weeks prior to the beginning of pay the next pay period.
  8. A management employee is required to review daily punch exceptions and alerts via the Kronos dashboard each workday. 
    1. Managers should appropriately reduce remaining scheduled hours to ensure employees avoid overtime and target hours are not exceeded when an employee works over their scheduled hours in a shift.
    2. If an already posted schedule requires changes (adjustments due to overworked shifts, employees swapping shifts, employees taking unscheduled time off), the schedule in Kronos must be edited and re-posted both electronically and physically.
