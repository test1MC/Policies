Jury Duty Policy

Upon presenting proper evidence, employees will be excused for jury duty examination and jury duty service.

  1. While serving on jury duty, employees are expected to work any time that may be available during their otherwise scheduled hours of work for the week.
  2. The employee will retain the jury duty fee in addition to any company pay as follows:
    1. A regular full-time and part-time employee will be paid for any time required for jury duty examination and service.
    2. Full-Time Employees
      1. For each regularly scheduled workday the employee does not work due to serving on jury duty, a full-time employee will be paid eight hours.
      2. If a full-time employee must be absent one full week to serve on jury duty, the employee will be paid one-half (1/2) of his/her biweekly pay. The employee must work one-half (1/2) of his/her biweekly scheduled hours during the other week of the biweekly pay period or the employee's check may be affected.
      3. If a full-time employee must be absent both weeks of a biweekly pay period to serve on jury duty, the employee will be paid his/her regular biweekly pay.
    3. Part-Time Employees
      1. For each regularly scheduled workday the employee does not work due to serving on jury duty, a part-time employee will be paid four hours.
    4. On any day an employee both serves on jury duty and works, the employee will be paid for the total hours served on jury duty up to the maximum noted above for their full-time/part-time status plus the hours worked. 
    5. To the extent the total hours of jury duty service and work exceed the employee's regular bi-weekly schedule, the employee will be paid additional overtime.
