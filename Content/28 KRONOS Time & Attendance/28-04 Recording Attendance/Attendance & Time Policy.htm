Attendance & Time Policy

Sherwin-Williams is committed to ensuring that that all employees are accurately and timely paid for all time worked. Accurate recording of attendance and time worked is critical function as employee pay is based upon these records. Employees are paid every two weeks, with the pay period starting on Sunday and ending on Saturday, fourteen (14) days later. Employees receive their paychecks on the last Friday of each pay period.

  1. All employees are responsible for recording hours worked and unpaid meal breaks. Recording should occur at or as close in time to the event as possible.  For example, an employee should punch in when reporting to work and punch out when taking an unpaid meal break. 
  2. Managers are responsible for approving and recording their employees’ unworked paid hours such as vacation, personal holiday, jury duty, and disability. 
  3. In the event an employee’s timecard is inaccurate or incomplete, the employee’s manager must edit the timecard. Both the employee and manager must make a comment explaining the edit such as for a forgotten punch, such an edit must be made by a manager. 
  4. All timecard edits for worked hours require comment and explanation.
    1. Employees should provide explanation via attestation comments. 
    2. Management should provide explanation this via comments on the timecard edit.
  5. Any employee, including a manager, who knowingly falsifies attendance or time records, is subject to immediate dismissal.
  6. For special state procedures, please contact Corporate Payroll.
