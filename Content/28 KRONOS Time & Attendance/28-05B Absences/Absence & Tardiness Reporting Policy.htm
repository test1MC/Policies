Absence & Tardiness Reporting Policy

Failing to properly record an absence or falsifying an attendance and time record are serious policy violations and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination. It is Sherwin-Williams' intention to comply with all federal and state laws governing an employee's time away from work, including FMLA and ADA. If it is determined that Sherwin-Williams has inadvertently or unknowingly violated some government regulation with respect to an employee's absence, the situation will be corrected as soon as possible after Sherwin-Williams has become aware of the violation.

  1. All absences from work for all employees whether full days or partial days must be recorded. 
  2. Employees must personally report both expected and unexpected absences to a member of management as soon as possible to allow management to properly staff the store. 
    1. If a member of store management cannot be reached, the District Manager should be contacted.
  3. Unless circumstances do not allow the employee to report their own absence, it is not acceptable for someone other than the employee to report an employee’s absence.
  4. Unless alternate arrangements have been agreed to and documented, an absent employee is expected to contact his/her supervisor every day of the absence.
  5. Failure to report an absence may result in an unexcused absence.
  6. Three unexcused absences will result in termination.
  7. Incarceration is considered an unexcused absence and will result in termination after three workdays.
  8. Store Management is responsible for entering unworked hours for absences and coding the time appropriately.
