Weather Absence Policy

During severe weather or a natural disaster, our primary concern is the welfare of our employees, their families, our customers, and others we can reasonably assist. In that regard, please review and implement the following policies when severe weather is approaching. For information on preparing your store for severe weather or an evacuation, see Severe Weather / Evacuation Preparation.

  1. All employees must follow the direction of  all civil authorities (police, fire, civil defense) before, during, and after a hurricane, severe weather, or other incidents that present a physical threat to people’s welfare.
  2. Communicate any evacuation orders that affect the store or employees to your District Manager as soon as possible.
  3. The District Manager or Area Human Resource Manager will decide when a store will be closed due to severe weather conditions or other natural disasters.
    1. Store Managers are expected to discuss such an incident with the District Manager or the Area Human Resource Manager before completing the attendance and time.
  4. The Store Manager is responsible for notifying store employees when the store is closed.
  5. If the facility is not declared closed, any employee who does not report to work as scheduled will be excused not to be paid (ENP), unless the employee chooses to take a vacation day or personal holiday.
  6. Employees will paid for weather absences as follows:
    1. Regular full-time employees, who would otherwise have worked that day, will be paid for eight hours.
    2. Regular part-time employees, who were scheduled to work that day, will be paid for four hours.
    3. If it is necessary for a facility to be closed for more than two consecutive workdays, the first two will be paid as described. The decision to pay beyond the first two days will be made by the District Manager with the approval of the Vice President of Sales and the Division VP of Human Resources.
