Funeral Leave Policy

Upon the death of a close relative, employees will be given excused time-off in accordance with the following policies.

It is appropriate for the facility to send, at Company expense, a moderately priced floral arrangement or make a charitable contribution of equal value as an expression of the Company's sympathy; the card should be signed from The Sherwin-Williams Company. The store should make such arrangements through the District Office.

  1. A close relative is defined as the following immediate family members: spouse, domestic partner, parents, stepparents, children, stepchildren, children of a domestic partner, siblings, and grandparents, in-laws [mother, father, sister, brother] mother-in-law, mother of a domestic partner, father-in-law, father of a domestic partner, sister-in-law, sister of a domestic partner, brother-in-law, brother of a domestic partner, daughter-in-law, daughter-in-law of a domestic partner, son-in-law and son-in-law of a domestic partner. Time off to attend the funeral service of other relatives or non-relatives will be treated as personal business.
  2. Employees may be required to furnish evidence of the relative's death.
  3. Paid funeral leave is based on the following:
    1. Regular Full-Time: 3 Days
    2. Regular Part-Time:  Paid for work time lost while attending the funeral not to exceed the number of hours the employee was scheduled to work that day.
    3. If circumstances warrant (i.e. sudden and unexpected death of a spouse or child, the funeral is being held in a distant city, etc.), the employee may be excused and paid for additional time not to exceed two days, or a total of five consecutive working days.
    4. These two additional days must be approved by the District Manager or Area Human Resource Manager.
