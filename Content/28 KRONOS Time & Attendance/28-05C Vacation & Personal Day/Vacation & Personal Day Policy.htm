Vacation & Personal Day Policy

  1. Vacation time does not rollover. Vacation time is to be scheduled and taken so that it is exhausted before the next qualifying year. 
  2. Employees must request time off for vacation, personal holidays, jury duty as soon as it is practicable to make these requests to ensure optimal staffing.
  3. No store employee will be allowed to take vacation on any day on which inventory is being taken.
  4. No vacation time may be taken during the first 60 days of employment.
  5. Vacation and paid time off may only be taken in increments of either four(4) or eight (8) hours.
  6. Full-time employees may take paid vacation:
    1. Half day is four (4) hours.
    2. Full day is eight (8) hours.
    3. Full week is five (5) days / forty (40) hours.
  7. If a full-time employee is scheduled to work 88 or 96 hours biweekly, the employee will receive his/her full biweekly pay provided he/she works half of the biweekly schedule during the other week in the same biweekly pay period.
  8. Part-time employees may take paid vacation:
    1. Maximum eight (8) hours per day.
    2. Maximum forty (40) hours per week.
  9. Personal days are subject to the following policies:
    1. All employees (excluding Canada) are granted two personal holidays per year.
    2. In some locations, personal holidays may be designated to match local holidays. In these instances, your District Manager will inform you of this decision.
    3. A personal holiday may not be taken during a new or rehired employee’s first sixty (60) days of employemnt.
    4. Upon termination, an employee will be paid for any earned unused personal holidays. 
      1. If an employee voluntarily or involuntarily separates from the company within the first sixty (60) days from the date of hire, the employee will not be paid for any personal holidays.
  10. An employee who is absent due to a lengthy disability may request and receive unused vacation pay during the absence as follows:
    1. Part-time employee: receive the vacation pay at the commencement of the absence.
    2. Regular full-time employee eligible for short term disability pay: receive the vacation pay at the point that full pay runs out and before 60% pay begins, or when the 60% pay runs out.
    3. Regular full-time employee who is not eligible for short term disability pay: receive vacation pay at the commencement of the absence.
    4. For employees returning from Long Term Disability Leave, the “work” requirement is to “work” at least 50% of the weeks since returning to work to qualify for vacation in the Plan Year after the year that they return from Long Term Disability Leave.
  11. A paid holiday falling during a vacation is not considered a vacation day. Absence from work, however, for circumstances such as illness, death in the family, etc. occurring during a vacation period is considered vacation.
  12. No requests for payment of unused vacation for the previous year will be honored after January 31st.
  13. Unless it is required by law, if an employee is unable to take all vacation during the Vacation Period because of unexpected and unusual business necessity, the District Manager may recommend to the Division VP of Human Resources the employee be paid for the vacation in lieu of having taken it. If the Division VP of Human Resources approves such a recommendation, the approval is forwarded to the Group Director of Employee Relations for payment during the month of December. This should not be used repeatedly as a method of giving pay in lieu of taking vacation.
  14. Canada Only: time worked requirements for holiday and vacation pay are dictated by individual provinces.
  15. For employees hired or re-hired during the year preceding the Plan Year, the work requirement is to work at least 50% of the weeks since hire or rehire to qualify for vacation in the Plan Year after the year of hire or rehire.
  16. At the time of separation, an employee who has not taken vacation days as of the previous Qualifying Date will be paid for the unused vacation days.
  17. If an employee voluntarily or involuntarily separates from employment within the first 60 days from the date of hire, the employee will not be paid for any vacation days.
  18. In addition to unused vacation, an employee who has worked more than 60 days will receive the pro rata portion of the next year's vacation if separated for one of the following reasons: retirement, death, store closings (if the employee had at least one year of service) and where required by law.
