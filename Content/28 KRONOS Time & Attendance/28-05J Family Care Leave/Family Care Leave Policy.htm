Family Care Leave Policy

In the event of the serious illness or injury of a spouse, child, or parent, an employee may need time off work to provide care for the seriously ill or injured person. Management will comply with the federal Family Medical Leave Act (FMLA), and any state laws governing such leaves of absence.

  1. With proper documentation, management will consider approving a request for unpaid leave of absence for up to twelve (12) weeks, or the amount of time required by state law whichever is greater, for a regular full-time or part-time employee, who meets the eligibility requirements of FMLA or any state law, and who requests a leave due to the serious illness or injury of a spouse, child, or parent.
  2. A request for a Family Care Leave of less than thirty (30) calendar days can be granted with the approval of the District Manager and the Division VP of Human Resources.
  3. A request for a Family Care Leave of thirty (30) or more calendar days requires the approval of the District Manager, Division VP of Human Resources, and the Corporate Employee Benefits Department.
  4. Requests for a Family Care Leave must be communicated to the Division HR Administrator, who will provide the required documentation to be completed and returned. Failure to contact the Division HR Administrator, and/or complete and return the required documentation, will jeopardize the employee’s employment status with Sherwin-Williams.
  5. An employee, who returns to work from an approved Family Care Leave of Absence, will return to the same job he/she held prior to the leave unless the position has been eliminated during the employee's absence.
  6. If the position was eliminated, the employee will have the same rights upon notification of return to work that he/she would have had at the time the job was eliminated.
  7. An employee who is on an approved Family Care Leave of Absence will continue to participate in the Group Life Insurance Plan at no cost to the employee and the Health Care and Dental Insurance Plans as long as the employee pays the appropriate monthly premium.
