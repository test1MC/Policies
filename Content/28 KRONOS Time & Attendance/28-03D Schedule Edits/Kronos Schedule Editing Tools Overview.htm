Kronos Schedule Editing Tools Overview

Kronos offers you tools in managing staffing of your store:

Tool

Description

Navigational Clues

Color coding on the suggested schedule identifies certain employee roles and shift roles. See 28-02A Kronos Navigation for detail on this color coding and navigating the schedule.

Open Shifts

Open shifts are blocks of time in which Kronos has not scheduled an employee but has identified that additional resources may be needed.    You may choose to fill an open shift or not. Once your schedule is posted, employees in your store can also see open shifts which are available and request to work these; a manager would need to approve the request before it is added to their schedule.

Borrowing Employees

Borrowing employees from another store is easy. Through the Staffing Assistant, managers are able to identify what employees from another store may be available; the list is sorted by the best match possible based on store role and availability. 

You still need to contact the manager of the employee’s home store to verify the availability and ability of the employee to be borrowed.  Factors to consider are whether or not an employee could enter overtime.

Borrowed employees will require set in the POS including an Employee Number if they will be using the POS while borrowed.

Job Transfers

Employees can be assigned shifts to work specific jobs inside and outside the store such as delivery, meetings, PSR, training, and working out of the store (sales calls, etc).

Meetings, training, and work outside of the store will count towards target hours.
