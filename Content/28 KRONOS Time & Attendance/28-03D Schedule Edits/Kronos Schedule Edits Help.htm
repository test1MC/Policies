Kronos Schedule Edits Help

Kronos will generate a schedule based on factors surrounding store hours, employee information, and labor demand. For more information on how the schedule is generated, see Kronos Auto Scheduler Overview . Kronos will only generate to the hours forecasted (these will be less than your target hours).  Do not delete the Kronos generated schedule and start over. Edit the schedule to achieve the desired results. Editing suggestions:

  • Ensure that all employees have their minimum required hours.
  • Validate that all keyholder shifts have been filled.
  • Add any shifts that were not previously requested through time-off requests required to manage special events such meetings, training, and working outside of the store (sales calls, etc).
  • Fill any holes in the schedule where you or Kronos thinks extra staff is required; Kronos will even identify open shifts for the store based on where additional coverage may be required.
  • Ensure target hours are met.
  • If there are unscheduled exceptions to an employee’s general availability and a conflict exists, remove the conflict by editing the shift or re-assigning the shift.
  • Add borrowed employees to the schedule.
  • Once all edits have been made, confirm all store hours are appropriately covered and review the Rule Violations tab (bottom left of the screen) to address any over/under scheduling.

Before editing the schedule, a few tips and tricks you will want to know:

  • Type P after Start/End times to indicate PM. Otherwise, the system defaults to AM.
  • Anytime you make a change, save the schedule and refresh your view so that you do not lose your work and you are reviewing the most current view of your edited schedule.
  • In the automated schedule, Kronos will suggest break times for employees. For all states (other than California), these breaks are suggestions of when an employee should take a meal break in relation to anticipated demand. The employee does not need to take their break at this time and what appears on the schedule will not affect payroll.
  • In California, breaks are scheduled according to the meal break laws and employees should take their breaks as scheduled in Kronos to ensure we are in compliance.

Once the schedule is finalized, post the schedule electronically and print a copy to post in the store. Fore more information, see Schedule Posting Overview.
