Kronos Availability Overview

Before Kronos can create a schedule, Kronos needs information about when employees work: availability and patterns.  These functions should be set once and maintained infrequently.

Availability is the knowledge of when your employees can and cannot work. Availability allows you to indicate to Kronos when an employee could be scheduled to work.  When the scheduler runs in Kronos, employees will be scheduled to meet the demand forecasted by Kronos but only at times matching their availability. Availability is a generality and only means that an employee could be scheduled during their available time, not that they will be. If an employee has a non-repeating exception to their availability, do not change the base availability; handle this with a time-off request for Unavailable. For more information on time-off requests, see Time-Off Request Overview. Kronos will default availability to “all the time” during store hours for employees; a manager will need to define the unavailable times in the system for an employee.

Note: Availability is not when an employee would prefer to work. For example, “Bob” may have classes at the local college on Tuesday and Thursday, work another job on Friday and Saturday nights, and want to watch the game with his friends on Sunday afternoon.  “Bob” is unavailable to work while in class or at his second job and that should be reflected in availability.  However, his Sunday leisure is a preference which all employees would like and is not something which should be reflected in availability.

Availability should be set for the following employees:

Employees Reason
Management Employees
  • Store Managers
  • Assistant Managers
  • Operations Managers

These employees should work when customer demand will be highest.
Store Associates

These employees should work when customer demand will be highest.
