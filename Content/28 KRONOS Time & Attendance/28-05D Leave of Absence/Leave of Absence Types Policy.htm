Leave of Absence Types Policy

  1. The following types of leave are available for employees:

    2. Leave Type

    Definition

    Disability

    An employee's own personal disability due to illness,  injury, or pregnancy

    Parental

    Birth or adoption of a child

    Family Care

    Caring for a spouse, child, or parent who is suffering from a serious illness or injury

    Education

    Undergraduate or graduate enrollment on a full-time basis in an approved program or area of study

    Personal

    Reasons other than those covered in the other leave types

    Admin

    A leave granted on an administrative basis, without request

    Military

    Service in the United States Armed Forces or Canadian Forces.   

  2. Unless the leave is covered by a benefit plan, company policy, or the company otherwise provides pay during all or part of an administrative leave, the leave of absence, if granted, will be without pay.
  3. Sole discretion to grant or deny leaves of absence and to establish the duration is retained by the company except as otherwise specified in a contractual arrangement or required by government regulation (i.e. the Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993 which mandates twelve (12) weeks of leave and certain states which mandate six (6) to thirteen (13) weeks to care for a child, parent, spouse or spouse's parent.)
  4. Sole discretion to require exhaustion of paid vacation, personal holidays, or disability leave as part of, or in lieu of, the requested amount of leave is retained by the company, except as otherwise specified in a contractual arrangement or required by government regulation.
  5. The decision to grant or deny a request will take into consideration among other things the nature of the request and the effect granting such leave will have on the business.
  6. The granting of a leave of absence shall in no way be construed as an employment contract.
