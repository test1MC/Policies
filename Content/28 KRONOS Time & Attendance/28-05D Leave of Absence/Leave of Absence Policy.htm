Leave of Absence Policy

Long term absences are absences of five (5) days or more. Although lengthy absences from work are discouraged, it is recognized that situations can arise which warrant an employee being granted a leave of absence from work.

  1. Requests for Information - All absences of five days or more require proper documentation as determined by the Division Human Resource Department. An employee who is anticipating the need for a leave of absence, or who experiences a sudden or unpredicted need for a leave of absence, is expected to cooperate with management’s requests for information necessary to make a determination with respect to granting the request.
  2. Failure to respond to or comply with a request from the Human Resource Department for proper documentation will result in adverse action including discontinuation of disability benefits and possible termination of employment.
  3. Medical Evidence - Management respects an employee's reasonable expectation of privacy regarding a physical or mental health diagnosis, however, certain information is required to determine an employee’s need for a leave of absence or possible reasonable accommodation to be able to return to work. 
    1. Medical evidence will be kept confidential and shared only with those management employees who have a need to know.  An employee’s medical file will be kept separate from the employee’s human resource file. With the employee's permission, the Human Resource Department may talk with an employee’s health care provider to assure that both the employee's and the company’s best interests are met to the best of management's ability.
