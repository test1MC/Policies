Visibly Frozen Procedure

1

Receiving Employee

Inspect shipments immediately upon receiving. Try to determine the severity of the freeze damage. If the shipment is severely frozen (i.e. frozen solid), you are authorized to refuse the delivery.

Note: Do not refuse the entire shipment. You must properly receive any item that is not affected by freezing, such as Associated Products, brushes, rollers, etc.

2

Receiving Employee

If

Then

You receive freeze-damaged products

Sign the proof of delivery with, “Shipment Frozen.”

You refuse freeze-damaged products

Sign the proof of delivery with, “Frozen and Refused.”

Complete receiving as follows:

Step

Action

1

Answer YES when asked, "IS ANYTHING VISIBLY DAMAGED?"

2

Enter each sales number for freeze-damaged products.

3

Press Comment [F9] on each frozen product’s line item and note, "Frozen Material." This will make damaged goods easier to identify when processing the IBAR.

4

These actions will result in the creation of a system generated IBAR, and the Store Manager must be notified to complete the IBAR process.

For more information on receiving, see Receive STAR Order Overview.

3

Store Manager

Store Managers must verify that the IBAR accurately lists all damaged or missing products.

Note: Products may be deleted from the IBAR before it is released, but additions of products and changes to quantity are not permitted. If these types of changes are required, cancel this IBAR and follow the Concealed Frozen Procedure below.

4

Store Manager

Store Managers must then release the IBAR. Credit will be granted once reviewed by the Global Supply Chain. If the Global Supply Chain rejects the damage claim, then a signed and dated copy of the BOL is provided to Stores Merchandise Billing within a month following the void on the store’s billing reports.

You are able to create the Carrier Claim IBAR before you have the disposal fees, but make sure that you do not release the IBAR until you have sent in the disposal fees to ensure these are filed together. Dispose of the product appropriately, pay the invoice in SWeep, and then send the invoice to Internalbilling@Sherwin.com for reimbursement.

See MMS IBAR Overview for more information.

If the frozen material is not discovered until after the driver leaves, then follow the Concealed Frozen Procedure:

Who

Concealed Frozen Procedure

1

Receiving Employee

Segregate the frozen material and allow it to completely thaw at room temperature (usually forty-eight [48] hours).

2

Receiving Employee

If “Frozen” was not noted on the delivery receipt, then you must immediately report to the carrier.

3

Receiving Employee

Upon thawing, if the product is not recoverable, notify the carrier to send a claims agent to inspect the material. If the carrier waives an inspection, ask for the full name of the person granting the waiver and record the date that the waiver was granted. Confirm the inspection request and the carrier's waiver in writing. Keep a copy of the letter for your records.

Note: Stores should contact the originating CTS Regional Fleet for CTS Shipments.

4

Store Manager

Forward the estimate for disposal costs to Internalbilling@Sherwin.com after the IBAR is released -> Dispose of the product appropriately, pay the invoice in SWeep, and then send the invoice to Internalbilling@Sherwin.com for reimbursement.

Note: If the disposal charges come in after the claim for the product has been filed, the carrier will more than likely deny reimbursement on the disposal charges. Carriers will not pay for charges without an invoice from the disposal company. For more information, see Store-Generated IBAR Overview.

5

Store Manager

To create and release a store generated IBAR:

Step

Action

1

From POS desktop, select Store -> Merchandise Management -> IBAR -> Add New IBAR -> Concealed Damage.

2

Add damaged products to the IBAR.

3

Use LOOKUP [F3] to select products from the original purchase order. Quantity and invoice must be noted for each product.

4

A Return Reason Code must be selected for each product added. Select Concealed Damage. Press COMMENT [F9] on each frozen product’s line item and note, "Frozen Material."

5

Release the IBAR.

6

Store Manager

Keep the following for your records:

  • Copy of the carrier's delivery receipt with frozen notation.
  • Copy of the inspection report or waiver of inspection.
