Frozen Material Overview

Sherwin-Williams takes precautions to keep products from freezing, including the installation of heaters in CTS trailers that operate in colder climates. Despite these preventative measures, frozen paint can still reach the store.

To identify frozen paint:

  • SHAKE the first few pails of freezable product by hand to see if paint has solidified.
  • FEEL the side of the pails to gauge temperature.
  • LOOK to see if any of the pails are bulging.

If any products do not feel or look right, open the paint and make a visual inspection. Pay particular attention to any product with a Green Sure designation. Typically, these products do not have any freeze-thaw stability and will be unusable once frozen.

  • Call (216-566-2297 [option #5]) Billing within a day of noticing the frozen paint. It is mandatory that all of these situations are recorded. You will need to provide the following information in your correspondence:
    • PO Number
    • Sales Numbers of Frozen Material
    • Quantity of Frozen Material
    • MMS Receiving Status of the Frozen Product

Do not file a freight claim directly with the carrier. Credit will be passed to the store via the IBAR process. All claims will be filed with the carrier by Global Supply Chain. This will ensure we have visibility of all shipments affected by freezing.

There are certain times when carriers issue embargoes on shipping. If Global Supply Chain has chosen to ship during an embargo it will not affect credits due to the store for frozen shipments. The credits will still be passed to the store via the IBAR process.
