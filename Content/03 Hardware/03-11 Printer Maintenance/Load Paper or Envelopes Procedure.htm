Load Paper or Envelopes Procedure

The SOURCE system notifies you on-screen when the thermal receipt paper roll on the Epson printer needs to be changed.

Step

Action

Epson, Loading Paper

1

Remove the old paper and plastic core from the printer. Insert the new roll.

2

Take the loose end of the paper, pull it forward toward you, close the lid and tear off the exposed paper.

Lexmark, Loading Paper

1

Remove the paper tray. Adjust the length guide to the correct length for your material.

2

Load the paper into the tray making sure it lies flat. If using a pre-printed form, have it face down. Do not load paper above the paper capacity mark. Adjust the width guide so it touches the edge of the stack of media.

3

Reinsert the paper tray.

Note:

  • To avoid paper jams, DO NOT overload the trays.
  • Make sure the adjustable paper guides are snug against the edges of the paper.
  • Don't remove paper from the feeder or remove the paper tray while printing.
  • Flex, fan, and straighten the paper before loading it.
  • Never load wrinkled, creased, damp, or highly curled paper into a tray or the multipurpose feeder.
  • Never mix paper types in a tray or the multipurpose feeder.

Lexmark, Loading Envelopes

1

Gently adjust the envelope support for the size you're loading.

2

Lift the envelope weight back toward the printer. It stays in place.

Note: Never place objects on the envelope feeder, and avoid pressing down on it or applying excessive force.

3

Slide the envelope edge guide to the far right.

4

Flex a stack of envelopes back and forth and then straighten the edges on a level surface.

5

Press down the corners to flatten them.

6

Fan the envelopes. This prevents the edges of the envelopes from sticking together and helps them feed properly.

7

Load envelopes evenly into the printer.

Note: When you load envelopes, do not exceed the maximum stack height of 85 envelopes.
