ASN Information Not in the System Procedure

If the message: INVALID ASN NUMBER appears on the screen after scanning the ASN pallet label, it is likely that the ASN information is not in the system.

Step

Action

1

Instead of printing a detailed list of everything that is short, enter the following information for the Pallet Variance Report if no ASNs are found and a report must be created:

  • Load ID
  • Ship DSC
  • Ship Date
  • Bill-of-Lading
  • Bill-of-Lading Pallet Count
  • Received Pallet Count

2

After two copies of the Pallet Variance Report ensure that the driver sign both copies. The driver may choose to look in the truck for any missing pallets or call the dispatcher for instructions.

Note: Ensure that any pallet shortage is identified on the BOL and receive a signed and dated copy noting the shortage. Give the driver a copy of the signed Pallet Variance Report. If the driver is not available maintain both copies at the store.

3

Later in the day check to see if the ASN information is in your system by scanning one of the ASN Pallet Labels.
From the POS Desktop, go to Main Menu -> Merchandise Management -> Receive/Verify ->  ASN.

4

When the ASN information is in the system, follow the normal receiving process by scanning every ASN Pallet Label.

Note: For more information, see STAR Order Receiving Overview.
