MMS Scorecard Overview

The MMS Scorecard is purposed to assist you with the continued review of the Merchandise Management System, providing a summary of key areas needing additional attention and key areas progressing well. To gain the full benefits of the system, review the Scorecard monthly and address areas that need attention.

 

Area

Summary

1

Total Star Orders

The number of Star Orders placed.

2

Suggested Star Orders

The number of Suggested Star Orders generated. Indicates time savings by using the Suggested Star.

3

Modified Lines on Suggested Star
  • Modified lines generated on the Suggested Star Order.
  • The number includes any changes from the suggested quantity including products added to the order.
  • The perpetual inventory and the Min/Max levels should be adjusted to reflect the changes made to the Suggested Star Order.
  • Continued adjustments to the Suggested Star Order would indicate perpetual inventory variances and/or unaddressed Min/Max issues.

4

Number of Manual Purchase Orders
  • The number of Manual B prefix Purchase Orders used.
  • A large number of manual purchase orders indicate a duplication of effort in placing orders since the manual purchase order must be manually written then entered into the system.
  • Without entering the Manual Purchase Order into MMS, the invoice will not get paid.

5

Internal Orders Received Greater Than 24 Hrs.
  • The number of internal orders (with the exception of ISRs) received 24 hours after a receipt should have been recorded.
  • Timely receiving of purchase orders on the MMS is critical for accurate perpetual inventory and cutoffs.
  • The perpetual inventory and inventory cutoffs are directly affected by improper receiving techniques. If this number is high; review receiving procedures.

6

No Matching Receipts Internal
  • The number of internal invoices (including ISTs) presented to be matched but the purchase order referenced on the invoice has not been received and accepted.
  • A high number indicates that internal purchase orders are not being received on the system.
  • Timely receiving of purchase orders on the MMS is critical for accurate perpetual inventory and cutoffs.

7

No Matching Receipts External
  • The number of external invoices presented to be matched where the purchase order, referenced on the invoice, has not been received and accepted.
  • A high number indicates that external purchase orders are not being received timely on the system.
  • Timely receiving of Purchase Orders on MMS is critical for accurate perpetual inventory, vendor relations, and inventory cutoffs.
  • Store Managers are responsible for timely closing all External Purchase Orders appearing in received status.

8

Total SKUs with Min/Max

  • The number of DSC SKUs on the store's Min/Max file.
  • District, Area, and Division rollup reports will be averaged.
  • Review Min/Max tabs to ensure that each tab has a completed date. A Suggested STAR Order should not be generated until all Min/Max tabs have been completely reviewed.

9

SKUs with Min/Max changes
  • The number of SKUs with adjusted Min/Max levels. This number will only appear when the complete tab in the plan-o-gram has been reviewed.
  • District, Area, and Division rollup reports will be averaged.
  • Min/Max levels must be correct to create an accurate Suggested Star Order.
  • Review Min/Max tabs to ensure that each tab has a completed date. A Suggested STAR Order should not be generated until all Min/Max tabs have been completely reviewed.

10

Unique SKUs Cycle Counted
  • The number of unique SKUs where Cycle Counts are being performed.
  • Low numbers indicate that Cycle Counts are not being performed. It may also indicate that Cycle Counts are being performed for the same products.
  • Any variances need to be resolved with an expense transaction, correction transaction, or an inventory adjustment.
  • The purpose of the Cycle Count is to ensure that all products are ordered, received, and sold using the same sales number. It is a periodic count of a small selection of products. Store employees have the ability to check the actual on hand quantity to the perpetual quantity.
  • Generate a Cycle Count Report to ensure that weekly Cycle Counts are performed to check perpetual inventory. Cycle Counts do not have to be performed on a single day. The cycle counts can be spread over several days.

11

Total SKUs on Perpetual

  • The number of SKUs with perpetual inventory records.
  • Generate a Cycle Count Report to ensure that weekly Cycle Counts are performed to check perpetual inventory. Cycle Counts do not have to be performed on a single day. The cycle counts can be spread over several days.
  • Any variances need to be resolved with an expense transaction, correction transaction, or an inventory adjustment.

12

SKUs with Negative Inventory

  • The number of SKUs that have a negative perpetual inventory balance.
  • These items must be counted in addition to the twenty items included in the weekly cycle counts. Any variances need to be resolved with an expense transaction, correction transaction, or an inventory adjustment.
  • District, Area, and Division rollup reports will be averaged.
  • Verify that all products with a negative balance have been included in the weekly Cycle Counts. On the manager's terminal, generate a Negative Inventory Report. This report takes approximately 15 minutes to generate and therefore should not be generated at a Point of Sale terminal. If weekly Cycle Counts have included products with negative balances, this report should be minimal.

13

Lines Sold by Class Number
  • The number of transaction lines where a Class Number was used to sell a product, not including Intermixes, Powder Coatings, or Spray Equipment Rentals and Repairs.

14

Number of Local SKUs

  • The number of local SKUs set up at the store on the stores' perpetual inventory.
  • District, Area, and Division rollup reports will be averaged.
  • An Inventory Adjustment Report is required to verify that Sales Numbers have been assigned for all class-controlled products.

15

Local SKUs with no sales
  • The number of local SKUs that are on the stores' perpetual inventory with no sales activity.
  • A consistently high number could indicate obsolete products that are not turning in inventory. These products should be returned to the vendor, sold, or recorded as obsolete, if appropriate.
  • District, Area, and Division rollup reports will be averaged.
  • Try to identify why there have been no sales for these local SKUs. They could be selling using the old Class Number or it could indicate obsolete products that are not turning in inventory.

16

Uncleared Return Dispositions

  • The number of Return or Correction transactions that have not been resolved.
  • A high number means the manager is not accounting for merchandise returned to the store. This could indicate the lack of credit for returned merchandise.

17

ASN Possibles
  • Number of lines that can be received by ASN.
  • Receiving by ASN is the most accurate method for receiving merchandise from the DSC.

18

Received by ASN
  • Number of lines that were received by ASN.
  • This area shows the actual number of lines that were received by ASN.

19

Lines Received manually
  • Number of lines that were received manually.
  • This entry should be very low if you are receiving by ASN. This area shows the number of lines that were received manually.
