Prepare Ready-to-Go Inventory Procedure

Step

Action

1

Identify any potential obsolete product in your store that will be counted as First Time Obsolescence at the upcoming November Physical Inventory.

  1. For most GOM products, this will be product with a batch date of 3 years or more prior to December 31st of the current year.  Please refer to the Product Data Pages to determine the product’s shelf life.
  2. Any S-W manufactured paint products discontinued prior to January 1st of the current year.
  3. For associated products, brushes and rollers, floorcovering, and spray equipment, this will be product purchased 2 years or more prior to January 1st of the current year.
  4. For wallcovering, this will be book order wallcovering on hand for more than 31 days or product that cannot be returned to the vendor for credit.
  5. For window treatment, this will be product on hand for more than 31 days or product that cannot be returned to the vendor for credit.
  6. For automotive only: The majority of National branded product lines (Ultra, ATX, Genesis, Dimension, etc) have a shelf life of 84 months (7 years), with the exception of AWX which has a shelf life of 36 months (3 years). The above shelf lives DO NOT apply to every product within the product line.

Note: For automotive only: Do not count any inventory that has been written off as obsolescence during a PHASE 3 write-off from a prior November Physical Inventory.

2

Identify any Obsolete product counted at the November Inventory. Do not mark these with an RTG sticker.

3

Segregate the Prior Obsolete product in the warehouse and clearly mark as being already obsolete. 

4

Apply the approved Stickers to the lid of each product that is counted as RTG so that it can be easily identified from the warehouse floor.

5

Marked products should be moved to the front of each pallet or warehouse shelf.

6

Instruct all employees that these labeled items are to be pulled and sold first when ordered / purchased by a customer.

Note: When selling the product, remove the RTG sticker.

7

These products MUST be identified, properly labeled, and recorded.
