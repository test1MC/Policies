Promo Property Listing FA217 Overview

This section applies to Automotive Branches only

This report details all of the approved capitalized promotional equipment that has been provided to our customers over the years.  You will find details on the report such as the customer name, ePromo number, original cost, and net book value of each piece of promotional equipment that is currently assigned to your branch.  It is available on the Automotive Field Reports website and is published on a monthly basis.

  • This field report needs to be referenced each time you are filling out a new ePromo that involves providing a customer with transferred equipment.  This will allow us to capture the most accurate NBV and it should help more accurately estimate the equipment's worth. 
  • If the equipment being transferred is listed on this field report, fill out a Promotional Merch Transfer/Retirement and either attach in the ePromo or submit to Accounting. 
  • Even if the branch number is not changing and the equipment is moving from one customer to another, this form still needs to be filled out.
  • Ensure when filling out the form you list the asset number for each item, and if the equipment being transferred is not listed on the Promo Property Listing FA217 report, do not list it on the form because it is not approved capitalized equipment.
  • Ensure equipment is properly assigned to your branch in order to confirm you are being charged with the correct monthly amortization, which hits the P&L on line 34, MGN ADJ - Promo Expense.
  • This report is different than the normal property listing; this report only shows approved capitalized promotional equipment.  You should continue to also review your normal property listing for fixed assets within the branch.
  • When reviewing the monthly report, if you think a change in assignment on certain equipment is necessary, please send an email to kathleen.m.dickey@sherwin.com or call Kathleen Dickey (216-566-3136). In the email, advise what asset number you are referring to and where the equipment should properly go. 
  • Before any promotional equipment is moved to a new branch, that new branch will be contacted to ensure the equipment re-assignment is accurate.
