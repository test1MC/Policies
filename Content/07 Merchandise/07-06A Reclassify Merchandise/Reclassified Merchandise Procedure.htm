Reclassified Merchandise Procedure

All Sherwin-Williams' products have designated life spans. For example, if an associated product is not sold during the course of its life span, it is removed from the active inventory and reclassified as non-current. The SMIS (Store Management Information System) system allows the company to monitor the sale of non-current, obsolete, discontinued, and unapproved merchandise.

Type

Description

Non-current Merchandise

After a period of time, based on product line, non-current merchandise is reclassified as obsolete and written off the inventory. (Refer to the current Annual Inventory Instructions for more information.)

Obsolete Merchandise

Merchandise may only be placed in obsolescence after the annual October 31st inventory. DO NOT store expense merchandise prior to inventory to avoid obsolescence.

Discontinued Merchandise

Does not apply to Automotive Branches

Re-label according to the procedures listed below. The Store Manager must also make sure that the merchandise is inventoried correctly.

Unapproved Merchandise

Does not apply to Automotive Branches

Non-manufactured merchandise not authorized for purchase by a Sherwin-Williams' store. Unapproved merchandise can also be items purchased from an unapproved vendor. (If these items are discovered in a store's inventory, the store will be asked to reclassify them.)
