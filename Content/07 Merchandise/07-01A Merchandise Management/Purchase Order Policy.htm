Purchase Order Policy

The Purchase Order process must be used to order all merchandise for your location.  If the store computer system is inoperable, use a manual B Prefix Purchase Order.  Enter this Purchase Order once the system is operating properly.  For more information, see Downtime Processing Instructions Overview.

  1. It is the Store Manager's responsibility to review and take appropriate action on all Purchase Orders.
  2. All physically received merchandise must be tied to a Purchase Order. All items must be received against a Purchase Order on the store computer system within 24 hours of physical receipt.
  3. All items must be sales number [SKU] controlled at the store location in order to maintain accurate unit movement and inventory value. The manufacturer part number, when available, should be included when establishing a sales number.
  4. Once a Purchase Order is released, any additional merchandise needed from the vendor requires a new Purchase Order.
  5. Additional items must be received against a Purchase Order even if they were not originally ordered.
  6. Free or No Charge merchandise must be placed on the same purchase order with the merchandise that qualifies you for the Free or No Charge merchandise. The promotion name must be noted in the COMMENTS [F9] section of the free line item. This is necessary for proper pricing of the promotional line item. Additional "free" merchandise at your regular cost must be placed on a separate purchase order because the sales number already appears on the purchase order at a zero cost.
  7. Headquarters will download a special Purchase Order for all automatic shipments. These will appear as Call-For-Orders.
  8. Purchase Orders must be used for all store expense merchandise. Please make sure that you use the proper Expense Merchandise Category. Received expense merchandise is automatically expensed as long as you use the Expense Merchandise Category.
