Cash for Trash Recycling Program – Safety & Environmental Rules Policy

  1. Employees must be trained on and understand the rules of the program before being allowed to accept a customer's or contractor's pails for recycling..
  2. The container is defined as empty and dry if no more than ¼ inch of residue remains in the pail and it is dry to the touch. Pails that are tacky or just skimmed over are not dry and cannot be accepted.
  3. Pails that do not meet the criteria of dry and empty must not be accepted into the recycling program and must be taken back by the customer.
  4. Stores are prohibited from cleaning, scraping, air drying, rinsing, pressure washing or otherwise removing paint and related materials from pails or lids on Sherwin-Williams property.
  5. Customers are prohibited from cleaning, scraping, air drying, rinsing, pressure washing or otherwise removing paint and related materials from pails or lids on Sherwin-Williams property.
  6. Stores are prohibited from storing, stacking or placing pails outside (even if they are dry), and customers are prohibited from leaving, abandoning, or otherwise discarding pails outside the store or near the dumpster.
  7. Contractors must bring the pails to the store with the lids removed.
  8. Pails that have been used to mix, blend, or otherwise store solvent based products, pesticides, insecticides, mildewcides, rust inhibitors, acoustical insulators, or other activities cannot be returned.
  9. Pails that have been used to mix, blend, or otherwise store other than latex or waterborne products cannot be returned.
  10. All pails are to be inspected by Sherwin-Williams' personnel before being accepted into the recycling program and stored on the premises. The inspecting employee should place their initials on each pail being accepted as a return.
  11. Stores are prohibited from driving to the customer’s site and picking up pails, drums, or other containers for the recycling program.
  12. Pails that have been brought in by a customer and are nested must be separated and each pail must be inspected to ensure it is empty and dry.
