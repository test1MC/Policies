Concealed IBAR Overview

A Concealed Damage, Shortage or Overage problem typically is identified while putting away the STAR order. Usually, these are items in repacked boxes or small packages that were not included during shrink wrapping at the DSC. A Concealed Damage, Shortage or Overage IBAR does not result in a return of goods to the DSC; however, it does result in a credit to the store or Paint & Coatings. 

For example:

  • Concealed Shortage: Store ordered product “A” and was shipped product “A” but did not receive the entire shipment.
  • Concealed Overaged: Stored ordered product “A” but received more than the quantity shipped.

A Concealed Damage/ Shortage / Overage IBAR can also be generated for merchandise that a store was billed for but never received.  The process provides many benefits, including:

  • No IBAR mailing with support will need to be sent to Headquarters.
  • Tracking mechanisms are used so performance can be gauged at both the store and DSC level.
  • Credit is automatic.
