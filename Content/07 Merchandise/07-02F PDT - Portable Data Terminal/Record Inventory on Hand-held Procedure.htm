Record Inventory on Hand-held Procedure

The use of the hand-held scanner will make the recording of inventory more efficient. It is important to be sure that the store is well organized. All merchandise should be properly received against Purchase Orders prior to taking inventory.

Step

Action

1

Select Record Inventory from the MMS Main Menu.

2

At this point, it is necessary to divide the store into logical locations for recording inventory. The system gives 24 possible locations to capture your inventory count:

  • Eight areas for the front of the store - F1-F8,
  • Eight areas for the backroom - B1-B8
  • Eight areas for other locations - X1-X8 (i.e. remote sites)

3

When prompted to identify the location you are going to record inventory, enter F (front room), B (back room) or X (other location) followed by the number 1-8 to identify the location.

4

After identifying the location, enter the Sales Number, Product (REX) Number, scan the UPC Code or manually enter the UPC Number when prompted for the Sales/Product Number.

5

After executing one of these methods, the product description and size will return to the screen. Verify that this is the product being counted and then enter the quantity at the QTY prompt.

6

Press Enter to record the count.

7

The scanner is now ready to count the next product in that location. If it is necessary to change locations, press CANCEL [F5] then identify the new location. Store-generated inventory reports will identify the location with the counts making the perpetual to physical variances easier to reconcile.

SALES NUMBER

1118-99993

SZ: 16 GALLON

LOCATION: FRONT 1

8

If previously recorded a quantity for a product in this location, the hand-held scanner will display, You have already recorded a count of 52 in this location. Press Enter to continue. This question will only appear if a previous quantity for a sales number in this location has been entered.

9

If the quantity is correct, press Enter. If needing to change the quantity in this location, enter the new quantity. The new quantity will overlay the previously entered amount.

10

Continue this process to record inventory in each area of the store.
