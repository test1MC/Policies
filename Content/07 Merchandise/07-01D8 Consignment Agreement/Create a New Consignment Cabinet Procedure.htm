Create a New Consignment Cabinet Procedure

This section only applies to Automotive branches and Product Finishes locations

It is critical that an approved consignment agreement is signed by the customer prior to merchandise being placed.

Step

Action

Store Level

1

Obtain approval from the Region Director of Sales prior to giving merchandise to a customer.

Note: This approval must be completed and documented through the E-Promo process. Automotive Accounting will review and validate all consignment arrangements submitted through E-Promo before final approval is authorized.

2

List all proposed consigned merchandise on a Consignment Inventory - Proposal For ePromo Approval Form (found in the Forms Repository), and attached to the E-Promo request.

Note: The selling price to the customer and branch cost must be included on the proposal document for each item. The notes section of the E-Promo should include comments regarding the reason/ business benefit for the requested consignment. Notes should include information regarding the customer’s proximity to the branch and specific shop’s needs.

3

Confirm that the proposed customer has a current credit account in good standing.

Note: A reserve will be established to cover the consigned amount in an Inventory Adjustment account, prime-sub 3720-099 when the consignment is placed. 20% will be allocated to the branch and 80% to the Division, per Division Policy.

Automotive Accounting Level

4

After approval, Automotive Accounting will prepare and forward a customer specific consignment agreement for the customer and SW Rep to sign.  

Sales Representative Level

5

The Sales Rep should meet with the customer, sign the agreement on behalf of SW, and obtain the customer’s signature.

Note: A copy of the of the agreement signed by both parties must be kept at the branch, one copy left with the customer, and the original sent to Automotive Accounting.  It is advisable to bring three copies to the customer’s location, or ensure in advance that a copier is available.

Store Level

6

Finalize the Consignment Inventory Master Form to mirror exactly the merchandise that was processed for consignment via store expense.

7

Print a copy of the form to be signed by the customer.

Note: The customer should sign each page of the Consignment Inventory Master Form, acknowledging receipt of all listed merchandise.

8

Make two copies and distribute as follows: 1) the original should be kept on file at the branch, 2) one photo copy given to the customer for their records. 3) A second photocopy to be marked “permanent” and attached/ placed inside the consignment cabinet.
