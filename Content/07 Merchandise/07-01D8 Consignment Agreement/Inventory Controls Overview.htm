Inventory Controls Overview

Control

Description

Tracking Product Usage

An Automotive Customer Tracking Report is available monthly through Source Reports for each customer that details usage of all products to specific customers (click on the toolbar binoculars and enter the customer’s account number to expedite locating information). As the Sales Representative visits consignment customers, it will be his /her responsibility to review inventory levels and determine if they should be increased or decreased.

Changing Products

Changing the products consigned and/or levels of consigned inventory should be handled in POS via a new Store Expense (adding a new product), or a Store Expense Cancel (removing a product). The Region Director of Sales will receive a monthly report detailing from Automotive Accounting containing all consignments by branch and customer for final verification. 

Increasing Consignment Levels

If the need arises to increase consignment levels more than 10% above what was initially approved, a supplemental ePROMO request must be made and approved prior to placing merchandise. If you have questions, please contact Automotive accounting to discuss.

Physical Inventory Counts

Semi-annually (April and October), Branches will be responsible for a physical inventory count of all consigned merchandise. Do not include a count of your consigned merchandise in your branch physical inventory count.  Automotive Accounting will make closing report adjustments to value consigned merchandise.  The Branch Manager or designated employee will travel with the Sales Representative and perform a physical inventory of the cabinets, comparing the inventory levels to the Consignment Inventory Master Form. Results of the semi-annual inventories must be documented and placed in the customer file in the Branch for audit purposes.

Handling Discrepancies

In the event that there is an inventory discrepancy in which the shop owner refuses to be liable for an inventory shortage, the Rep or Branch Manager must contact the Area Sales Manager. All parties should work together to resolve the issue. Any unresolved discrepancies or resolutions that require expensing additional merchandise must be immediately reported to Automotive Accounting.
