Consignment Agreement Overview

A consignment is when Sherwin-Williams retains ownership of the products it supplies to a customer, until the occurrence of certain events. These events should be defined in the written Consignment Agreement between Sherwin-Williams and its customer prior to supplying any products to the customer on a consignment basis. In order to enter into such a relationship with a customer you are required to obtain and retain on file at the store supplying such products, the prior written approval from your Division: (a) Controller; (b) District Manager [CC Regional Sales Director or Automotive Area Manager]; (c) District Credit Office; and (d) Loss Prevention Department. In addition, you will be required to adhere to the policies and procedures set forth.

The Consignment Agreement is available in the Forms Repository. Search using the keyword, consignment, in the Form Name field. All available forms containing your search word will appear below in the form description field, including the following inventory forms:

  • Proposal For ePromo Approval
  • Consignment Inventory Master
  • Consignment Inventory Tracking

The Consignment Agreement shall commence on the date identified on the Consignment Agreement and shall continue thereafter until terminated by either party hereto upon:

  • 10 days prior written notice to the non-terminating party, or
  • The occurrence of any of the termination events as set forth in the Consignment Agreement.
