Request for Consignment Agreement Procedure

Step

Action

1

Complete the Request for Consignment Agreement and forward it to your District Credit Office to begin the approval process.

If

Then

Credit is approved
  • The District Credit Office will forward the request to the District Manager [CC Regional Sales Director or Automotive Area Manager] who will submit it for final Division approval.
  • If the consignment agreement is approved, the District Manager [CC Regional Sales Director or Automotive Area Manager] will provide you with a Sherwin-Williams prepared consignment agreement for your customer to sign and return and will also provide additional instructions for managing the consignment process.

2

Customer orders for consigned products may only be processed after receiving written approval from the District Manager [CC Regional Sales Director or Automotive Area Manager] and Division Controller:

  • Include the customer Purchase Order Number
  • Designate the order for delivery
  • Each delivery to the consignment location must be processed on a separate Customer Order.

Note: The employee will be reminded of the Ready for Del orders on the Reminder Screen.

3

A Sherwin-Williams employee must use the Consignment Inventory Reconcilement By Product document to perform a physical inventory of the consigned products weekly (or according the schedule established in the Agreement) to compare the actual inventory of consigned products with the customer order and point of sale transactions.
