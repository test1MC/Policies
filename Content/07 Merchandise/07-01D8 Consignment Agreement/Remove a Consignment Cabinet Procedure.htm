Remove a Consignment Cabinet Procedure

In the event that a consignment cabinet needs to be removed (i.e. - loss of business, decision made to halt consignment), the following steps should be taken:

Step Action

1

Physically count and verify all consignment inventory in the presence of the shop owner against the Consignment Inventory Master Form.

2

Note all inventory shortages on the tracking form.

3

Have the shop owner sign to confirm the product consumption.

Note: Products (toners, clearcoats, etc.), which are partially used, will not be taken back and the shop is responsible for the full value of the product, and proper disposal. Re-saleable inventory should be removed from the shop along with the cabinets.

4

Invoice all consumed products to the customer.

Note: In the event that this invoice remains unpaid and is written off as bad debt, the branch must notify Automotive  Accounting at (216) 566-3136, to inform Accounting that the unpaid balance is the result of a consignment inventory removal. 

5

Complete a POS Store Expense cancellation in order to bring the inventory back into the branch. Automotive Accounting and the Region Director of Sales must be notified that this cabinet is no longer in use.

6

Place all the documents in the customer’s branch file.
