Receive & Verify Purchase Order Policy

  1. The person(s) receiving the order is/are responsible for verifying the accuracy of all shipments received.
  2. Sign and date the bill of lading for the driver. No need to retain the BOL because the document is available via OnBase.
  3. Accept all deliveries by common carrier which are accompanied by a legitimate delivery receipt.
  4. A store employee must open, in the presence of the driver, any carton that appears to be damaged. The store employee must also sign and date the bill of lading and note the damages.
  5. Do not accept a damaged delivery if the damage results in a health hazard to employees.
  6. Incoming shipments cannot be left unattended outside the warehouse area.
  7. All merchandise must be received against the Purchase Order:
    1. Internal orders must be received within POS within twenty-four hours of physical receipt or confirmation of customer direct shipment via mypaintconnection. 
    2. External orders must be billed to the customer and received against the Purchase Order on POS within twenty-four hours of either physical receipt or confirmation of customer direct shipment from the vendor.
  8. Purchase orders shipped directly to customers must be received and billed to the customer within twenty-four hours.
    1. Ensure the order has been received before billing the customer.
  9. Make sure to accept the receipts with the proper quantity and unit cost as soon as possible. An employee could be presented with invoices from vendors with valid purchase orders when there is no receiving information on file. This could mean that the merchandise is at the store, but it has not been received on the system.
  10. If the store employee has at least Level 2 MMS Security, they must approve each line item variance before the whole Purchase Order can be approved as received. They will also have the ability to adjust the received costs until the merchandise line is accepted.
  11. Each line must be designated as complete  to close the Purchase Order. You must have at least Level 2 MMS Security to close Internal Purchase Orders. It is the Store Manager's responsibility to verify and close external Purchase Orders.
  12. For externally purchased products, the system will automatically match received Purchase Orders to vendors' invoices. Any quantity or pricing discrepancies between the received Purchase Order and the vendor's invoice will result in an automatic chargeback generated by Headquarters.
  13. For internally purchased products, the system will automatically match received Purchase Orders to the invoices.
  14. Serial Numbers for spray equipment must be captured during the receiving process. Other special product identification information must be captured in the Special Instructions area. (If serial numbers are not recorded during receiving, you will not be able to sell the merchandise.)
  15. Automotive Branches must record serial numbers for products listed on the Serial Numbered Products Listing. Find the Serial Numbered Products listing in the Forms Repository.
  16. Actual freight must be entered at receiving.
    1. The received freight amount is booked to inventory.
    2. The difference between invoiced and received freight goes to freight variance (margin) account.
    3. Freight is allocated back over all received products proportionately
    4. If you place one purchase order and received several deliveries, make sure that you adjust the current freight charges to reflect only the merchandise that is being received.
  17. Receiving takes the place of reviewing the Stores Purchases Report (SPR) for merchandise. You are still required to review expense items on the Stores Purchases Report.
