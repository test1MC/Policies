Receive Unplanned Product on STAR Order Overview

Occasionally, a store will receive a product on their STAR that they did not order.  Sometimes, this is planned by Sherwin-Williams through the DSCs as a Marketing Promotional Shipment.  Every attempt is made to notify the stores when this is taking place, but occasionally it arrives unexpectedly.  Other times, a product is shipped to the store as a replacement product- this means that a new SKU has replaced an existing SKU that had been ordered.  There could be several reasons for this including a newer version of the product has been introduced or a product has been discontinued. 

When a product has been replaced, the store is made aware of this on their Order Advisory Report found in MMS.  This will often show the SKU that has been sent in its place.  It is vital that the Store Manager check and ensure that any discontinued product has been zeroed out in Min/Max.   If this does not happen, the product may continue to appear on the Suggested STAR and ship into the store.  The reason for this is because the Perpetual Inventory will continue to show you are under the Min amount set since you are not actually receiving the initial product in, only the replacement product.  Be sure to verify that the replacement product also has a Min/Max established. Customer Service can help determine the problem if you need assistance with the Order Advisory Report. The Order Advisory Report can be accessed from your MMS Reports section.
