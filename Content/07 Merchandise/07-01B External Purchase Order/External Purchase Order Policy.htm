External Purchase Order Policy

  1. The only way to place orders for inventory is through the Purchase Order functionality in MMS. Stores are not to purchase inventory through any other means including online ordering, payment by credit card, or payment by Store Draft.
  2. A store manager has the authority to place external purchase orders up to $10,000.
    1. Any external purchase order over the $10,000 limit must have written District Manager approval on file, prior to placing the order with the vendor. Two or more purchase orders placed within 7 days to the same vendor and totaling more than $10,000 must have written District Manager approval prior to placing the purchase order that would take the total over $10,000. Approvals for purchase orders over the approval limit must be retained for eighteen months from date of the purchase order. This approval level does not apply to Automotive Branches, PFG Facilities, Floorcovering Facilities, nor floorcovering purchases by TAG locations.
    2. Free or No Charge merchandise must be placed on the qualifying purchase order but with a different sales number. The promotion name must be noted in the comments section of the free line item, which is necessary for proper pricing. Additional free merchandise at regular cost must be placed on a separate purchase order because the sales number already appears on the purchase order at a zero cost.
    3. Orders for an individual customer must be placed within 24 hours of processing the deposit. If the sale involves installation, do not place the order until the Rescission Notice expires. The Rescission Notice Policy does not apply to Automotive Branches.
    4. The merchandise must be labeled with the sales number and selling price if it will be stocked on the sales floor. Refer to the Inventory Price List if the manufacturer’s suggested selling price was not entered as the selling price on the Purchase Order. For more information, see Inventory Price List Policy.
  3. Regardless of where you get the product, the same sales number should be used to order, receive, label, and sell each product.
  4. Because class numbers allow you to provide definition as to what product you want rather than a direct relational tie as we have with sales numbers, it is possible to mis-use and order other products under these numbers. This should not be done.
  5. It is the Store Manager's responsibility to review and take appropriate action on all Purchase Orders.
  6. All merchandise vendors must have established Credit Accounts and be paid through Accounts Payable. Find an alternative source if the vendor is not willing to establish credit terms with the Company.
  7. The Credit Application must be faxed to Accounts Payable at Headquarters (FAX 216-566-3939). For more information, see New Vendor Request Procedure.
