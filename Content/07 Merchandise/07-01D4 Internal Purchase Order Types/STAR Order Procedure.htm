STAR Order Procedure

Scheduled Transfer and Replenishment [STAR] Orders are to be used for creating the weekly replenishment order(s) from the DSC. Once released, the STAR order will immediately be routed to the DSC through EDI.

Step

Action

1

From the POS Desktop, go to Main Menu -> Merchandise Management -> Purchase Order -> Add New Purchase Order.

2

Select the STAR Order option from the Order Type List. An "A" Purchase Order Number will be assigned by the system.

Note: You do not need to complete entry of all STAR Order information at one time. You may save it without releasing the order to the DSC and continue to work on it throughout the day by selecting Merchandise Management ->Purchase Order -> Change/Release Purchase Order. However, the order must be released by the end of the STAR punch day. If you have already placed your STAR Order with Customer Service using the downtime Manual B-Prefix Purchase Order procedure, override the "A" Purchase Order Number by typing in the "B" Purchase Order Number. You must do this to prevent duplicate receipt.

3

Indicate whether or not a Standard or Suggested STAR will be created:

If Then
Standard STAR Order

Select NO.

Note: The Standard STAR Order process allows you to enter by line. The system will NOT suggest any order quantities to use as a basis for your order. You will need to walk around the store to review on-hand quantity.

Suggested STAR Order Select YES.

Note: Remember that when using the suggested STAR Order option, your order will be created based on current perpetual inventory levels in the system compared to Min/Max levels. If either are inaccurate, do not use the Suggested STAR process.  For more information, see Change & Assign Min & Max Levels Procedure.

For further information on Suggested STAR Orders, see Suggested STAR Order Overview.

4

If applicable, include any Memo Demands to be automatically included for this order period for a Standard STAR Order. For more information, see Memo Demand Policy.

  • The calculation takes into consideration On Hand, On Order, and Memo Demand quantities and will not suggest an order quantity higher than the maximum level except in the two following scenarios:
    • The Memo Demand quantity could take you above your maximum level because Memo Demands are added to the maximum order quantity.  

5

Enter the Sales Number and Quantity desired for each product you wish to receive. Use the information provided for each line item to determine the quantity needed. Add lines, delete lines, and change quantities as required. The following information is provided:

Type

Description

Min

Minimum amount of product that should be at your store.

Max

Maximum amount of product that should be at your store.

O/H

Merchandise that is currently on hand at your store.

O/O

Merchandise that has been ordered but not received.

Min/Ship

Minimum amount of product that will be shipped.

PK SZ

Merchandise package size.

Memo

Merchandise recorded for your customer that needs to included.

Sugg

Calculation of what you should order after considering the above.

Note: The word Auto in the Selling Price column designates that the item will be automatically priced from a Sherwin-Williams' Price List.
6

Once you are confident that you have included all needed items from the DSC on your Standard or Suggested STAR Order, release your order to the DSC.

If

Then

Order is successfully released

You will be returned to the Purchase Order Menu.

Order has not been successfully released

You will see an error message. Follow the EDI Downtime Procedure.

Errors are found on the STAR Order after it has been released to the DSC

Call Customer Service [Arch: 1-888-677-8673 / CC: 1-888-792-4364 / Auto: 1-800-357-4956]. You will need to provide the representative the following information:

  • Store Number
  • STAR Purchase Order Number
  • Order Date
  • Explanation of the error
  • Correct Sales Number, Size and/or Quantity.

If the order CAN be corrected, Customer Service will correct the error before shipping. If the order CANNOT be corrected, you must receive the order as originally placed.  Once the order has been received, you may request approval within forty-eight [48] hours to return the incorrectly ordered merchandise to the DSC if the mistake falls into the return guidelines.

7

After placing your STAR Order, reference the STAR Order Pending & Cancelled Lines Overview to verify which ordered products may not ship. Do not close the Purchase Order until all shipments have been received.

Note: Some STAR orders may be split, which means that stores may receive mulitple STAR orders from one site but only one from another. Even though the orders may be split, stores should place full orders each time.
