Record Damaged Freight on the Delivery Receipt Procedure

When the carrier picks up the material at the Distribution Center, they sign for and accept the accuracy of the piece count that appears on the Bill-of-Lading. The Branch receives a Delivery Receipt or Freight Bill with the shipment that also shows the total number of pieces that were given to the carrier, which the carrier prepares from the Distribution Center Bill-of-Lading. When the material is delivered, the Branch will be asked to sign the Delivery Receipt.

Receive and Sign the Delivery Receipt:

Step Action

1

Count the number of pieces delivered by the carrier.

Note: Pieces consist of full cartons, un-opened mixed cartons marked with “mixed carton” tape, and items such as pails and drums.

2

Check the number of pieces received with the totals shown on the Delivery Receipt. The numbers should be the same.

If Then

The numbers are the same

Proceed to Step 3.

There is a discrepancy between the two

Note on the Delivery Receipt that the order is short or over by the number of pieces you counted and have the driver verify and sign the Delivery Receipt. The Distribution Center is not responsible for this shortage.

Note: Do not sign a Delivery Receipt as being received complete if it only shows the number of pallets being delivered. It should show the number of pieces. If you sign for the number of pallets, you will be unable to file a claim with either the carrier or the Distribution Center.  In this case note the number of pieces received on the Delivery Receipt, not the number of skids.

3

Inspect the condition of all cartons and open any cartons that are crushed, damaged, or rattled in the presence of the driver.

Note: The Branch should not accept a damaged carton if at least 50% of its contents cannot be salvaged.

4

Document any discrepancies, variances or damage on the Delivery Receipt and have the driver sign it.

If Then

Damages or variances have occurred

Refer to Loss and Damage Claims.

Damage is discovered after the driver leaves

Report to the carrier that this is concealed damage.

Note: An inspection must be requested within 15 days after delivery or it will be almost impossible to collect.

Note: Failure to obtain the driver's signature could result in loss or damage being nullified.

5

Once the piece count and concealed damage problems are resolved with the carrier, substitution errors can be resolved with the Distribution Center.

6

Enter the received items into MMS within 24 hours of receipt and show the date entered into MMS on the Delivery Receipt for inventory integrity.
