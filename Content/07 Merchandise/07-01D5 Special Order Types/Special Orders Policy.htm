Special Orders Policy

  1. Stores are held financially responsible for all inventories placed into the DSC at an employee's request.
  2. It is required to complete the Special Order Log. This form is available via the Forms Repository.  A copy of the Special Order Log form must be retained at the store for the current year plus 3 previous years. The Store Manager must sign the completed Special Order Log and fax to the Plant Manufacturing Coordinator.
  3. All special orders must meet the minimum batch size for the product ordered. [A 10% plus/minus manufacturing variance is considered acceptable.]
  4. A Manual “B” Prefix Purchase Order must be completed for any non-stocked special orders. The Manual “B” Prefix Purchase Order number must be entered on the Special Order Log form before faxing the form to the Plant Manufacturing Coordinators. The Manual “B” Prefix Purchase Order number must be entered into the computer system. 
  5. Store managers, in order to request that inventory of GOM (goods of our manufacture) paints be placed into the DSC, must contact the District Manager for the approval form to be sent to the Duron Division Coordinator. This type of inventory is referred to as a Restricted Stocking/Forecasted Special.
  6. Special stock may be held in the DSC for up to six months following the manufacture date. At the end of sixth month, the store will be notified by the Plant Manufacturing Coordinators to issue a Purchase Order to remove any remaining inventory that has not sold per the stocking special criteria.
  7. The store will receive a weekly Special Products Account Management (SPAM) report detailing the inventory status for all products placed in the DSC on your behalf.
  8. Regardless of Product Status, stores can be restricted from product availability based on criteria [test product, product usage knowledge, VOCs, etc.].  In order to become an approved store for a restricted product, contact the Duron Division Coordinator.
