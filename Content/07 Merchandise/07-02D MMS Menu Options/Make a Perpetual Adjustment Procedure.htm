Perpetual Adjustment

If it cannot determine what happened to the product, a store employee will need to do a Perpetual Adjustment so that the perpetual inventory is correct. If not, the employee will not be able to use the Suggested STAR Order or check stock on the system with any accuracy. Process a Unit Movement Inquiry Report to review all history for the product.
For more information, see Unit Movement Procedure.

The Perpetual Adjustment selection is used to adjust receiving errors and any perpetual adjustments needed as a result of Cycle Counts that could not be traced to a sale, expense or other transaction.

Step

Action

1

From the POS Desktop, select Main Menu -> Merchandise Management -> Inventory Adjustment -> Perpetual Adjustment.

2

A reason must be entered in the memo field to support the adjustment. Once the adjustment has been made, the perpetual inventory is updated.

3

After identifying the reason for the discrepancy, select Inventory Adjustment.

4

Enter the correct sales number or press Lookup [F3].

5

Enter the actual on-hand count for the product in the Actual Count field.

Note: If the adjustment is the result of a receiving error, enter the Purchase Order number when prompted. This will alert Headquarters to compare the received Purchase Order with the Inventory Adjustment. For all other reasons, press Enter through the Purchase Order number field.
