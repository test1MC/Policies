Check the Quantity of Manual Purchase Orders Procedure

A significant number of Manual Purchase Orders being used indicates that employees are not comfortable with automated Purchase Orders. This would also indicate that they are not receiving the benefits of the Merchandise Management System because they are performing duplicate work.

Step

Action

1

Locate the clipboard containing the store copy of the Manual Purchase Orders and find the first date after the District MMS training session.

2

Count the number of Manual Purchase Orders used since training.

If

Then

Number is significant

Inquire with the store staff to determine a reason. Emphasize the various methods to complete a Purchase Order.

  • A Purchase Order can be completed when an employee is placing the order while on the phone with the vendor. Suggest that store employees generate the Purchase Order prior to calling the vendor with the best information available. Then answer No to the question, Would you like to Release this Purchase Order? Recall the Purchase Order by selecting 2. Change/Release a Purchase Order at the time of calling the vendor. This method will enable store employees to see the true benefits of MMS.

Number is insignificant

Proceed to Step 3.

3

Ensure that all manual purchase orders have been entered into the system. Orders not entered into the system are not polled and Headquarters will not pay the vendor.

Step

Action

1

From the POS Desktop, go to Main Menu -> Merchandise Management -> Purchase Order -> Print/View Purchase Order.

2

Enter the start date for the date range when prompted.

3

Press Enter when prompted Enter Ending PO Entered Date to include today.

4

When prompted to Enter Beginning PO Number, enter the letter B to only bring back manual (B Prefix) Purchase Order information. Press Accept.

4

View the Purchase Order Number column to ensure that all manual purchase orders have been entered into the system. A manual purchase order entered into the system will begin with a B prefix and the Purchase Order number with a total of eight characters.

Note:  Every Manual Purchase Order not received by the end of the second day of MMS Implementation must be entered into the system.
