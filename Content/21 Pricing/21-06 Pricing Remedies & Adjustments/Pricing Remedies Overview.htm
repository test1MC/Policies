Pricing Remedies Overview

Price Remedy Reason

Use When

Pricing Accommodation

The customer’s price needs to be adjusted because our business is at risk and the customer is prepared to take their purchase elsewhere.

Post Sale Remedy

A DIY customer purchases products during a sale event and comes in after the sale event to purchase the same product at the sale price to finish a job. This remedy will only be active and should only be used for a specified length of time after the National Sales Event. This length of time is predetermined for each sale.

Unregistered Coupon Remedy

A customer presents a coupon that is not registered in the Point-of-Sale system.

Substitution Remedy

One product or size must be substituted for another due to a stock out.

Correction Remedy

You need to change a price on the positive side of a transaction. For example, if a customer received an incorrect price before an increase, then employees can use this remedy on the positive side of a correction instead of changing the customer’s PRC.

Shelf Label Remedy

The scanned price at the time of sale is higher than either the shelf price or the advertised price at the time of sale.

Visiting Customer Remedy

The home store cannot immediately update the customer’s PRC.

Ready to Go (RTG) & Overstock Remedy

An RTG product is being sold or when an overstock situation cannot be handled through store specials (i.e. bulk sale of overstock to one customer).

Note: You can dictate the price to customers on an individual basis.  

Note: Access Order Express for copies of US and Canadian Price Remedy Scan Sheets. New sheets will be mailed annually.
