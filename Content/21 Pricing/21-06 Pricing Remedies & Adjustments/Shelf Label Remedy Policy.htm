Shelf Label Remedy Policy

  1. If the scanned price at the time of sale is higher than either the shelf price or the advertised price at the time of sale, the customer will immediately receive the lower price upon notification of the error.
  2. Customers have thirty (30) days from the date of the original transaction to notify Sherwin-Williams of price discrepancies between charged price (displayed on customer receipt) and shelf label pricing or advertised pricing at time of the original sale. 
  3. Claims of pricing errors within 40% of the current list price of paints and 30% of the current list price of brushes, rollers, and associated products on the date of the claim will be refunded with proof of purchase (receipt). Adjustments above these percentages require District Manager approval.
  4. An adjustment to promotional pricing outside of promotion dates is not permitted.
  5. Sherwin-Williams stores are also subject to additional state requirements regarding the handling of scanned price errors.
    1. MICHIGAN ONLY: If a scanning error is not corrected at the time of purchase, customers are also entitled to a bonus payment in addition to a refund of the difference between the displayed price and scanned (charged) price. This bonus amount should be equal to 10x the difference in price. However, this bonus amount can never be less than $1.00 nor more than $5.00. In order to receive the bonus payment, the customer must notify Sherwin-Williams within thirty (30) days from the date of purchase and provide their receipt. Sherwin-Williams must refund the difference between the charged and the advertised or shelf label price and pay the bonus amount to the customer within two (2) days of notification. In addition, the bonus payment can only be applied once per unique sales SKU in a transaction. At this time, bonus payments can only be paid in cash. (
      1. For example: Customer buys two of the same brush. Shelf Label reads $10. Customer Charged $11. Customer is owed the difference of $1 per brush for a total of $2. In addition, a bonus payment of $5 is required. 10x the difference of $1 is $10, but the cap on the bonus payment is $5 and the bonus payment can only be applied to a single instance of the item.
    2. Bonus payments are to be processed under Non-Merchandise Code Price Label Error Penalty. These amounts will book to 7402-028 Fines and Penalties.
  6. If a customer or state pricing auditor requests a copy of our price discrepancy policy, please print and provide the Sherwin-Williams Price Discrepancy Policy.
    1. New York, Wisconsin, and San Diego County require an in-store sign posting of the Sherwin-Williams Price Discrepancy Policy.
    2. San Diego County also requires stores that fail a scanner inspection must post a Department of Agriculture, Weights, and Measures Sign (available at county website) provided by the auditor after failing the inspection. Stores should be aware of the County of San Diego Scanner Awareness Program.
  7. The Store Manager must review all shelf label errors and use Shelf Labels on Demand to print and display new shelf labels for each error.
  8. PAINT STORES ONLY: If the price remedy barcode “Shelf-Label Error” is used during a transaction, the system will track required corrections. These corrections must be made using Shelf Labels on Demand within 24 (twenty-four) hours.
  9. A Correct Shelf Label Error report will generate in Remedy Reports.
  10. A shelf label report will automatically print when Start of Day is run.
  11. The Status Reminder Box also displays a Shelf Error counter, just below the Return Disposition Report area.
