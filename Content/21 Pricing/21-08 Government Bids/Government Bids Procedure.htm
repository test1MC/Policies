Government Bids Procedure

Step

Action

1

Read the entire bid and all attachments. Note the length of time the store will have to hold the prices quoted, and determine if the bid is a Federal Government, City, State, or County Bid.

2

Check to see if an officer of the Company is required to sign. If so, the Division President is the only person in the Division that is authorized to sign the bid.

Note: Automotive Branches may have the Division President, Vice President, or Controller sign the bid.

3

Verify that Sherwin-Williams can provide the requested products.

4

Obtain written product guarantees and/or warranties from suppliers before entering a bid.

Note: Review all local fire regulations before recommending products in a government bid.

5

Review the store prices for the requested items. Obtain written guarantees on prices from the suppliers before entering the bid. If the delivery date is far in the future, you may need to get an escalation clause from the supplier.

6

Contact the District Manager if a competitive price is below store authority.

7

When bidding on an installation job, get a firm price from the installer for all installation costs, so that extra charges do not reduce the store's profit. Ensure the store has a sub-contractor's agreement on file for all installers used on a government project.

Note: For more information on sub-contractors, see .

8

Ask the District Credit Manager to approve credit for the government office requesting the bid. The District Credit Manager will also have the contract reviewed by a legal authority.

9

Send all copies of any Federal Government Bid to the Wholesale Marketing Department. Wholesale Marketing will have the bid signed and returned to the store. Automotive Branches should forward the bid to Financial Services.
