Gross Profit Overview

The Gross Profit is the difference between a product's total cost and its selling price. The total cost is the cost of the product, inbound freight (if not included in the delivery price), and any handling or cutting charges. Floor Covering Use Tax or any Hazardous Waste fees should be considered as part of the cost when developing a selling price but should not be used in the calculation of gross profit. To calculate the gross profit, subtract the total cost from the selling price, then divide that amount by the selling price. Refer to the vendor invoice, the purchase order, or the store cost book to find the cost.

Note: For information on Gross Profit Tax, see Use Tax Overview. For more information on Spray Equipment, see Gross Profit Worksheet for Rentals Overview.
