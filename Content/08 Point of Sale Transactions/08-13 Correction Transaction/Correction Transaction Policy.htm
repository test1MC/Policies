Correction Transaction Policy

  1. Employees are not permitted to process any of their own point of sale transactions. Another employee must process the transaction.
  2. While processing a correction transaction, all original information must be entered from the original transaction.
  3. Customer information (name, address, city, state, zip code, telephone, customer signature) must be complete and accurate (no abbreviations) anytime the customer receives cash or credit card return or a store draft on a Cash Refund or Cash Correction transaction.
  4. Employees  are required to enter customer specific information while processing Preferred Customer or Corrections.
  5. Employees are required to enter the name of the person signing all Sherwin-Williams Charge Correction Transactions.
  6. A Store Draft should be used if there is not enough cash in the drawer to cover the refund or if the store would be left with insufficient operating funds.
  7. The customer must sign the correction transaction if receiving a refund.
  8. A Correction Transaction should not be used when merchandise is returned and the customer makes an additional purchase. A return transaction should be processed to return merchandise and a sales transaction should be processed to sell new merchandise.
  9. The employee must sign the correction transaction.
  10. Sales corrections can be used to correct errors discovered after an invoice has been run.
  11. Any pricing adjustments should be made at the original location.
