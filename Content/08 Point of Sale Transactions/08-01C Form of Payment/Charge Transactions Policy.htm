Charge Transactions Policy

  1. Each Sherwin-Williams store has its own charge customers. However, a customer may still charge merchandise at other store locations.
  2. District Credit Manager approval is required to establish customer credit. Credit limits for Sherwin-Williams Charge Accounts are established for each individual account.
  3. Any charge transaction with a Credit Lock Out must be entered on a customer order once approved by District Credit. The order must be processed on a charge point-of-sale transaction as soon as the account is unlocked.
  4. Transactions must be processed within 24 hours of delivery after using Customer Order Entry and the Confirmation of Delivery ticket.
  5. EAD, CDN, MWD, SWD:  District Credit Manager approval is required if a customer wishes to charge merchandise to an OVERLIMIT or PAST DUE ACCOUNT.
  6. Ask to see the customer's Drivers License for any authorized buyer that does not look familiar. Enter the License Number in the Comments Section while processing the transaction.
  7. Store employees may not provide blank or store documents to anyone.
  8. Credit policy is circumvented when a store employee uses their personal charge account to charge merchandise for others. This is a conflict of interest and is subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination. If an employee is found using a personal charge account for others, contact Division Loss Prevention.
  9. You are required to enter the name of the person signing all Sherwin-Williams Charge.
  10. The customer's signature is required on all Charge transactions.
  11. Sherwin-Williams employees are NOT permitted to pre-bill merchandise.
  12. SW Charge Accounts cannot be used with other forms of payment to split orders.
