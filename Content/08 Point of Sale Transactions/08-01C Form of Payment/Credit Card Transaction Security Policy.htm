Credit Card Transaction Security Policy

To prevent credit card fraud, take the following security precautions when completing a credit card transaction:

  1. NOT APPROVED: Return the card to the customer and ask for another form of payment or void the sale. If the customer has any questions, refer them to their bank.
  2. Always check the expiration date on the credit card. Do not accept an expired credit card.
  3. Verify that the signature on the back of the credit card matches the customer's signature on the credit card form.
  4. If there is a reason to suspect that the credit card is invalid or does not belong to the customer making the sale, ask the customer for positive identification.
  5. All processed manual forms, including imprinted manual credit card forms and processed manual POST forms, must be retained in a folder titled MANUAL POST FORMS – USED and placed in a locked filing cabinet or locked store safe to protect customer information. The processed manual POST form must be retained for 5 years plus the current year. However, the manual credit card imprint may be cross-cut shredded after 18 months. For more information, see Credit Card Information Retention and Disposal Policy and Destroy Records Policy.
  6. Questions related to credit card processing or ordering Sales/Credit invoices should be directed to the following:
    1. US Paint Stores: Contact Chase Merchant Services at 1-800-326-7990.
    2. Canadian Paint Stores: Contact First Data Customer Service at 1-888-263-1938. You will need your 11-digit Merchant ID Number. If fraud is suspected, call for code 10 authorization from First Data at 1-800-370-0466.
    3. Any questions related to actual credit card deposits on the Profit and Loss Analysis Report (P&L) should be directed to Store Accounting at 216-566-2460.
