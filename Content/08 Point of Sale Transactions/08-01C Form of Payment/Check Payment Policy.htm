Check Payment Policy

A transaction is considered a check payment whenever a customer uses a Personal Check, Business Check, Travelers Check, or Money Order to pay for merchandise. Some customers use a combination of cash and check to pay for a single purchase.

  1. Employees are not permitted to process their own point of sale transactions. This includes ALL point of sale transactions. Another employee must process the point of sale transaction.
  2. The check must be made out to The Sherwin-Williams Company - no two-party checks. A Joint check can only be accepted when the Joint Check Job Account policy is followed. For more information see Joint Check Agreement Overview.
  3. The check may not be postdated. The check must be dated the same day as the purchase.
  4. Employees are not permitted to hold customer checks for future purchases.
  5. All customer checks must be processed the same day received.
  6. Check cashing is not a valid POS transaction.
  7. Stores may not accept checks for any amount over the actual invoice amount resulting in a balance due the customer. 
    1. A customer may write a check over the invoice amount if the resulting balance due the customer is to be applied as a deposit on a future order or processed as a payment on account. 
      1. Note that this will result in a discrepancy in the daily check log, but will not cause a register imbalance.
  8. Sherwin-Williams employees in all divisions are not permitted to receive cash back from a transaction paid by check.
  9. A driver's license is the only form of identification required to accept a check as a method of payment. Do not request a credit card as a second form of identification.
  10. Temporary New Account Checks: Checks where the Name and Address are not pre-printed should not be accepted without first contacting the bank to verify the account balance. If you are unable to verify account balance, please ask the customer for another form of payment.
  11. Travelers Checks and Money Orders: Process through the computer system using the same guidelines as a personal check. The customer’s signature should be witnessed at the point of sale. Process Traveler's Checks and Money Orders by selecting Check as the Form of Payment.
  12. NSF Checks: Checks for a cash sale or accommodation transactions may not be accepted from customers with current unpaid NSF checks or NSF fees. 
  13. Canada ONLY: Telecheck is used only when a business needs to pay by check. The total of the check cannot exceed $2,500.
    1. Stores must contact Telecheck for an approval code number.
      1. Note that if Telecheck denies approval, another form of payment is required to complete the sale.
    2. Approval number must be included on the back of the check along with the customer’s I.D. type and phone number.
    3. If the check is returned, contact the customer for a replacement, which must include the returned item fee of $50.
      1. Note that if the customer cannot send an immediate replacement then the check must be sent with a completed warranty card within 60 days of the date of the check to the following address:
        Telecheque Head Office
        1100 Cremazie Blvd. East Suit 701
        Montreal, Quebec H2P 2X2
        c/o Cheque Processing
  14. The following disclaimer displays at the bottom of any receipt paid by check: 10 day waiting period for returns on transaction processed by check. This notification should help when a customer wants to return merchandise prior to the 10-day waiting period.
