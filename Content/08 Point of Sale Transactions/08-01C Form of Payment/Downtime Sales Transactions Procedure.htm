Downtime Sales Transactions Procedure

Step

Action

Sales Transaction Downtime

1

Determine the Downtime error:

If

Then

Computer System and PAX are Down

Proceed to Step 2.
Computer System is Down

Proceed to Step 2

PAX only is Down (for credit card sales)

Complete Steps 3-6

 

2

 

If

Then

Cash sales paid by cash
  • Capture all customer and order information on the Manual POST Form. This information will be used later when entering the transaction into the Computer System.
  • Proceed to Step 7.

 

Note: Open and Close cash drawers with the key using the top lock release.

Cash sales paid by check
  • Capture all customer  and order information on the Manual POST Form. This information will be used later when entering the transaction into the Computer System.
  • Write “Paid by Check” on the invoice form.
  • Mark the check with the [For Deposit Only] stamp.
  • Proceed to Step 7.

Cash sales paid by credit card
  • Capture all customer  and order information on the Manual POST Form. This information will be used later when entering the transaction into the Computer System.
  • Proceed to Step 3.

Note: Use the Manual POST Form for all transactions at the time of sale. List each merchandise item by sales or class number, quantity, and unit price. Under the Description Heading, label the items purchased as “Merchandise.” Manually calculate the total. Capture all information on the Manual POST Form. Don’t forget to include Payment on Accounts.

PAX Downtime

3
If Then
POS is operational Select Credit from the Form of Payment Menu -> Follow prompts for Manual Authorization.
POS is not operational Proceed to Step 4.

4

To receive authorization, call the appropriate number:

If

Then

US

1-800-430-7161

Canada

1-855-457-0423

5

Provide operator with your Merchant ID (Main POS Menu -> Help Desk -> Credit/Gift Card Downtime), cardholder account, card expiration date, and amount of the sale (including tax).

Note: If POS is not operational and you do not know your Merchant ID, contact the HelpDesk.

6

If

Then

POS is operational

Follow prompts to complete Manual Authorization -> Complete Sale

Note: Do not enter CVV numbers.

POS is not operational

Proceed to Step 7.
POS Downtime

7

Give customer their copy of the Manual POST Form.

8

Retain the store’s copy of the form in a locked filing cabinet or locked store safe until you are ready to batch. All completed Manual Forms must be retained in a locked filling cabinet or locked store safe after batching in a folder title MANUAL POST FORMS – USED.

9

If

Then

Hardware is operational before end of day

From the POS Desktop, select Store -> Point of Sale -> Enter & Validate a Manual Form.

Note: The computer will not call for authorization when a Manual POST Form is used. For more information, see Validate Manual POST Form Procedure.

Hardware is not operational before end of day
  • Follow Manual Close Procedures.
  • After the Computer System is operable, total each bank deposit and enter the total amount as one Interim Deposit for the current day’s close.

 

Note: For more information, see Manual Close.

10

After processing the transaction, enter the customer account number, name, card expiration date, and the authorization number.
