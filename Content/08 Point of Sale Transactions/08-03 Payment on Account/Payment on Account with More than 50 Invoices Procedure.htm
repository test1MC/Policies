Payment on Account with More than 50 Invoices Procedure

The benefits of properly applied payments include:

  • Improved Cash Application (keying of payments to invoices)
  • Reduction of Past Due Follow-Up
  • Improved Collection Activity
Step Action

1

Verify that paid invoices match the check total. If not, reconcile with the customer prior to processing the payment. The payment cannot be held up for same-day processing. Verify from the Open Items List or by selecting Accounts Receivable HEADQUARTERS from the Manger Functions Menu, then select PF3 Open Item Keyout.

Note: Process any known tax/pricing corrections prior to processing the payment on account.

2

From POS desktop, select Store -> Point of Sale -> Select Payment on Account from the Sale/Payment Menu. Enter the customer’s account and job information, verifying their accuracy.

3

Enter the job payment amount and the transaction numbers to be paid. Include any tax/pricing correction transaction numbers.

Note: Accuracy is extremely important for proper payment application. Enter accurate information for every payment on account.  

4

There are two options for payments of 50 invoices or more:

  • Enter the 50+ invoice numbers while processing the payment on account.
  • Fax the Customer Payment Information to the National Credit Center Contact Information Help

5

Press ACCEPT [F1].

6

Press COMMENT [F9] and type, “More than 50 invoices; will fax list with copy of payment.”

7

You may verify that the faxed payment information was properly applied after 5 days by first selecting [10. Accounts Receivable HEADQUARTERS] from the Manager Functions menu, then selecting [PF3 OPEN ITEM KEYOUT].

8

The National Credit Center may notify you that they will ALWAYS need a faxed copy of the Customer Payment Information on select customers. Please enter the Customer Payment Information while processing the payment transaction in addition to faxing the remittance on these select customers.

9

Press ACCEPT [F1] or CANCEL [F5] if you are not able to enter specific transaction information, press the COMMENTS key and type "no remittance information provided," or, "the customer specifies that he/she is paying no specific invoices."

10
If Then

The customer is paying all transactions except for a select few

Press the COMMENT [F9] key and type "PAYS ALL BUT (enter the transactions NOT paid)".

The customer is paying an amount other than the total amount of the invoice(s)
  • Press the COMMENT [F9] key and enter "Paying (amount being paid) on (invoice number)."
  • When you correctly process your payment on account transactions, you may verify the cash application after 48 hours, provided the transaction(s) listed/paid by the payment key out exactly to the penny, (in other words the customer paid listed transaction(s) in full).
  • To view the key out, first select [10. Accounts Receivable HEADQUARTERS] from the Manager Functions menu, then select [PF3 OPEN ITEM KEYOUT].

11

After processing the payment, attach any Customer Payment Information and check stub to a copy of the Payment On Account transaction and retain in a folder titled CUSTOMER PAYMENT INFORMATION. The customer payment information could include the check stub or any other information provided by the customer. At the end of the month, retain the payment information with your monthly batching.

Note: It is not necessary to send a copy of the Close Transaction since you are not sending in the customer remittance information.

12 If the payment remains unapplied (unkeyed) for some period of time, it could mean that the Credit Center in Cleveland is researching the payment for any of the following reasons, which require their involvement to perform manual cash application. They may need to contact you for additional information on these payments, potentially delaying the cash application process.
Problem Recommendation to Store

Payment may be misapplied to an incorrect account and/or job.
  • Check Open Item Listing for appropriate account/job for open invoices.
  • Search POS for transaction number(s) on remit to determine account/job to process payment.
  • Use Accounts Receivable Management Inquiry to perform name search or job search.

Transactions appear on the remittance, which are not open on that account and/or job.
  • Check Open Item Listing for appropriate account/job for open invoices.
  • Search POS for transaction number(s) on remit to determine account/job to process payment.
  • Use Accounts Receivable Management Inquiry to perform name search or job search.

Payment amount does not equal the invoices listed on the remit, thereby requiring a customer chargeback to be issued or an ARJV to offset the difference.

Mail customer's remittance stub with a copy of the payment transaction to the Credit Center for their use in identifying discrepancies.

Note: Questions or concerns regarding the payment process, please contact your District Credit Office OR you may contact the National Credit Center in Cleveland.
