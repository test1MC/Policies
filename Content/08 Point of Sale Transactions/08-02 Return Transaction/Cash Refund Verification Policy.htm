Cash Refund Verification Policy

Cash Refund Verification letters are used to verify customer satisfaction needs for employee training, and to minimize losses related to cash refunds.

  1. Customer information (name, address, city, state, zip code, phone, customer signature) must be complete and accurate (no abbreviations) anytime the customer receives (cash or credit card) credit or a store draft on a Cash Refund or Cash Correction Transaction.
  2. Internal Audit will review the accuracy and completeness of customer information for all Cash Refunds and Cash Corrections during store audits.
  3. Refund Verifications are sent by the Division Office. Any Cash Refund Verification letter returned to the store must be forwarded by the Store Manager to the Division.
  4. A minimum of ten transactions (Cash Refunds or Cash Corrections) are selected each month by the Division. Division Loss Prevention will review all completed Cash Refund Verification letters.
  5. Cash Refund Verification letters are sent twice a month, each covering a two-week period. Up to three refunds will be listed on each Refund Verification Letter. For example: If a customer makes three returns in a two week period, they will receive one Cash Refund Verification letter with all three transactions listed.
  6. All undeliverable Refund Verifications returned by the Post Office will be resolved by Division Loss Prevention.
