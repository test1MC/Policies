Sales Policy

  1. Employees are not permitted to process their own point of sale transactions. This includes ALL point of sale transactions. Another employee must process the point of sale transaction.
  2. Cash Transactions must be processed through SOURCE before the material leaves the store. For more information see Store Deliveries Overview.
  3. No Sherwin-Williams employee is permitted to sell any paint or non-paint products to a customer knowing that they intend to resell those products out of a store, over the Internet, through a telemarketing call center, or any other place of business. For more information see Resell Paint & Non-Paint Policy.
  4. Lead Paint: Customers renovating, remodeling, or repainting a home, building or other structure built prior to 1978, or any customer raising concerns about lead exposure, should be provided with information on lead dust exposure. Refer to Safety & Environmental Policy for additional information on what information may be provided to a customer.
  5. No merchandise may leave the store with a customer unless a sales transaction has been processed for that product and this sale has been noted on a SOURCE generated sales receipt or Manual POST Form. Any merchandise leaving the store with a Sherwin-Williams employee or third-party carrier must be noted on either a Customer Order Delivery/Installation Form or an ISR Purchase Order.
  6. Process a deposit if a customer chooses to pay for purchases in advance. When the customer makes a purchase, it is the Store Manager's responsibility to keep a manual ledger and monitor the cash sales to make sure they do not exceed the deposit amount made by the customer.
  7. Use Customer Order Entry to prepare an order for the customer. To learn more about applying several customer orders to one point-of-sale transaction, see Several Orders to One POS Transaction.
  8. Manual POST Forms should only be used for the following reasons:
    1. If the computer system is not working properly: Manual POST forms must be processed as soon as the system is working.
    2. As a busy time form: the POST Forms must be processed prior to Close.
    3. To release material once approved by the DCO and prior to having the new Job Number in your system: the POST Forms must be processed as soon as the new Job Number is available.
  9. Occasionally you may be required to follow the After Hours sale procedures. For more information see After Hours Deposit & Sale Policy.
  10. Manual POST Forms should not be used for Locked Out Accounts.
    1. Process an order in Customer Order Entry for Locked Out Accounts once you receive approval from the DCO. Answer YES to the “DELIVER?”, so that the document has a customer signature line.
    2. Enter the DCO approval number in Special Instructions.
    3. Process the point of sale transaction as soon as the account is unlocked by the DCO.
  11. The Customer must have a valid account in good standing for all Charge Sales.
  12. You must call for approval for visiting customer purchases over $250.00. For more information see Visiting Customer Overview
  13. If the customer requires the use of a Purchase Order, it must be secured while processing the customer order or point of sale transaction.
  14. If the sale is going to be NO TAX, a completed Tax Exemption Certificate MUST be on file at the store. Customer must sign each NO TAX transaction.
  15. Pricing, Credit Approval and a Tax Exemption number MUST be obtained from the Home Store for all Visiting Store NO TAX Purchases. Enter the Tax Exempt Number along with the Home Store information in the Comments Section of the header. The Tax Exemption Certificate must be on file at the purchasing store. For more information see Transaction Taxability Overview.
  16. Stated Gross Profit must be an accurate calculation of the profit for the items being sold.
  17. When processing a sale, only the assigned employee numbers can be used.
  18. Sales Representatives are not permitted to process any point of sale transaction. Store employees must process all transactions.
  19. Sherwin-Williams employees are NOT permitted to pre-bill merchandise. Prebilling is billing the customer for merchandise before confirmation of shipment to the customer. Prebilling is not only a policy violation, but it is also an illegal practice that was enacted to ensure that businesses are accurately reporting sales and revenues so their company investors are not being misled.
  20. Sherwin-Williams employees should only enter appropriate comments on point-of-sale transactions and customer orders because all comments print on customer invoices and statements. Any racial, ethnic, or derogatory remark entered on a point-of-sale transaction or customer order is considered Gross Misconduct Overview and usually results in termination.
  21. Customer orders may be placed in the POS during a promotional pricing period to hold the price when stock is not available in the store to fulfill the customer's request. The order must be processed within four (4) weeks of the promotion end date or the order lines must be canceled and re-entered at regular pricing.
  22. Sales can only be run under an account for individuals authorized to use the account. 
  23. Sales should not be run against other accounts for the purposes of granting discounted pricing. Store Employees should not open retail/DIY accounts classifying them as commercial for the purposes of granting discounted pricing.
