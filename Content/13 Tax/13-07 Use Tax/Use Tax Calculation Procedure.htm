Use Tax Calculation Procedure

  • Use tax must be calculated per the guidelines of the state in which the material is being installed. Use tax applies to the cost of materials used in the installation and is included as part of the selling price of subject material to the customer. Use tax is typically calculated at the same rate as Sales Tax.
  • Gross profit should always be calculated BEFORE figuring use tax. Gross profit is to be figured excluding use tax because it is not considered part of the cost of the merchandise when paying the vendor invoice. Use tax is an expense item appearing under prime-sub 7613-001, line 53 of the P&L (non-controllable item).
  • If presently receiving material from a S-W Workroom, use tax is to be figured on the workroom’s cost of material - not store landed cost. S-W Workrooms generally have an “up charge” in cost for handling.
  • Figuring use tax is a two-step calculation:
    • Step Action
    1 Calculate the amount of use tax based on the cost of the material
    2 Add that use tax amount to the selling price of the material.

Example:

Mr. Contractor purchases carpet and window coverings in New Hampshire (a non-use tax state) to be installed in a home in Vermont (a use tax-state).  After consulting the USE TAX SUMMARY BY STATE to see what products were considered taxable for use tax in Vermont, the Sherwin-Williams employee determines that use tax must be collected for the carpet being installed in Vermont.  The window coverings are not subject to use tax, but will still incur sales tax.  Let’s the review transaction breakdown before and after use tax was implemented in Vermont:
