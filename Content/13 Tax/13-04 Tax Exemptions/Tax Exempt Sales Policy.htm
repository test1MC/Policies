Tax Exempt Sales Policy

  1. Customers are required to have a valid cash or charge account for any tax exemption transaction.
  2. Since Tax Record Cards are available to all stores, there is no requirement to call the home store and request a copy of an exemption certificate to support a tax exempt sale.
  3. If the customer does not have documentation to support a tax exempt sale, the store employee must collect tax for the order/sale. It may be refunded later via a correction transaction in accordance with the state and local regulations once the customer provides documentation.
  4. All exempt sales must reference a Tax Record Card.
  5. Any store can create a Tax Record Card for any customer with support for tax exemptions; however, always retain any customer documentation received at the store.  
  6. If the transaction is job or project specific, the Tax Record Card and receipt MUST reference the same job name.  Exempt transactions made against the 01 House Job must always be for the job named on the Tax Record Card. There are no exceptions or overrides.
  7. There are some gray areas with the Taxability Rules Matrix (please review Product Taxability in Determine Product Taxability Procedure where products purchased by an entity may be taxable or exempt based on the buyer’s end use of the product.) 
    1. For example, sandpaper purchased by a manufacturer may be exempt if used in creating their end product for their customer but taxable if used in maintenance of their equipment. 
  8. A Tax Record Card must be established for a customer and applied to an order or sale in order to be able to perform a Line Item Override.Line item overrides will be monitored by the Corporate Tax Department and Internal Audit.
