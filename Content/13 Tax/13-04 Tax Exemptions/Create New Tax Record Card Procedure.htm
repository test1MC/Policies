Create New Tax Record Card Procedure

Step

Action

1
If Then
From the POS Desktop Main Menu -> Tax Record Card System -> enter employee username and password -> press Enter to add a new record.
From the SOURCE Homepage Source Operations -> Processes -> Tax Record Card System -> enter credentials -> Search Tax Records Card -> Add New.

Note: Always have the customer’s Exemption Certificate or supporting documentation available when creating the Tax Record Card as you will create it based on the information the customer has provided.

2

Enter the Customer Account or Customer Name and click Search.

3

Select the desired Customer Account to create a Tax Record Card.

4

Does any Customer Info populate using information from the A/R System?

If Then
Yes Compare this information to that provided by the customer. Make any changes required to the address and phone.
No Enter Customer Info using the information the customer has provided on their Exemption Certificate.

5

Certificate Info must be entered for every new Tax Record Card.

  • Select the most appropriate entry for Document Type from the drop-down listing. 

    Note: The Document Type indicates the type of support the customer has provided for their exemption.

  • Select the correct Vendor entry from the drop-down listing.  Then, click the box labeled Vendor Verified to confirm your selection. 

    • Note: The Vendor Field identifies the Sherwin-Williams’ business entity to which the customer presented their certificate.  A certificate presented to Sherwin-Williams may not be used for Sherwin-Williams Automotive Finishes. 

Automotive Stores are designated as 02-Sherwin-Williams Automotive Finishes while all other locations are identified as 01-Sherwin-Williams Company.

Enter Customer Business based on the information the customer provided to describe the type of business activity in which they are involved that grants them the exemption from tax. 

Note: This entry should be along the lines of Contractor, Retailer, Government, Manufacturer, Wholesaler, Automobile Repair, etc.  If the customer documentation has this information, enter exactly what the customer has provided.
6

From the drop-down listing, select a Reason. Make your selection for Reason based on the customer information.

Note: Understand who your customer is and why they are claiming the exemption.  For example, a public school may be coded as Government – State or Local while a private school may be coded as Nonprofit Organization.  The correct selection of the Reason will drive product taxability on your customer’s invoices.

7

Select the job(s) for which the Tax Record Card is being created.  Jobs in bold are available for selection.

8

Click the State Box for the state(s), province(s), or territory(ies) listed on the exemption certificate to complete the Location Info.  If the customer has a Multi-Jurisdiction Form, select all states for which the form is acceptable.  Click Next.

9

Additional Location Info will be required for each state, province, or territory selected.

State ID Numbers are generally state-issued and may be any of the following:  FEIN, Tax Number, SSN, Registration Number, etc. 

Was 01 Contractor was selected as the Reason?

If Then
Yes

A specific Exempt Project Description is required for the Tax Record Card to be valid.  For example, Church is not acceptable while First Southern Baptist Church of Avon is acceptable. 

Note: Locality / Local ID Number is only required for select states where exemptions may be granted at the local level- this could be city, county, parish, etc.
No Enter N/A in this field

10

Ensure the Signed Date is the date on which the customer signed the document.

11

Click the Document Signed by Customer Box to indicate the signature has been verified by a store employee.

12

Enter Title as the title of the signer - NOT their name.

13

Entry of the TRC Exp Date is required. This is generally the same as the Signed Date unless the certificate indicates a specific effective date.

Note: If the certificate or state has an Expiration Date for certificates, enter that date in this field.  Otherwise, enter a date corresponding to when the customer job is expected to end or a date ten years out.

14

Click Submit.

15

Click the Printer icon to print the generated Fax Coversheet.  Exit the Fax Coversheet using the Back Button.  Do NOT use File -> Close and do NOT click the red X button in the right-hand corner. These options will close the Tax Record Card System and it will need to be reopened if the task is not yet completed.

Note: It is required to fax the customer’s Exemption Certificate and the Fax Coversheet to the Tax Record Card Administration Group for review and validation.  If they do not receive the faxed copy or the Tax Record Card entry does not match the information on the customer’s document, the Tax Record Card could be invalidated for use and it will be required to charge the customer tax for any future transactions.

16

The Tax Record Card will be in Reviewed (Not Valid) Status and may be used immediately for processing transactions for the customer.
