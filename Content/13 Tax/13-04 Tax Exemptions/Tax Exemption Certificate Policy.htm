Tax Exemption Certificate Policy

  1. Tax exemption documentation must be on file with Sherwin-Williams in order to grant an exemption.
  2. All certificates must be complete and correct. Incomplete and/or incorrect certificates cannot be accepted for exemption purposes and tax must be charged. Correct and complete certificates will have the following information:
    1. The name on the customer’s certificate must match the name on customer invoices.
    2. The vendor or seller on the certificate must be Sherwin-Williams or Sherwin-Williams Automotive Finishes. Automotive customers may submit certificates notes as Sherwin-Williams. This is acceptable as long it is signed after 1/1/1996.
    3. A contractor who does not hold a resale permit MUST supply a specific job name.  For example, “Church” is not acceptable while “First Baptist Church of Avon” is acceptable.
  3. Usually, a copy of a permit may not be accepted as a substitute for a signed certificate.  Please see Locate State Tax Bulletin Information Procedure for more information.
  4. The customer certificate must be for the state in which they take delivery.
  5. If no documentation is provided, Sherwin-Williams is legally obligated to collect sales tax from the customer until a certificate is provided. 
  6. Store personnel are not permitted to complete exemption certificates or assist in the completion of exemption certificates by customers.  The only part of an exemption certificate that a Sherwin-Williams employee may legally complete is Sherwin-Williams' name and address.
  7. Certificates are legal documents and may never be changed, altered, or updated by store personnel. Altering a customer’s exemption certificate can have serious consequences, including the possibility of dismissal.
  8. If a change is required or a certificate has expired, obtain a new certificate from the customer.  Use the Tax Exemption Request Letter to communicate with the customer when they need updated exemption certificates for the file.
    1. If a customer changes their name, treat them as a new customer. First, complete a new credit application for the customer using their new name. Then, be sure to obtain a new exemption certificate in the customer's new name.

Find the Tax Exemption Certificate in the Forms Repository. Search form name or keyword “tax” in the Form Name field. All available forms containing the search word will appear below in the form description field.
