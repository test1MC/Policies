Interstate Sales Overview

Since The Sherwin-Williams Company, its divisions, and affiliates are registered for sales and use tax and operate in all states that presently impose such a tax, S-W cannot exempt out-of-state sales that are taxable. In addition, many states have entered into reciprocal agreements, which make the collection of another state's tax mandatory.

Examples:
Scenario Solution
A customer residing outside of the state purchases merchandise from a Sherwin-Williams store in the state requesting the store to charge it to his account. The customer takes the merchandise with him. The sale is taxable as the transaction is completed in your state. There is no interstate shipment of goods.
A customer in state orders a drum of materials from a Sherwin-Williams store in the state to be delivered in the state. The store places the order on an out-of-state factory, instructing that factory to ship direct to the customer in the state.

The sale is taxable because the order was taken and material received within the state.
An in-state customer purchases merchandise from an in-state Sherwin-Williams store requesting the store to ship the merchandise by common carrier to a point outside the state. This transaction is in interstate commerce and not subject to store sales tax. The most acceptable proof of an interstate shipment is a Bill-of-Lading which shows the destination to be out of state. The sales slip must indicate the merchandise is shipped to a location outside the state.
