Transaction Taxability Overview

SOURCE allows for three basic taxing situations to be processed for transactions: taxable sale (standard rate), tax exception sale (non-standard rate, amount or exemption.)  It is critical to note that a tax exception may not apply to the entire transaction. Some line items may still require the collection of standard tax based on the taxability of the product for the transaction.

Geographic Location

Each state/province (and some local entities) sets its own rules regarding tax rates, tax holidays, use tax, tax exemptions, and product taxability for its jurisdiction. The taxing jurisdiction for a transaction is determined by where the transfer of product occurs.  Orders, which are delivered to a customer or have an installation address, will be taxed according to the delivery/installation address. Orders, which are sold to the customer within the store, will be governed by the tax rules for the jurisdiction in which the store is located. 

Note: Be aware of agreements certain states have made with others regarding the collection or exemption of tax for certain qualifying residents.

Product Taxability

Products may or may not be taxable based not only on where the product is purchased, but also based upon who purchases the product and for what reason or use.

Tax Exceptions

In certain situations, customers may qualify for a tax exemption or to be taxed at a non-standard rate or amount for the prevailing jurisdiction.  Customers requesting either an exemption or exception are required to provide valid documentation to support the transaction.
