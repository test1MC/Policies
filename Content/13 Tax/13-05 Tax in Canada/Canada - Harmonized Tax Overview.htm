Canada - Harmonized Tax Overview

This section is for all locations in Ontario and British Columbia

Effective 07/01/10, Ontario and British Columbia harmonized their sales tax so that GST and PST tax is replaced by the HST (harmonized) tax.

  • Ontario and British Columbia sales invoices on the POS list the total tax as one percentage.  Ontario's combined tax rate is 13% (5% federal; 8% provincial).  British Columbia's combined tax is 12% (5% federal; 7% provincial).  Also at this time Nova Scotia is increasing their rate to 15% HST.
  • General Ledger account numbers for tax paid will change.  Please start using the new accounts as of July 01, 2010.  The old accounts will no longer be available. The new accounts will be mapped to the GST, HST, QST Tax Fields in SWeep as of July 1, 2010.

  • PST penalized businesses by taxing at each step in production, distribution and retail. The new Ontario and British Columbia HST is a value-added tax, which means Sherwin-Williams, as a company, is refunded taxes paid on most expenses.  It is important to code the tax in the proper fields in SWEEP so that SW may recognize these refunds or tax input credits. Click here to review the SWeep Vendor Messages and Examples.
  • Along with the benefits of HST, there are some restrictions. Stores were recently notified that utility invoices have been returned to the field to pay through SWEEP.  The provincial portion of Ontario and British Columbia HST on utility invoices is a restricted tax that must be reported separately and therefore coded to a different GL account.
  • When entering an invoice for a utility or telecommunications vendor in SWEEP, you will see "pop up" instructions on how to enter the tax.  Only follow these instructions for these specific utility or telephone vendors.
  • When this message for utility and telephone vendors appears, enter 5% Federal tax in the HST tax box.  Then let the remaining tax along with invoice amount default to the Goods & Services field.  Then on the distribution screen on page 2, enter the provincial portion of HST (8% tax for Ontario; 7% tax for British Columbia) to the account provided in the message.  Accounts are also listed below:
