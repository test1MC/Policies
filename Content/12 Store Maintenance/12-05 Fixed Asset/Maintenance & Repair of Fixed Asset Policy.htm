Maintenance & Repair of Fixed Asset Policy

  1. Stores Managers have an obligation to keep all Fixed Assets and store property in good repair.
  2. Be sure to understand any store lease requirements (such as HVAC Preventative Maintenance Requirements).  If lease requirements are not properly performed, we could be held responsible for equipment failure that would otherwise be covered by the landlord.
  3. Should any part of the store or its fixtures be in need of repair, the Store Manager must identify whether the items needing repair are the responsibility of The Sherwin-Williams Company or that of the landlord. For more information, see Confirmation of Responsible Party Policy.
    1. The division Real Estate Department can help you determine who is responsible in a given situation.
    2. If the needed repair is the responsibility of the landlord, this must be communicated to the landlord as soon as possible.
    3. Any delay on the part of the landlord to respond to a needed repair should be reported to your division Real Estate Department for further action.
    4. If an asset is damaged in an accident, the store manager must contact Division Loss Prevention to ensure that any and all paperwork is filled out.
    5. If the repair is the responsibility of Sherwin-Williams the store manager must ensure that the company called in to quote the work is properly licensed and insured.  Any quote should breakout the following details:
    1. Is there any charge to diagnose the problem (where applicable)?
    2. What will be repaired and/or replaced?
    3. What is the store’s cost for all parts to be replaced?
    4. Part number and manufacturer should be specified.
    5. Serial number (where applicable) should be on the final bill.
    6. What is the warranty on the replaced parts?
    7. What is the hourly rate for the technician(s) that will complete the repair?
    8. How many hours will the repair take?
    9. When can the job be completed?
    10. What is the total cost of the project?
    1. Find the Proposal Review Form in the Forms Repository. Search form name or keyword “proposal” in the Form Name field. All available forms containing your search word will appear below in the form description field.
  4. Service and Repair under $500 - Store Managers are authorized to arrange for maintenance and repairs up to $500.00.  Be sure to document the details outlined above.
  5. Service and Repair over $500 (but less than $1000) - The District Manager must approve all service or repairs exceeding $500.  E-mails from DM’s PDA or District office will suffice for approval documentation. 3 bids must be reviewed when a service or repairs will cost more than $500. It is important to maintain good communication with both the district office and the division Real Estate Department to ensure that all repair approved by a Sherwin-Williams representative are the responsibility of our company (and not that of the landlord). For more information see Competitive Repair Bid Over $500 Policy.
  6. Service and Repair over $1000 - When the Service or Repair will cost more than $1000, the materials being replaced could be capitalized as a fixed asset and would require an FCA.  It is critical to obtain both the district office and division Real Estate Department approvals prior to authorizing the work. All Services or Repairs over $1000 will require a review of at least three (3) bids.
  7. The Store Manager is responsible for coordinating the repair of all equipment and exterior signs. However, when experiencing problem with the signage, it is critical that the division Installation Manager or division Engineer be contacted prior to engaging a service provider to diagnose the problem.
  8. The Store Manager is responsible for coordinating the replacement of Ballasts and Light Bulbs.  Details on these procedures and approved suppliers for lamps and ballasts can be found in the LIGHTING POLICY section of the policy manual.
