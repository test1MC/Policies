Fixed Asset Policy

  1. Any tangible asset, not intended for resale, which has a useful life of less than one year or costs less than $1,000 regardless of the useful life, will be expensed upon acquisition. However, if two or more items costing less than $1,000 each are part of an initial or major installation exceeding $1,000, then these items must be grouped and capitalized together.
  2. Two or more capital items purchased should not be grouped together to exceed $999 unless such items are part of an initial or major project or part of a single purchase for a number of locations (i.e. a $400 item for every paint store).
  3. The cost of capital asset and FCA total includes:
    1. Net invoice costs (including any taxes), contract price or deed value
    2. Installation costs (parts, labor, ands burden*), recording fees, survey costs, title fees, permits, etc.
    3. Transportation costs
    4. Certain internal and external engineering/drafting services
  4. Improvements, which are expenditures which materially enhance the value, change the use, or increase the life expectancy, strength, or capacity of a capital asset as compared to the status of that asset prior to the expenditure,  should be capitalized and require an approved FCA.
  5. Repairs, which are expenditures that merely maintain a capital asset in an ordinary efficient operating condition, without materially enhancing, changing, or increasing an aspect of that asset from its status prior to the expenditure, should be expensed.
  6. When expenditures that would normally be classified as repairs are made part of a general plan of reconditioning, improving, or altering the property as a whole, then these expenditures are considered to be improvements, included in an approved FCA and should be capitalized. Refer to the CORPORATE Controller for guidance in determining the proper classification of such expenditures.
  7. You are required to process an IST to transfer expense merchandise to another store. Process an IBAR to transfer expense merchandise to another cost center other (Rep. or other inside or outside of Stores Group). Installation expense should not be transferred by an IST or IBAR. Contact Fixed Asset Accounting at 216-566-2254 or 216-566-2205 to transfer any installation expense that should be capitalized. For more information see MMS IBAR Overview.
  8. You are required to use FactFinder to complete a Transfer Retirement Fixed Asset Report anytime you choose to retire or transfer an asset. District Manager Approval and signature are required on the completed form.
  9. As long as the asset is still in the store you do not retire freight and tax even if there is a book value of zero. Only retire freight and tax when you are retiring the asset.
