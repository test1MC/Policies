Dispose of Records (Store Closing) Overview

Be prepared to assist the District Manager in the collection, disposal, and retention of records.  If any store records have been stored off-site, be sure that your District Manager is aware of this and that the records are brought back into the store well before the closing date for review.  Always keep in mind that any records or store document, which is disposed of, must be cross-shredded.  Store paperwork requiring shredding would include unused B-Prefix Purchase Order Forms and Manual POST Forms.  Store Drafts should not be shredded—these must be given to the District Manager for disposal.

Some records require retention past the closing of a store and may be transferred to the customer’s new store location.  The District Manager may ask store employees to help prepare for the transport of critical customer records by sorting these out and identifying what store they must be shipped to:

  • Sales and Use Tax Exemption Forms
  • Authorization and Release Forms
  • Authorization to Retain and Use Forms
  • Customer Color Cards
