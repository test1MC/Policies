Fixed Asset & Expense Item (Store Closing) Procedure

If the District Manager has decided to sell fixed assets, this process will begin approximately one month before the final day of business.  Similarly, the District Manager will begin disposition of expense items approximately one month before the closing. Fixed Assets may be sold at any time one month before closing, but employees are not to release assets that are required to display or store product until the stock remaining in the store has depleted so that the asset is no longer needed. Assets required for store operation may not be released until after the final day of business.

Step

Action

Cash/Check/Credit Card Payment

1

Process a Miscellaneous Receipt Transaction. Code the receipt to ‘Fixed Asset Retirement Clear’ (0869-003) and ‘Taxes – Sales Use’ (7613-001).

2

A receipt is to be issued, but the item will be released to the buyer at the company convenience.  Fixtures will NOT be unloaded at the time of the sale.

SW Charge Payment

1

Sales of fixed assets may also be charged to a S-W account. The store is to contact the District Credit Manager to obtain approval for the charge. The District Credit Manager is responsible for approving the charge on either existing S-W accounts or by opening a new account for the sale.

2

When the sale is approved, the store is to complete a Manual POST Form, noting "for the sale of a fixed asset". List on the invoice the unit number and the account number from the Property Listing, item description, price and manual extend, add tax and then total.

3

Have the customer sign the invoice.

4

Memo all but the customer's copy of the Manual POST form.

5

Call the National Credit Center requesting an ARJV to charge the customer's account.

6

Instruct them to code the ARJV to ‘A/R Suspense – Transfers 0300-003’ with a notation to recode it to ‘Const in Progress’ (0815-001) and the tax to ‘Taxes – Sales Use’ (7613-001).

7

Note the ARJV number on the POST form.

8

Send a copy of the POST form to:

Expense Items
1

Disposal of expense items are handled in the same manner as fixed assets with the following exceptions:

  • Price all items at current store cost.
  • Code to expense code where originally charged.
  • Process a Store Expense Transaction for all items destroyed.
  • Process all items sold on a Miscellaneous Receipt Transaction.

Note: After all products have been cleared from the store, contact anyone who purchased a fixed asset and has not yet claimed it. Notify them that the must make arrangements to pick up their purchase at the time. The transfer of any fixed asset to another locations to be recorded on the Asset Retirement / Intra-Company Transfer of Fixed Assets or Expense Items Form. Find the Asset Retirement/Intra-Company Transfer Form in the Forms Repository. Search using the keyword “asset" in the Form Name field. The disposal of unusable or unsalable fixed assets is also to be recorded on Asset Retirement / Intra-Company Transfer of Fixed Assets or Expense Items Form. Alongside the description of the item, insert the word "destroyed".  Separate forms should be used to record transfer and destruction.
