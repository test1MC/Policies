Conduct Store Inventory (Store Closing) Procedure

Upon announcement of a store closing or blending, store employees must take a complete inventory of the store, following November inventory procedures with regards to counting.  The closing location will NOT receive an inventory packet and is not required to submit any reports or information to Cleveland. The District Manager is to oversee the inventory and to determine the disposition of remaining product.  The purpose of the inventory is to identify exactly what is on hand in the store.  This task will assist in determining the disposition of on-hand inventory and the last STAR Order date for the closing location.  While taking the inventory, consider these options and scenarios when reviewing the following types of products:

If

Then

Floorcovering

Reverse rolls all merchandise. Priced to sell, there have been no problems moving floorcovering out of our closed stores.  Large pieces should be transferred using the IST Transaction to large floorcovering stores.

Spray Equipment

Spray Equipment should be sold at regular or promotional prices. Attempts should be made to return units and parts to our vendors rather than double up another store's inventory.

Wallcovering

Past experience indicates that the most successful method of closing out Wallcovering has been to prepare room lots, based on the available quantities, priced individually and discounted.   Attempts should be made to return the wallcovering to our vendors rather than double up another store's inventory.

Core GOM

Product offerings should be reviewed for IST transfer to nearby locations.  This is the most cost-effective method of unsold GOM product disposition.  Other options available are returning product to the DSC via a Store Closing Return.  Remember that GOM product is never to be sold to dealers for resale.  As well, GOM product cannot be set aside to be purchased at a later date nor can S-W unload a shelving unit immediately. The store, as directed by the District Manager, and in cooperation with Store Operations, should begin to eliminate min/max assignments for slower moving products a few months leading up to the closing date.  The store should gradually order less product leading up to the last few weeks as to dwindle down inventory levels, ordering only highly important product the last few weeks.

Hazardous

Identify and segregate products, which can be considered hazardous.  This assessment should include tint (in machine), rags, and cleaning materials requiring proper disposal methods.
