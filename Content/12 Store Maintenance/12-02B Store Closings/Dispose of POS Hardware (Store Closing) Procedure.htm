Dispose of POS Hardware (Store Closing) Procedure

Step Action
1

The District Manager will ask you to inventory and record the serial number for all POS hardware on the Closing Store Documentation Form.  This information will be critical in ensuring that all hardware is returned to Cleveland. 

Note: POS hardware to be inventoried includes:  Manager Workstation CPU and Monitor, Wrap-Counter Workstation CPUs and Monitors, PDT, all keyboards, all mice, all credit card readers, all scanners, all cash drawers, Epson receipt/validation printer, and the Lexmark printer. 

2

Inform the District Manager if the store still has packing materials and boxes for returning the POS Hardware to Cleveland.  If not, the District Manager will arrange for packing materials to be sent to the store.
3

The District Manager will arrange for pickup or transfer of POS Hardware.  Be sure that all transactions have been completed the final polling has been run in the system before the field engineer begins to disconnect hardware. 

Note: This hardware includes all ISTs, chargebacks, and your Store Closing Return.  Do not attempt to disconnect and package equipment.  A store employee or member of the district closing team should be present to ensure the equipment is well packed and minimize damage during shipment.
4

Ensure all auxiliary items such as cabling assembly, all cables, operating manuals, etc. are packed for shipment back to Depot Maintenance. 

Note: Equipment supplies such as printer paper, receipt paper, tinting labels, printer ribbons, toner cartridges, etc. should not be returned to Depot Maintenance and may be distributed to other stores in the District.
5
  • Verify that all packages are clearly labeled.  Write the serial number of the contents on the box.  If any hardware is to be transferred to another location in the district, follow all directions supplied by Depot Maintenance.
