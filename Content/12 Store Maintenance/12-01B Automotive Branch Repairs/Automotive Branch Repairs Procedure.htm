Automotive Branch Repairs Procedure

This section is only for Automotive Branches.

Step Action

Repairs at Branch

1

Call the Landlord / Property Manager explaining the nature of the item in need of repair.

2

Follow up with a letter to the Landlord / Property Manager confirming the conversation. Provide a timeline for when the repairs should be completed.  Please complete the Automotive Branch Repair Request Letter #1.  Find the Repair Request Letter in the Forms Repository. Search using the keyword landlord in the Form Name field. All available forms containing the search word will appear below in the form description field.

Note: Automotive Real Estate must be copied on the letter.

It is important to include pictures of the repair item with the letter.

3

If a reasonable time passes for non-emergency repairs (14 days) and the item has not been repaired/addressed forward a second letter to the Landlord/Property Manager.  Please complete the Automotive Branch Repair Request Follow-Up Letter.  Find the Repair Request Follow-Up Letter in the Forms Repository. Search using the keyword landlord in the Form Name field. All available forms containing the search word will appear below in the form description field.

Note: Automotive Real Estate must be copied on the letter.

4

If after the second letter the item is not repaired by the Landlord, it is your responsibility to obtain a written quote from a contractor to repair the item.

Note: DO NOT PROCEED WITH THE REPAIR AFTER OBTAINING THE QUOTE.

5

Contact Automotive Real Estate and forward all letters, photos, estimates, documented times and dates of all calls to the Landlord/Property Manager at the address below. 

Sherwin-Williams Automotive Finishes
4440 Warrensville Center Road # H 225
Warrensville Heights, Ohio 44128-2837
Phone: (216) 332-8352
Fax: (216) 332-8600
E-Mail: todd.d.felder@sherwin.com

The branch-specific lease will be reviewed to determine if Sherwin-Williams has language to proceed with the repairs and deduct the specific amount from our rental obligation. However, in order to enforce this, written documentation that every effort has been made on your behalf to contact the Landlord and notify them of the specific issue is required. Failure to follow this process may delay completing the specified repair.
