Electrical Equipment Hazards Overview

Electrical hazards are a leading cause of fires and can cause electrocution accidents. For more information, refer to the Work Safe Manual. Here are some "do's and don'ts" for the use of electrical equipment in the store:

Do's Don'ts
Replace any burned out bulbs or ballast as soon as possible to ensure proper lighting throughout the store. Don't use extension cords, except on a temporary basis. If you must use an extension cord, remove it as soon as a properly placed electrical outlet is installed.
Verify that all electrical equipment (such as motorized displays and cabinet lighting) is operating properly. Don't perform electrical repairs or maintenance yourself.
Report breaker switches that have "tripped". Don't plug and unplug machines; use switches to turn power on or off.
Use "hard-wired" receptacles for all plugs. Don't use ordinary electrical equipment (or other potential ignition sources) near flammable vapors. (Check with your Manager regarding approved electrical equipment).
Use only properly grounded plugs. Never use electrical items in the P.S.R. Room. For example: Telephones, Speakers, Electric Mixers or Mixing Equipment.
Use only electrical power tools that are grounded or "UL Listed" as "double insulated."
