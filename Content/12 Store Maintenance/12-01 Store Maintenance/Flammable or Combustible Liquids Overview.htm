Flammable or Combustible Liquids Overview

Flammable products ("Class I Liquids") must be stored in closed containers to prevent hazardous vapors from escaping. Do not leave a container of flammable or combustible liquid open, even if it is being used frequently. These containers must always be kept tightly closed between uses. Sherwin-Williams' flammable products are normally marked with a red, diamond-shaped label. Flammable or combustible liquids include:

  • Solvents and paint reducers
  • Lacquers
  • Floor enamels

These materials must be placed away from any heat or electrical power source (such as a furnace or electrical panel) and must be separate from other products whenever possible. Know the location of flammable or combustible liquids and the nearest fire extinguisher.

Local fire codes dictate how flammable and combustible liquids should be stored:

  • Flammable and combustible liquids must not exceed 4 1/2 feet high when stacked on the floor.
  • Do not stack drums of flammable and combustible liquids.
  • Do not exceed 5,000 gallons in any one pile of flammable and combustible liquids.
  • Separate piles of flammable and combustible liquids by 48-inch aisles.
