Warehouse Overview

Every Sherwin-Williams store has some type of warehouse area to hold an inventory of paint and associated products. Several of these products are hazardous; many are heavy or awkward to handle. All are valuable. For these reasons, Sherwin-Williams must follow strict warehousing procedures.

  • Stores that handle large volume mixing, custom blending, or solvent dispensing have special product service rooms or Liquid Storage Rooms designed to meet strict safety or code regulations.
  • Keeping the warehouse area of the store neat and clean is very important for safety as this area contains hazardous chemicals and heavy bulk materials. Sherwin-Williams’ employees follow strict warehouse procedures that must be followed by every store employee. For more information, refer to WorkSafe chapters 3 and 6.
  • Proper storage of materials prevents fire and accident hazards in the warehouse. The warehouse layout has been designed to organize all materials safely. If you see any problems, report them to your immediate supervisor.

There are two general rules that apply in the warehouse:

Rule Procedure
No customers If you see a customer alone in the warehouse, ask if you can help him and assist him out of the area.
No smoking

If you see someone smoking, politely tell him that smoking is not allowed in this area.

Note: Any employee who smokes or permits a customer to smoke anywhere on store property including but not limited to the sales floor, manager office, warehouse, restrooms, and, anywhere where flammable material is stored is subject to immediate termination.
