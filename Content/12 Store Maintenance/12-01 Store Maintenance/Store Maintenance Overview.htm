Store Maintenance Overview

  • The customer's first impression of the store is based on such things as the manner in which merchandise is presented and arranged, the cleanliness of the store, and the professionalism of the sales personnel. Clutter, dirt, and disorganization present a negative image and discourage sales. Therefore, understand that maintaining the store's appearance is a top priority.
  • The landlord and the Manager usually share building maintenance.
  • The store's lease details the parts of the building that will be maintained by the landlord.
  • The Manager is usually responsible for floorcovering, painting, window repairs, lights, the hot water heater, the heating / air conditioning systems, and the plumbing.
  • Routine maintenance and repair costs paid by the store are handled as normal expense items.
  • The LiveSafe Administrator's Guide and the related START Units are additional sources for store maintenance Policy and Procedure.
