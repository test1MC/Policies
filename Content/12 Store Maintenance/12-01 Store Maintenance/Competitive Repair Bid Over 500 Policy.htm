Competitive Repair Bid Over $500 Policy

  1. Competitive bids are required for any repair over $500 when the repair is not managed by Division Real Estate.
  2. Manager is required to obtain District Manager approval and three viable bids for all repairs over $500.00.
  3. Written [email] District Manager approval is required if you are not able to obtain three competitive bids.
  4. All SWeep transaction or other invoices paid by Sherwin-Williams over $500 that are initiated and processed by the store personnel must contain 3 competitive bids.
  5. Written [email] District Manager approval is required if the initial repair is quoted under $500 (not requiring 3 bids), but at the time of the initial repair additional items needing repair are discovered.
    1. Example: an electrical repair is quoted at $450 but during the repair a breaker is found to be faulty.  The addition $75 charge now make the repair over $500, but it would cost more (additional trip charges) to have others bid the breaker replacement than to just allow the technician on site to complete the repair.
  6. A competitive bid is not required if the Manager contacts Division Real Estate and they are notified of an established arrangement for a specific company to do the kind of repair needed. 
    1. Example: Sign maintenance, if the division does not pursue additional bids and the repair is handled by an FCA.
  7. In the instance of an emergency, the store has the ability to bypass the three quote process; but, must still inform the District Manager and Stores Services at that time. 
  8. An example of an Emergency is an event which may cause harm to employees or products outside of someone's normal control.  A good example would be a store having "no heat" and negative temperatures outside.
  9. For reference, Stores Services supports the stores 24/7/365 with off-hours support and can be reached at 877-470-4615.  Their response time on evenings and weekends is within 1 hour.
  10. For more information, see Maintenance & Repair of Fixed Asset Policy.
