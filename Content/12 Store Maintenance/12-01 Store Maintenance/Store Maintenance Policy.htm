Store Maintenance Policy

  1. The Manager is responsible for keeping the store premises in good condition. Part of this responsibility involves ensuring that the landlord upholds his or her maintenance obligations.
  2. The store and warehouse areas must meet federal and local safety, environmental, sanitation, fire, and building codes as presented in WorkSafe.
  3. The store must be opened for customers before or at the time posted on the door and website.
    1. DM approval in writing is required for any exceptions.
  4. The store cannot be closed to customers nor all the POS terminals closed before the time posted on the door and website.
    1. DM approval in writing is required for any exceptions.
  5. A W-9 form must be secured from all U.S. vendors/independent contractors as well as anyone providing services to The Sherwin-Williams Company, regardless of status (i.e. Corporation, LLC, Partnership, Sole Proprietor, Individuals, etc.). A properly completed W-9 must be obtained even if the form of payment to a contractor is a credit to their account.
    1. Failure on the part of the company to secure a correct Taxpayer Identification Number from a service provider can result in severe penalties to The Sherwin-Williams Company by the IRS.
    2. Capture the Taxpayer ID number each time you pay the service provider. Fax the completed W-9 to Financial Shared Services at 216-566-3634 or mail the completed W-9 to The Sherwin-Williams Company, Financial Shared Services, 1530 Guild Hall, 101 Prospect Avenue, Cleveland, Ohio 44115.
  6. One W-9 form can be obtained and kept on file at the District Office if a company provides service to all stores in the District.
  7. Anyone providing a service to Sherwin-Williams such as window cleaning, floor cleaning and waxing must be paid through SWEEP on the SOURCE Intranet. Please include the taxpayer identification information.
  8. All sidewalks must be kept clean and shoveled during the winter to prevent a customer from accidentally slipping or falling.
  9. Spills should be cleaned up immediately. Refer to WorkSafe Chapter 2 for further instructions.
  10. Unpacked stock or any object that may be hazardous to a customer or employee should not obstruct aisles.
  11. The Manager can negotiate a store's lease only after obtaining special permission from the Headquarters Real Estate Department. However, the Real Estate Department can request a Manager's assistance in negotiating a lease in order to take advantage of the Manager's knowledge of the community.
  12. If a landlord contacts a Manager with important information on the property or the lease, such as a change of ownership or a proposed rent increase, the Manager must forward this information to the Headquarters Real Estate Department.
  13. Owners and tenants are jointly responsible for maintaining building and hot water heater temperatures in compliance with federal law.
  14. If considering a contractual agreement for services with a local vendor (ex. waste hauler or cell phone provider), contact Stores Services at 877-470-4615 for assistance prior to signing agreement for these services. Check with the District Manager for direction on any other agreement being considered.
  15. Review/perform the following maintenance tasks daily:
    1. Sweep and/or wash the store and warehouse floors regularly. If possible, do this work before or after business hours to avoid inconveniencing customers.
    2. Keep all washrooms sanitary.
    3. Have the store windows (inside and outside) washed regularly.
    4. Ensure that the sidewalk and parking lot are always free of litter
    5. Ensure that temperatures in the store will never go above 68 degrees in the winter or below 74 degrees in the summer. Make sure the hot water heater temperature is no higher than 105 degrees. It is recommended that units are set on their middle temperature setting.
    6. Make sure that the building's heating and cooling systems are inspected regularly. The store's lease will identify whether the Manager or the landlord is responsible for this maintenance.
    7. Keep the back room and office organized and neat.
    8. Wipe dust and dirt from shelves and products.
    9. Vacuum carpeted areas regularly. (Bissell Hand Sweeper – Order Express #4000254)
    10. Restock shelves and pegboard first thing in the morning or near closing time each day.
    11. Replace burned-out light fixtures immediately.
    12. Keep the wrap counter clean. Wipe dirt and smudges from the counter regularly.
    13. Keep the Store Computer System and the Manager's Workstation clean and free of clutter.
    14. The dumpster outside the building should be emptied before it becomes overly full. Keep the area around the dumpster free of litter.
    15. Have the building's electrical system inspected if questions or problems arise.
    16. If the store has a sprinkler system, have a qualified contractor inspect it annually.
    17. Make sure that all rubbish is removed daily from the premises.
    18. Use the approved red/black entrance mat (Order Express #7000201) at store entrances.
    19. Always make sure that the store's sidewalks are shoveled during the winter to prevent a customer from accidentally slipping or falling.
    20. When cleaning floors, do not use a detergent that will leave a slippery residue. Always use a non-skid wax.
