Isolate Damaged Material, Material Shortages, or Process No Inventory Procedure

Step

Action

Isolate Damaged Material

1

If some of the material is usable, use the Cut Zone to split the roll into usable and unusable sections. See Cut Zone Overview for more information.

2

Make the cut for the amount of the damaged material. 

3

Mark the Cut Tag as Damaged and place in the WAITING FOR CHARGEBACK folder.

4

Create the Chargeback for the unusable material only.

Isolate Material Shortage

1

If your cutter notices that a roll was shipped short, it is critical that they identify and remove the shortage amount.

2

The cutter should measure the roll balance before making the last cut on the roll. If the roll comes up short, use the Cut Zone to split the roll into actual and non-existent sections. See Cut Zone Overview for more information.

3

Make the cut for the amount that doesn’t exist. 

4

Mark the Cut Tag as Short and place in the WAITING FOR CHARGEBACK folder.

5

Create the Chargeback for the non-existent material only (the quantity on the cut tag).

No Inventory

1

If the material is no longer in your inventory, you must temporarily place the material in your store inventory even though you have no material.

2

Process a Perpetual Inventory Adjustment:

  • From POS desktop, select Store -> Merchandise Management -> Inventory Adjustment -> Perpetual Adjustment -> Enter information.
  • If the carpet color is not in the system, press CANCEL [F5] to manually enter the color information. If you do not know the color number, enter CHARGEBACK. When prompted for the dye lot, enter CHARGEBACK.
  • When prompted for the registration number, enter CHARGEBACK. Enter the width, length and unit cost. Enter detailed information in the memo area.

3

Once you have added inventory, process Chargeback:

  • Bypass the tag identification, color number and dye lot fields.
  • Select the roll you have just adjusted. 
  • After entering the cost press COMMENT [F9] to capture the original purchase order information and the reason for the NO INVENTORY CHARGEBACK.
  • Select NO when prompted to re-print the stock tag. 
  • The released chargeback will offset the inventory amount entered in the Perpetual Inventory Adjustment.

Process Chargeback

1

From POS desktop, select Store -> Select Merchandise Management.

2

Enter your Employee Number and Password.

3

Select Chargebacks from the Merchandise Management Menu.

4

Select Add New Chargeback from the Chargeback Menu.

5

Select the appropriate Chargeback Type.

6

Enter the Vendor Number to whom you wish to issue a Chargeback. 

7

Select No when prompted to create a Spray Warranty Claim.

8

Reference the tag in the folder entitled WAITING FOR CHARGEBACK. Enter the Sales Number of the product requiring the Chargeback. Press ENTER.

9

 

Enter or scan the barcode of the Tag ID of the material and press ENTER.

10

 

Enter the Chargeback Length and press ACCEPT [F1].

11

Enter the Reason Code for the chargeback and press ENTER.

12

 

All the details of the Sales Number will display. Enter the next item or press ACCEPT [F1].

13

Enter the Vendor Contact, Vendor Authorization Code, and Vendor Authorization Date.  ACCEPT [F1]. Select YES if the Chargeback is complete and you wish to release or select NO if you will work on a Chargeback all month for roll shortages.

Price Adjustment Chargeback

1

If you realize the vendor has charged you the incorrect amount after receiving and closing the purchase order, process a chargeback.

2

Select receive credit from the vendor.

3

Select COUPON as the Chargeback Reason. This is the only selection that will not affect your physical inventory.
