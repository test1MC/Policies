CanWest Inventory Management Policy

  1. CanWest will not accept inventory into their warehouse without the proper paperwork from the manufacturer and/or Sherwin-Williams. Examples of accepted paperwork: Manufacturer Packing List, SW Purchase Order.
  2. CanWest facilities will recommend reorder points to Sherwin-Williams stores for material to be held at the CanWest facility based on the facility’s knowledge of upcoming jobs and demand. The Sherwin-Williams manager should take this information into consideration when placing purchase orders with vendors for additional stock to be held at CanWest.
  3. Once an order is approved by Sherwin-Williams, CanWest will make roll selection for orders from the stock belonging to the store owning the order. 
    1. Roll selection will be made to best minimize waste and to match required dye lots.
  4. If a store does not have enough stock at CanWest for a job, CanWest may take, with approval from Sherwin-Williams, stock belonging to another SW store. 
    1. SW stores are to respond same day to these requests for approval. 
    2. CanWest should contact the store requiring the inventory and will provide information on the store that has inventory available. 
    3. The SW manager of the requesting store must obtain approval from the transferring SW store before granting CanWest approval to proceed with the stock transfer. The store requiring the inventory must place an ISR to the store holding the inventory on the same day that approval is given to CanWest. 
    4. The store transferring material is required to complete an IST.
  5. Once on site, if the CanWest installer finds the details of a job (labor or material required) to be different from quoted labor or material, they are required to obtain approval from the appropriate Sherwin-Williams store before proceeding with the order. 
    1. SW stores are to respond same day to these requests for approval.
  6. When a roll end of SW owned stock is found to be short by an CanWest Warehouser, they will adjust the length of the roll. 
    1. SW Store Managers are to perform a monthly chargeback to the vendor for all roll shortages noted. These shortages can be identified using CanWest Reports.
  7. Canadian stores are to set their Roll Balance Threshold to 09.00. This threshold matches CanWest’s policy of tagging all material under 25 square yards as a remnant (pieces under 12 square yard is considered scrap). 
  8. Stores are required to review the CanWest Reports monthly and perform any required adjustments to inventory.
  9. CanWest Inventory Visits
    1. Stores are required to visit the CanWest facility:
      1. To perform annual April and November inventories. 
        1. Stores should prepare for the visit by printing the Perpetual Inventory Tag Report. See CanWest Reports Overview for additional information about this report. 
        2. While at the facility, stores are required to compare the Perpetual Inventory Tag Report against the physical roll tags at CanWest and CanWest Reports. Match by registration number and verify current length. 
        3. All discrepancies are to be noted on the Perpetual Inventory Tag Report and recorded when recording the counts in POS. 
        4. The Perpetual Inventory Tag Report used during the physical review is to be signed by both the Store Manager and Assistant Manager and filed with all inventory reports. 
        5. When recording inventory, inventory housed at CanWest should be recorded as its own area. 
        6. The CanWest inventory visit may be conducted as a pre-count. 
        7. Stores that are not within proximity to conduct an CanWest visit for inventory must contact their District Manager to arrange for another SW representative to visit the facility and conduct the inspection.  The store is required to produce the Perpetual Inventory Tag Report for the SW representative and must record inventory based on the notations made upon this report by the SW representative.
      2. Quarterly Cycle Counts. 
        1. Stores should prepare for the visit by printing the Perpetual Inventory Tag Report. See CanWest Reports Overview for additional information about this report. 
        2. While at the facility, stores are required to compare the Perpetual Inventory Tag Report against the physical roll tags at CanWest and CanWest Reports. Match by registration number and verify current length. 
        3. All discrepancies are to be noted on the Perpetual Inventory Tag Report and used to perform any required adjustments to inventory through POS. 
        4. The Perpetual Inventory Tag Report used for the quarterly cycle count should be signed and dated by the SW Store Manager and filed in a Floorcovering Cycle Count Folder.
    2. Stores are permitted to roll and measure rolls during their CanWest visits for verification.
    3. Stores are always welcome to visit their inventory at CanWest. 
      1. CanWest requests notification before the visit so that they may ensure that their warehouser will be available to assist during the visit. 
      2. CanWest hours are 7:30AM – 4:30PM. CanWest prefers that visits be arranged after 10AM as warehousers are busy with installers early in the morning.
