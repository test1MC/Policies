Installer Payment Policy

  1. Installer Payments must be processed through Disbursement.
  2. Each installer must be set up with a special Installer Payment Account prior to making any payment to the installer. Installers with these special accounts will be the only installers that you will be able to pay.
  3. Set up all Installer Payment Accounts through the District Credit Office. You will need the installer Name, Address, City, State, Zip Code, Phone Number and Installer Identification Number/Social Security Number. Installer Identification Number/Social Security Number are required when the Installer Payment Account is opened at the District Credit Office.
  4. A completed Subcontractor Agreement, W-9 Form, Declaration of Liability and Automobile Insurance, and Workers Compensation Insurance, if required by the state. If Workers Compensation is not required, the installer must provide a Workers Compensation exemption form and a Sherwin form located in the IPA packet. This information must be on file at EACH store location that uses the installation services of the installer.
  5. Open a separate installer charge account for each installer, if approved, for miscellaneous purchases.
    1. Do NOT deduct supplies from the installer’s bill. Use the approved charge account to handle supplies purchased by the installer.
  6. Installers must submit their invoice to the store before payment can be processed. Installers can add additional charges to the work order for payment.
  7. Installers are not to be paid prior to installation.
  8. Installers are not to be paid in full for a job until the job is completed and inspected. If necessary, a partial payment (draw) can be made to the installer for the amount of work completed. Before issuing a partial payment, contact the customer to make sure that the completed portion of work meets their satisfaction.
  9.  Advanced payment can only be made for the completed portion of installation.
  10. The partial payment (draw) must reference a current job in process.
  11. The customer must be satisfied with the completed installation prior to final payment.
  12. If your installer payment involves a customer settlement in which a second installer is to be paid out of the retainage account of the first installer, do NOT pay the second installer until the retainage amount has been confirmed with the District Credit Office.
  13. All installer paperwork (including invoices and work order amendments) must be retained per schedule requirements. For more information, refer to Filing & Retention Batch Box Overview.
  14. The following policies apply only to stores in Canada:
    1. Tax must be added to Installer Payment disbursements as required by province in Canada.
      1. HST:  British Columbia, Ontario, New Brunswick, Nova Scotia, and Newfoundland and Labrador
      2. GST:  Alberta, Manitoba, Prince Edward Island, Quebec, Saskatchewan
    2. Tax must appear as a separate line in the Installer Payment.
    3. Metals are NOT to be included in installer payment amounts for labor. 
      1. The CanWest Invoice will contain a line for metals used for the installation. This should be added to the sale using non-inventoriable sales number 908-5465.
      2. Pay the installer for metals used via SWeep.
    4. Retainage is NOT required when CanWest is the installer as their work is insured and warrantied.
