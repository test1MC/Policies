Floorcovering Complaint Policy

The key to handling customer complaints is to have a plan and act quickly. Respond to the customer within 24 hours and agree on a time to inspect. Get your sales rep involved. The sales rep always needs to be aware of the situation so there are no surprises when they visit the customer. Below are the approval limits for floorcovering complaints:

Complaint (material & labor)

Authorization Level

Up to $1,500

Store Manager

$1,501 to $5,000

District Manager

$5,001 and up

Vice President of Sales
  1. Floorcovering complaints must be inspected and documented.
  2. Do NOT process customer complaints through the Product Quality Reporting process on the SOURCE system.
  3. A Release Form is required for all customer complaint settlements, excluding warrantee claims.
  4. If the complaint is the fault of the selling store (i.e. incorrect order, wrong measurements), the complaint settlement will be charged back to the selling store.
  5. If the complaint is due to the installer, the repair/resolution can be deducted from the installer’s retainage account.
  6. Product complaints must be registered with the Vendor Sales Representative and the Vendor’s Claims Office.
    1. A Vendor Chargeback must be completed for defective material.
  7. CanWest INSTALLER COMPLAINTS:
    1. Customer complaints should be handled on a case-by-case basis. 
    2. CanWest warranties installation:  Labor complaints not involving replacement material should be handled solely by CanWest. Sherwin-Williams should not make any further installer payments related to resolution of the complaint.
    3. Complaints involving both labor and material should be resolved by joint efforts between CanWest and Sherwin-Williams. 
      1. In general, CanWest should resolve labor complaints and Sherwin-Williams should resolve material complaints unless the material complaint is a result of good material which has been rendered unusable through installation.
      2. If the material complaint is a result of good material which has been rendered unusable through installation, Sherwin-Williams inventory should be used to resolve the complaint and should be sold to CanWest for completion of the installation.
      3. Damaged or unacceptable material should be replaced as requested by the customer and the damaged or unacceptable material charged back to the vendor. Review Floorcovering Complaint Procedure to determine how to handle billing the customer for original or replacement product.
    4. Material quality complaints should be handled by Sherwin-Williams. Damaged or unacceptable material should be replaced as requested by the customer and the damaged or unacceptable material charged back to the vendor.  Review Floorcovering Complaint Procedure to determine how to handle billing the customer for original or replacement product.
