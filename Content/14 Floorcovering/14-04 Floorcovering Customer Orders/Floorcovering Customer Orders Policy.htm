Floorcovering Customer Orders Policy

  1. Each customer/job must have a Customer Job File. This file is to include:
    1. Sherwin-Williams Proposal and Contract or Floorcovering Quote and Contract
    2. Signed Customer Order Entry Confirmation of Installation
    3. Sherwin-Williams Purchase Order (copy on special orders)
    4. Charge Invoice (duplicate copy)
  2. All work resulting in billing a customer MUST be entered into Customer Order Entry as an Installation Order.
  3. The correct paperwork is required for each installation:
    1. DIY Customers:
      1. A Floorcovering Proposal and Contract must be completed for each floorcovering installation. The online Floorcovering Proposal and Contract available on SherLink may also be used. Canadian Stores – contact methods@sherwin.com for information on Canadian Rescission Notices before proceeding with retail orders.
      2. US Stores - Rescission Notice (“Notice”) must be provided to retail customers when Sherwin-Williams will be installing merchandise (e.g. floor covering, blinds, etc.) at the customer’s principal dwelling (i.e. residence) pursuant to a consumer credit transaction (i.e. a transaction entered into primarily for personal, family or household purposes).
        1. Note that two (2) copies of the notice must be provided to all customers who have an ownership interest in (and reside at) the subject residence and the exercise of rescission rights by anyone having such an ownership interest will void the entire transaction.
        2. Retail customer(s) will have three (3) business days after receipt of the Notice or credit disclosures regarding the subject transaction, whichever shall occur last, to rescind the transaction.
        3. Notice of rescission will be deemed to have been given when the Notice is mailed or when filed for telegraphic transmission; otherwise, notice of rescission will be deemed to have been given when received by Sherwin-Williams.
        4. Note that Sherwin-Williams should not proceed with a rescindable transaction until it is reasonably certain that the customer has not exercised his or her right to rescind. Accordingly, before proceeding you should allow for a sufficient amount of time after the expiration of the three (3) business day rescission period for the Notice to reach S-W by mail or in the alternative, you may obtain written confirmation from a customer after the expiration of the three (3) business day rescission period that they have not exercised their right to rescind the subject transaction. When calculating the three (3) business day rescission period the last of the events to occur that are referenced in Section 1 of the Notice should be used as the first day of the three (3) business day rescission period. For purposes of rescission, “business days” mean all calendar days except for Sundays and the following legal public holidays: (a) New Year’s Day; (b) the birthday of Martin Luther King, Jr.; (c) Washington’s Birthday; (d) Memorial Day; (e) Independence Day; (f) Labor Day; (g) Columbus Day; (h) Veterans Day; (i) Thanksgiving Day; and (j) Christmas Day.
        5. Do not place orders for special order merchandise with vendor until the rescission notice has expired.
    2. Commercial Customers must complete one of the following:
      1. A Floorcovering Proposal and Contract must be completed for floorcovering installation that requires a special order or will be COD.  The online Floorcovering Proposal and Contract available on SherLink may also be used. 
      2. Completed and signed Commercial Floorcovering Quote and Contract (Apartment Replacement) (not every day turn business), identifying the cost of material, agreed installation and cost per unit for each commercial account.
  4. The use of Price Record Cards for pricing for repeat jobs is required.
  5. Orders must be entered before installation occurs.
  6. The customer must have a valid account in good standing before releasing any materials. Available credit balance should be verified or District Credit should be contacted for job approval before each installation.
  7. A COD account (cash on delivery) may not be given any materials without first obtaining full payment. A COD shipment must be delivered by a Sherwin-Williams delivery truck or by a store employee. Any exception to this policy must be approved in writing by your Division Financial Manager and/or Area Credit Manager.
  8. Customers are to be charged for an amount of padding equal to the total amount of carpet (in length) billed on a customer order regardless of the length of padding used on a job, unless partial pad replacement has been requested by the customer.
  9. The following policies apply only to stores in Canada:
    1. Floorcovering should be purchased on a separate job account unless customer requests separate paint and floorcovering accounts.
    2. SW stores are to approve all order requests received by CanWest before installation may begin. Available credit must be verified before approval is given to CanWest.
      1. Customer orders must be entered into POS before approval is given to CanWest. The Customer Order Number should be provided to CanWest as PO # for CanWest. Use the Customer Order Number as a reference between SW and CanWest to verify continued credit availability and to verify any discrepancies between the originally approved order and the final installation.
      2. Stores should communicate any lockout information that may be relevant during a job to CanWest (communicate lockout information via email to CanWest to hold installs due to credit lockouts).
    3. Labor should be charged at a 10% upcharge from to all new customer business gained after implementation of the POS Floorcovering Software. 
      1. Existing customers will continue to be charged as usual.
      2. District Manager Approval is required if a rate lower than the 10% upcharge is needed for competitive advantage.
