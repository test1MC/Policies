Floorcovering Complaint & Customer Pays Original Invoice Procedure

Step

Action

1

Create a Re-Work Work Order ensuring that you identify the original work order. Please see Re-Work Work Order Procedure for additional information on creating Re-Work Work Orders.

2

List materials on the re-work order stage for expense to the Floorcovering Complaints G/L Code, and the customer will not be charged for re-installation. 

Note: If the inventory/labor used for the re-work should be charged back to the vendor, you may cancel their staging to expense in Work Order Disposition so that they may be charged back to the vendor.

3

If the installer should pay for the materials, contact the District Credit Office for issuance of a debit ARJV which will remove the requested amount from the original installer's IPA retainage account and credit your Floorcovering Complaints account. If you have already paid the installer, include the original installation amount in the ARJV.

Note: The ARJV cannot be processed for an amount greater than the installer retainage account. If there is not enough in the account, you will need to have another ARJV processed once the retainage amount is able to cover the balance.
