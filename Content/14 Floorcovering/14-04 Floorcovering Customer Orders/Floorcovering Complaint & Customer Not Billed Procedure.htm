Floorcovering Complaint & Customer Not Billed Procedure

Step

 Action

1

Untag the material in the Work Order. 

2

Determine if this inventory should be charged back to the vendor or expensed to the Floorcovering Complaints G/L Code.

3

If the complaint was due to installer error, do not pay that installer for the original installation.

4

If a different installer is used for the re-installation, update the installer information on the Work Order to the new installer.

When processing the payment for the second installer, from POS desktop, select Store -> Point of Sale -> Press Page Down to access Special Menu -> Disbursement -> Installer Payment by Store Draft -> Enter Pay-To Info -> Select Customer will not be Billed -> Customer Satisfaction -> Original Installer Not Available. Making these selections will trigger a debit against the complaint account. The ARJV will credit the complaint account.

If the first installer has a retainage balance, contact the District Credit Office to have them issue a debit ARJV, which will remove the requested amount from the original installer's account.

5

Once you have determined how to handle the customer settlement, you will also need to determine how to handle the originally installed product. If your complaint is in regards to a product and not an installation issue, a chargeback to the vendor is required:

  • Notify your vendor sales representative to register the complaint. Obtain the claim number from the Vendor Claim Office before processing the vendor chargeback.
  • Contact the vendor to receive a Return Authorization (RA) number.
  • Process a Chargeback for defective material and vendor reimbursement for labor.

If your complaint is solely in regards to an installation issue and no chargeback is required to the vendor:

  • Process a Store Expense transaction if material is used to satisfy the complaint and the customer will not be billed. Use CUST COMP F/C INSTALLATION, prime-sub 7504-101, while processing the Store Expense transaction.
