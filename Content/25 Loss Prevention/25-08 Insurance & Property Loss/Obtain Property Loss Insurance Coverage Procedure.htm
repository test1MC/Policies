Obtain Property Loss Insurance Coverage Procedure

Step

Action

1

Notify Division Loss Prevention of the loss so an LOI report is generated and entered into the LOI database.

2

Contact Risk Management (risk.management@sherwin.com) immediately if the loss is caused by an identifiable third party such as a customer or delivery vehicle or if the loss approaches $100,000.

Note: If the party at fault is known, obtain a police report as well as the third party’s insurance, if applicable, and forward to the Risk Management Department.

3

After reporting the loss, send documentation (invoices, expense transactions) of the loss to Stores Accounting (FAX:  216-566-3537 / PHONE:  216-566-2255). If criminal activity (break in, vandalism, vehicular incident, etc.) was the cause of the incident, a police report must be obtained and forwarded to Risk Management and Stores Accounting.

4

Inventory of lost or damaged merchandise is reported at store cost as follows:

If

Then

Destroyed merchandise

Proceed to Step 5.

Merchandise sold below store cost

Contact District Manager prior to salvage sale and include salvage income in your loss documentation.

Note: Every practical effort should be utilized to identify an accurate inventory loss. When possible, include the actual inventory figures for damaged or salable inventory as documentation for an insurance claim. When inventory damages are extensive, and sometimes unidentifiable, i.e., a store fire or natural disaster, it may not be practical to count all damaged inventory and expense through the store computer system. Special arrangements can be made through Stores Accounting to obtain a value of damaged inventory.

5

Send copies of receipts, invoices, or other proof of expenses for repairs, clean up, debris removal, asset replacement to Stores Accounting.  Use prime sub 0515-006 (Recoverable Loss – Insurance) when paying invoices through SWEEP or by draft.

6

Notify Fixed Asset Accounting of all fixed assets damaged or destroyed. This can be done by completing an Asset Retirement/Transfer of Fixed Assets form, which can be found in the Forms Repository (keyword: fixed asset).  Once Fixed Assets Accounting removes the damaged/destroyed asset(s) from a store’s property listing, the store will no longer be charged a monthly depreciation charge for the asset that was removed from the property listing.
