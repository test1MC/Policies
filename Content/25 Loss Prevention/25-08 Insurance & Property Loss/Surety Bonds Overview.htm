Surety Bonds Overview

Bond Type

Description

Bid Bond

A bid bond is an indemnity bond to bid on work. If a contractor fails to enter into a contract awarded to him, the bond is then forfeited. A bid bond is submitted with the bid proposal and takes the place of a cashier's check or a certified check. When requesting a bid bond, the percentage amount of the bond as well as the actual amount of the bid is required. There is no cost to the store for the bid bond.

Performance Bond

A performance bond guarantees that the job will be done in accordance with the contract, plans and specifications that are made part of the bond. A performance bond is issued after the contract has been awarded.

Payment Bond

A payment bond guarantees that any laborer who performed the work covered by the performance bond as well as suppliers who furnished necessary materials will be paid. A payment bond is also issued after the contract has been awarded.

Utility Guarantee

Bond in lieu of a deposit and guarantees payment of utility bills.

License Bond

Bond required by certain municipalities before work can be performed in that particular locale.

When required, fax a copy of the bond requirements together with the relevant sections of the contract to Corporate Risk Management (216-566-3025). Make sure the due date of the bid, the percentage amount of the bond and the total amount of the bid are included in the information that is faxed.

Note: If a special bond form is required, also fax a copy of this to Corporate Risk Management. To obtain additional information, contact the Risk Management Department (risk.management@sherwin.com).
