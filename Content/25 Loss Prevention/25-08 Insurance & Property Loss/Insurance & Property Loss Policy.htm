Insurance & Property Loss Policy

  1. Any loss is to be reported to the Division Loss Prevention Director (Automotive Division report to the Director of Facilities and Safety) in one of two ways:
    1. Complete a Loss, Offense, and Incident Report. For more information, see Loss, Offense, & Incident Policy.
    2. Call in the information to Division Loss Prevention if this process is approved in your Division.
  2. After Division Loss Prevention enters the loss into the LOI database, Stores Accounting will collect the information from the database and contact the store. If a loss is not in the LOI database, store credit will not be given.
  3. The claim is the total of:
    1. Replacement cost of Fixed Assets
    2. Actual cash value of Fixed Assets is not replaced
    3. Average selling price of inventory lost or destroyed less salvage value
    4. Clean up expenses, including employee wages, outside temporary help, or other companies hired to assist in clean up
    5. Actual disposal costs
    6. Other actual costs incurred due to the loss incident that are the responsibility of the store
  4. Any coverage for outside storage areas must be approved in advance.
  5. Losses of an undetermined cause or date are not covered.
  6. Claims are subject to review by Risk Management.
  7. Large claims will require an inspection by our insurance company adjuster.
  8. Do not dispose of any damaged property (inventory or otherwise) until instructed to by the Risk Management Department.
  9. Once the credit entry is processed by Risk Management, the loss is considered "settled” and cannot be reopened.
  10. Any loss not reported within one year of the occurrence date will not be credited. Any loss reported but not actively followed-up on will be closed one year after the date of the incident.
  11. Personal property should be reported to employee’s homeowner insurance.
  12. All claims are subject to the deductible of $5,000.
  13. The responsible party, (Landlord or Sherwin-Williams) who should pay for all building damage, must be determined.
