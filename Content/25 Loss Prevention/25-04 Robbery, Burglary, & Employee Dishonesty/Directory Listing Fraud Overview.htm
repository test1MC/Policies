Directory Listing Fraud Overview

Scammers use directory listings as an opportunity to solicit unneeded and unwanted non-value added services from businesses. They may contact you by fax, phone, mail, or email under the guise of validating and confirming your store’s contact information. The fine print within the document states that by signing and returning the document, the company is ordering an expensive directory listing. Once signed and returned, the company is invoiced for the unwanted service. Con artists will often follow up with additional billings or even sell company info to other scam services.

Be on the alert!  Whatever the scam, these con artists and their methods continuously evolve. Best practices:

  • Know Sherwin-Williams’ policies on interacting with external businesses and services.
  • Always read correspondence carefully.
  • When in doubt, contact your Loss Prevention representative.
