Audit Closing Conference Procedure

Step

Action

During the Audit Visit

1

Review the auditor's credentials before granting access to store records, merchandise, or funds.

2

If necessary, clear an area so that the auditor has adequate workspace, including access to a desk.

3

If the auditor wants to take documents out of the store, make a list of the requested items and make sure that the auditor signs for all items.

4

Answer the auditor's questions as fully as possible.

5

If any serious policy violations are discovered, the auditor may report them to the District Manager before the audit is completed. Under these circumstances, the District Manager may decide to visit the store immediately.

6

When the auditor asks to schedule a closing conference, arrange for the store to be adequately staffed so that the conference can proceed without interruptions.

7

During the closing conference, discuss with the auditor all items listed on the Closing Conference. Request clarification for items you do not understand or disagree with. The Manager's signature on the closing conference memo indicates his/her acknowledgment of the Auditor's statements, all audit exceptions, and his/her responses.

Note: Respond to each audit infraction and add comments, especially if you understand but don’t agree with the results.

Sign the Closing Conference papers. Make sure that you are in total agreement with the closing conference. If not, call your District Manager.

After the Conference

1

After the conference, the auditor will leave the Store Manager one copy of the closing conference memo and will e-mail a copy to the District Manager.

2

All responses in the Closing Conference will be reviewed by the District Manager who will follow up with the auditor regarding any questions or concerns for the Manager's prepared responses.

The District Manager has 10 business days to review responses and discuss with Store Management and the auditor. The District Manager is not permitted to make changes to Management responses and is not permitted to generate responses for the Manager. The District Manager can add their own response if they find that the Manager’s response is not acceptable.

3

If a response is not accepted, the District Manager cannot change a Manager’s response. However, they can provide their own comments to the auditor to be included as part of the audit report.

4

Any specific concerns regarding a response can be forwarded by the District Manager to the Division Vice-President of Sales and the President of Store’s Group. All audit responses can be viewed online.

5

If there are any questions about the audit grading, contact the District Manager.
