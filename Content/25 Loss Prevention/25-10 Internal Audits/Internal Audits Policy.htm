Internal Audits Policy

  1. The auditor's identification must be verified before permitting access to store records.
  2. The auditor must sign for all documents removed from the store.
  3. If requested, the auditor is allowed to work in the store after normal operating hours. The Manager or the Assistant Manager must be present during an after-hours work session.
    1. The auditor cannot be left alone in the store.
  4. Each audit closes with a conference between the Store Manager and the auditor to review the entire audit.
    1. It is the Manager's responsibility to review the auditor's findings and receive clarification prior to the Auditor leaving the store.
    2. If the Store Manager requests, the District Manager may be present.
  5. A reply to each item discussed in the Closing Conference is required during the audit. The reply should include comments and the corrective action that has been or will be taken with respect to each audit finding. The responses should be addressed to the District Manager and should include measures taken to prevent any further policy violations. District Manager questions or concerns regarding the Closing Conference should be routed directly to the auditor.
  6. Questions or concerns about grading issues should be routed to the District Manager. Grading issues will be routed from the District Manager to the Division Office.
  7. The District Manager is responsible for monitoring store activities after an audit to ensure that violations cited in the Audit Report do not reoccur. Audit points will be assessed if two or more repeat infractions and no manager change since the previous audit.
  8. At any time during the course of an audit, an auditor may discover a particular situation that represents a potential for immediate and/or actual loss of the Company's assets. When such a situation is encountered, the actual comment describing the situation is classified as a "Red Border/Potential Irregularity".
