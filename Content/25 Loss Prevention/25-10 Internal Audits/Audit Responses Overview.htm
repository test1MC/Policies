Audit Responses Overview

  • The purpose of obtaining management responses is to:

    • Provide a more proactive audit approach, where managers have a complete understanding of the proper way to resolve identified audit exceptions.

    • Provide management with a final product in a more timely manner.
    • Increase communication between the auditor and store/district personnel throughout the audit process.
    You should be interacting with your auditor daily in order to take time throughout the audit process to discuss and review the topics that they have covered each day. During this time you should begin to think about the steps that should be taken to resolve the current problems and how to prevent any future occurrences.
  • Once you have determined the best approach to resolve the audit infraction the Manager will need to provide the auditor with a daily written response for all items discussed so that the Closing Conference is a simple review of the topics reviewed during the audit. The written response should be prepared with the District Manager as the target audience. Each response should be proactive to show how the problem will be resolved and future occurrences prevented.
  • Final Audit Reports will be issued with Management responses for review by Stores Group or Division Management.

Note: Audit Responses are required to be obtained at the time of the audit. If responses are not as outlined per policy, the auditor may contact the District Manager to involve them in the process. District Managers are no longer required to send a cover letter with the copy of the audit to Stores Group or Division Management Stores Group and Division Executives are able to review all audits on the SOURCE Intranet.

Acceptable Responses:
Hazardous Materials Shipping Papers Not Completed Training will be given to all store employees, especially drivers, regarding this form. The order maker will be responsible for creating the hazardous materials shipping paper, but the driver will be responsible for making sure the paper has been completed and is accurate. This paper is to accompany the signed customer order entry ticket and kept with the batching.
Tax Exempt Certificates Not On File Periodic reviews of accounts purchasing tax exempt, along with checking tax cards at the point of sale allow for more accurate accounts of tax exempt certificates.
All employees will be trained to check the tax exempt folder prior to releasing material as tax exempt. These folders will now be kept near the point of sale for easy access during transactions.
MMS Inventory Control Weakness Every Monday, the negative inventory report will be printed, reviewed and cleared by the manager and assistant manager.
Weekly Cycle Counts Not Completed Cycle counts will be performed every Sunday. This should give us ample time to perform a thorough and complete cycle count. The cycle count reports will now be available and posted to serve as a reminder throughout the week. In addition to the items on the cycle count bulletin, 40 of our core products (at least 20 on the sales floor) will be counted to help ensure an accurate inventory.
Mispriced Items on Sales Floor We will continue to check store pricing on the shelves, and update any pricing that is inaccurate. This will be done in conjunction with cycle counts, so as a product is counted, a price check will be done at the same time. The printed cycle count can then be used to create new shelf tags.
