Keyholder Control Policy

  1. The Manager, Assistant Manager, Operations Manager, and Management Trainees are the only persons authorized to possess store keys without additional approvals on file.
  2. Additional keyholders:
    1. Must be approved before you may provide the employee with a key to your location.
    2. May be considered for a keyholder position after successfully completing a minimum of ninety days employment with the company.  This ninety day prerequisite is to ensure that the employee has a strong understanding of the systems and operations of the company and store, and will allow the employee the ability to be able to appropriately handle most situations that may arise while they are managing the business during those operating hours. 
      1. Exceptions to this requirement require prior approval from the Vice President of Sales.
    3. Must possess a valid driver’s license and have minimal moving violations. 
    4. Must complete the Sherwin-Williams Auto Insurance Declaration Form stating that they have, at least, the minimum liability insurance coverage requirements within their local area.
      1. The keyholder must check the box on the form: “I may need to use my personal car for company business.” 
      2. Keyholders must agree to perform deposit procedures according to policy.
    5. Must complete a successful background investigation based on company background investigation criteria. 
  3. When a keyholder is transferred, suspended, dismissed, or resigns, the keyholder must return the key immediately.
  4. Only approved keyholders are to open/close the store, open/close the POS, and make deposits. 
    1. If a keyholder is not present at the time an interim deposit is needed, the deposit should be prepared and placed in the safe until the keyholder is available to make the deposit.
  5. Any employee is permitted to take money to the bank to make change; however, non-keyholders are restricted to taking $50 or less to the bank.
  6. Non-keyholders are permitted to be in the store without keyholders.
  7. For safety concerns, it is not an acceptable practice for any Sherwin-Williams employee to walk to the bank, ride a bike to the bank, be picked up by another party to ride to the bank, or take public transportation to the bank in order to make deposits. 
    1. Any exception to this policy requires prior approval of the Vice President of Sales and the Vice President of Human Resources prior to the request being submitted to Loss Prevention.
  8. When proceeding to the bank to make deposits, the employee must go immediately to the bank without:
    1. Making extra stops between the store and bank.
    2. Having extra passengers who are not Sherwin-Williams employees.
