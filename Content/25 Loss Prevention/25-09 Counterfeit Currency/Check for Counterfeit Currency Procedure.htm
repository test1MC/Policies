Check for Counterfeit Currency Procedure

Step Action
Counterfeit Currency

1

Use the U.S. Currency Counterfeit Marker, which contains an iodine solution that reacts with the starch in wood-based paper, to test currency.

If ... Then ...

A black or gray stain appears

The currency could be counterfeit.

Proceed to Step 2.

A yellow or gold mark appears

The currency is authentic and the sale can be completed. 

2

Handle the counterfeit currency carefully.

3

Ask for a different form of payment.

If... Then...

The customer can provide an alternative form of payment

Process the sale.

The customer cannot provide an alternative form of payment

Do not process the sale or release any merchandise.

4

After the customer leaves,  note as much as possible about the person: a physical description,  name, or automobile license plate number if visible from inside the facility.

5

 If you are able to retain the counterfeit currency, limit the handling of the note by placing it in a protective covering, such as an envelope.

6

Contact Loss Prevention for more instructions.

7

If your bank detects a counterfeit bill in the deposit, note the finding on the close sheet and file an LOI with Loss Prevention.
