Physical Security Review Procedure

The Physical Security Review identifies areas where the store can be compromised by gaining access through hardware that may not function properly or provide adequate protection to an area. The Physical Security Review should be completed once a month by the Store Management team and done so on a consistent basis.

Step

Action

1

Test the panic alarms to ensure the contacts, motions, and glass are functioning properly.

Step

Action

1

Notify the alarm provider before testing.

2

Set the alarm and test each panic device.

3

Verify with the alarm provider after the test to ensure all panic device signals were received.

Note: All non-functional devices should be scheduled for service immediately.

2

Verify fire exit push bars are engaging and releasing once the bar is pushed.

3

Verify all locking hardware is working properly.

4

Ensure that all sets of management keys are accounted for and with the appropriate parties.

5

Verify that necessary cores have been changed after an involuntary management termination where the keys are not recovered or management loses the key.

Note: Any change to keys and cores will be ordered through Bass Security. Loss Prevention should be notified in situations where cores and  keys are being reordered. Unused keys must be kept in a secure safe or locked filing cabinet.

6

Ensure that the safe combination is not on day lock, if applicable, and that the manager is the only person with the safe combination.

Note: If the store uses a locked cabinet, ensure that the keys to it are only maintained by the managers and approved 3rd key holders.
