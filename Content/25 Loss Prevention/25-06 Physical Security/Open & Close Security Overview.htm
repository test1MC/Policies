Open & Close Security Overview

In order to ensure employee safety and discourage theft or robbery, employees should follow these practices:

  • Two or more people should arrive at the store at approximately the same time and enter the store together.
  • Employees should note any strangers or vehicles in the area. If they suspect suspicious activity, employees should not enter the store, but rather, notify the local police department immediately.
  • The perimeter of the store should be checked for signs of illegal entry such as windows or doors left ajar, broken glass, etc. If signs of illegal entry are found, the employees should notify the local police immediately.
  • Once in the store, turn off any alarms and conduct a thorough investigation of the premise by observing the lights, ensuring the safe has not been tampered with, and that no merchandise has been removed from the store.
  • If a break-in has occurred, contact the police immediately, then notify the District Manager and Division Loss Prevention.
  • At night, employees should not count the money on the Point of Sale Wrap Counter in plain view of the store's windows. Count all currency and checks in the Manager's Office. For more information, see Close Policy.
  • Prior to the final exit from the store, a member of management should check to ensure that all customers and employees have left the store.
  • A final check of the cash drawer should be made to see that all the money had been placed in the safe and/or bank deposit.
  • If the store has an alarm, it should be set and checked to ensure that it is working properly.
  • All doors should be locked and the person responsible for the Close (keyholder) should ensure that all employees are safely in their cars prior to leaving.
