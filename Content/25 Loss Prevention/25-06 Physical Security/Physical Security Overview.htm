Physical Security Overview

The Sherwin-Williams Company is concerned with the security of its stores' premises and merchandise. Security involves protecting the company's personnel and physical assets, including: money, merchandise, equipment, supplies, records and confidential information. The cost of theft and vandalism to the company can be enormous. Sherwin-Williams relies on the honesty and integrity of its employees to help safeguard the company's assets.

In order to ensure that all aspects of the physical security (e.g. alarm systems, access control, panic /push bar alarms, locks, cores, keys, safes/locking file cabinets and off-site storage facilities) are functioning properly, they must be inspected on a consistent basis.  Although specific security measures may differ according to the floor plans or operations conducted at the site, there are similarities in our operations where these guidelines can be universally applied:

  • Maintain building security by keeping all warehouse windows locked at all times and ensure that the back door of the warehouse cannot be opened from the outside.
  • Keep all windows and doors in good repair to help prevent break-ins.
  • Do not keep any copies of the safe combination anywhere in the store.
  • When an employee authorized to know the safe combination leaves employment at the store (termination, promotion) and does NOT return their key to the store, follow the Lost & Unreturned Keys Procedure to have the core replaced. Post police and fire emergency numbers next to the store's telephones.
  • Install a buzzer at the rear entrance for deliveries. Have chimes at the front door to signal when a customer enters the store.
  • Ensure all skylights and air conditioning vents are secure.
