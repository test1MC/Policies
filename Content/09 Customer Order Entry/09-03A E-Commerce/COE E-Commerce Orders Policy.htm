COE E-Commerce Orders Policy

  1. E-Commerce orders are generated when a customer places an order online. While placing the order, the customer will know their cost, and you will not be permitted to change the selling price or add items to E-Commerce customer orders.
  2. Locations may receive an E-Commerce order that contains only comments without a sales number. Employees are required to research the customer request and modify the line on the customer order.
  3. EDI orders from select customers will contain only the last four digits of the customer credit card number. Employees should secure a signed Authorization to Retain and Use Account Information Form, located in the Forms Repository, from the customer. Reference the form to match the last four digits of the credit card to the complete credit card number and expiration date.
    1. After completing this form, it must be retained in either a locked filing cabinet or locked store safe.
    2. Use the credit card information while processing the point-of-sale transaction to bill the customer. Contact the customer if updated credit card information is required.
    3. Employees could receive a faxed letter from National Accounts with the GSA Purchase Order Number and credit card number. Follow the instructions on the letter to process the sale. Shred the faxed document after processing the sale to protect customer credit card information and meet PCI Compliance.
