Storage & Trailer Maintenance Policy

  1. Each trailer must be maintained appropriately and in good working order before every use. If the trailer is determined to not be safe for transportation or the mixing of paint, address the concerns before moving forward with any demonstration.
  2. The trailer’s license plate tag must be up-to-date and legal for road use.
  3. Before each use, check to ensure the tires are safe for the road and all lights (turn signals, breaking lights, etc.) on the trailer are in good working condition.
  4. Any safety concerns within the trailer (spills, loose equipment, etc.) should be addressed before road transportation.
  5. A fire extinguisher, pair of goggles, and spill kit must be with the trailer at all times.
  6. When the trailer is not in use, it must be locked and stored indoors. If the trailer is too large to be stored indoors, all material must be removed from the trailer and stored in a Sherwin-Williams Automotive branch.
  7. The trailer should always be stored at a Sherwin-Williams location.
  8. The material on the trailer should be turned daily in order to prevent it from going bad.
  9. Any fixed asset/equipment maintenance/additions must be approved via an eExpense or eFCA prior to any work being completed.
