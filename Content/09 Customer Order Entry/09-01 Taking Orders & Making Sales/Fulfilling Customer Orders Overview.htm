Fulfilling Customer Orders Overview

Method Features

Quick Order Entry

  • Quickly enter customer orders since customer account or product number are not required
  • Search  the local database and complete Manual POST information.

Note: Do not use Quick Order to create orders when the system is down as they may be lost when the system is restored.

Customer Order Entry

  • Receive EDI orders
  • Manually enter customer orders
  • Access Customer Service Options
  • Access other functions in SOURCE such as purchase orders for shipments made direct to the customer, sales transactions, store expense transactions for demonstrations, a BOLDOC for creating BOLs for delivery order

Note: Customer Order Entry requires more specific information such as Customer PO #, Order Job #, Delivery Information, and certain types of customer service option requests. 

Point-of-Sale

  • Process a sales or return transaction
  • Process a customer’s payment on an account
  • Internally dispose of product (store expense)
  • Manage customer complaints  through Product Quality Reporting, which allows stores to log requests for merchandise or financial reimbursement, notify headquarters about product issues, and ask product quality questions.

Note: A sale transaction is almost identical to a customer order. However, since customer service and tinting are not available in the sale, the transaction has limited use as a starting point for any transaction. Typically users will pull their Customer Order into a sale transaction to complete the billing.

No matter how a customer’s request is recorded initially, it is completed with a Point-of-Sale Transaction. 

Note: All orders requiring tint must be initiated through COE or Quick Order. Tinted product is not returnable, however, it can be adjusted for customer satisfaction.
