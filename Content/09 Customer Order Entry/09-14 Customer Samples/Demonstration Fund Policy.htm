Demonstration Fund Policy

This section does not apply to Automotive Branches.

  1. All demonstrations should be processed in the home store of the customer or the location that will benefit.
  2. Demonstrations are for customers who have a valid Sherwin-Williams cash or charge account number.  Demonstrations for walk-in customers are not allowed.
  3. The Demonstration Fund is for GOM Paint and GOM Brushes, including Purdy, that will be supported by paint sales profits.  The Demonstration Fund is not to be used for additional products required to do the demonstration, such as: drywall, nails, wood, etc.  Additional items are considered store expenses.
  4. The Demonstration Fund can be used for purchasing competitors’ paint products to compare to GOM paint products.
  5. Unused drawdowns must also be recycled in the PaintCare bin and recorded on the PaintCare Mistint and Drawdowns Recycling log. Please refer to section PaintCare Recovery Program Overview for more information on Mistint and Drawdown Handling.
  6. The Demonstration Fund is not to be used to release, test, or demonstrate merchandise to employees. Store expense merchandise must be used for employee demonstrations or tests.
  7. The Demonstration Fund is not to be used for donations, mistints, complaint settlement, obsolete, or damaged products.
  8. The Demonstration Fund is not to be used for spray equipment demonstrations.  For more information, see Spray Equipment Demonstration Policy.
  9. A second demonstration is acceptable only if the first was unsuccessful or the demonstration is given to a wider audience with different participants. 
  10. Samples cannot be IST'd from one store to another if the Demonstration Fund is depleted.
  11. Districts will lose their demonstration credit if they attempt to move demonstration funds from one store to another.
  12. All demonstration products on a Customer Order must remain on a separate order than products that are not marked for demonstration.  This means that two separate picking tickets may be needed.
  13. Districts using Test Drive Program:  All Demonstration Customer Orders with the exception of drawdowns must be approved by the District Manager via email.  Retain approval with order paperwork in batching.
  14. Districts using Test Drive Program:  While the Demonstration Fund may be used with DM approval, it is recommended to use Test Drive rather than Demonstration Fund in particular situations, such as: drawdowns, customer refuses to pay for Test Drive, and large investment conversions for property management and home builders.
  15. All demonstrations must be processed through the Demonstration Order Option in Customer Order Entry.
  16. The Demonstration Customer Order (with the exception of drawdowns) must be signed by the customer. If delivered, the signature on the Confirmation of Delivery will suffice.  If not, print a copy of the order and have the customer sign it. This copy must be retained and filed appropriately.
