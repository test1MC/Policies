Demonstration Fund Overview

This section does not apply to Automotive Branches.

The monthly Demonstration Fund is used to encourage customers to try new and existing products by offsetting some of the demonstration expense. 

The following chart provides examples of what situations qualify as appropriate use of the Demonstration Fund. This list is not conclusive, though, and some situations are better handled through other programs such as Test Drive:

Appropriate Demo

Inappropriate Demo
  • GOM paint and GOM brushes, including Purdy products, which the customer does not regularly purchase.
  • Paint or paint-outs submitted to an architect or owner for approval of color or finish.
  • Product provided to a contractor for use as a sample to secure a job or project.
  • A drawdown for sampling a color. Drawdowns should be expensed under the prime-sub for drawdown, Draw Down Expense 7599-119 [old 7599-896]
  • Mistinted Paint.
  • Paint provided at no charge as a price concession to a customer.
  • A donation to any organization, whether on the company’s or customer’s behalf.
  • Paint provided as a complaint settlement to a customer.

Note: Instead of a monthly demonstration credit, Product Finishes Group facilities receive an annual Demonstration Expense at the beginning of the year. However, PFG facilities must comply with all Demonstration Fund procedures. Contact the District Manager regarding any questions related to the proper usage of the Demonstration Fund.
