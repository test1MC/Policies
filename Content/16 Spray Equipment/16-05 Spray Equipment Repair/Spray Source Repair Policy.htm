Spray Source Repair Policy

  1. Stores with a Spray Source Repair Center within their district are required to use this center for repair of any equipment brought into their store.
  2. One customer order should be created for each repair. Ex. If the customer brings in three units for repair, three customer orders must be created (one for each repair).
  3. Equipment leaving a store for a Spray Source Repair Center for repair must be tagged with the paperwork created during the repair order creation.
  4. Stores are only to use the class number 7401-GP when an external repair facility is being used for the repair.
  5. Store employees should make available all current labor rates for repair to customers before initiating a repair contract (placing the customer order).

  8. If your customer is requesting a repair, always ask the customer is they would like to rent a unit for the duration of their repair.
  9. A $100 deposit for repairs is required to be collected from customers without a Sherwin-Williams account in good standing. If the customer does not want to have the unit repaired, the $100 deposit will cover the diagnostic fee and other incidental charges. For more information on deposits, refer to Deposit Processing Overview.
    1. When the customer returns, refund the customer the different between the deposit and diagnostic fee using a Correction Transaction. 
    2. If the customer abandons the unit, the deposit may be used to cover the diagnostic fee and other incidental charges incurred in disposing of the unit, and the deposit should be processed as a sale to the customer.
  10. Orders transferred to Spray Source Repair Centers must be billed by the Spray Source Repair Center. The originating location is responsible for communicating form of payment information to the Spray Source Repair Center as follows:
    1. If a credit card is provided for payment, the Authorization to Retain and Use Form must be completed and retained at the originating store. Once the Spray Source Center is ready to bill the customer, they are to call and receive the credit card information from the originating store by phone, after which the originating store will destroy the Authorization to Retain and Use Form. This form is NOT to be faxed or transported to the Spray Source location.
    2. A check may be presented to the delivery driver as payment upon delivery of the repaired unit.
    3. If the originating store took a deposit for the original order, SMIS Operations should be contacted (216-566-2287 or 216-515-7475) by the originating store to transfer the deposit to the Spray Source Repair Center.
  11. Once a unit has been diagnosed, an estimate should be presented to the customer.
    1. If the repair is being made at a Sherwin-Williams location, it is the responsibility of the store completing the repair to handle this communication with the customer. 
    2. If the repair is being handled at an external location, the vendor should be notified NOT to proceed with the repair until the estimate has been approved by the customer. The estimate should be communicated to the Sherwin-Williams store initiating the repair who will communicate directly with the customer. The vendor is not to have direct communication with the customer.
  12. Customers are to be charged a diagnostic fee when no repair is completed.
  13. Customers are to be charged a Shop Charge for nuts, bolts, washers, and hazardous materials disposal.
  14. Consumable items such as lubricants, oils, or other items falling under the shop charge caption, which are not billed to a customer or charged back to a vendor, should be expensed to prime-sub 7402-104 (Spray Repair Misc). 
  15. Freight charges should be added to invoices for any special order parts.
  16. Customers should be notified that weekly storage charge rates will begin to apply if their unit is not picked up within one week of notification of the completed repair.
    1. Additionally, customers should be aware that Sherwin-Williams is not responsible for units left at the repair center sixty days after the customer has been notified that the repair is complete.
  17. List prices associated with the standard repair sales numbers for labor are not to be discounted for contractors.
  18. Repairs cannot be processed free of labor. 
    1. All billable hours, even those for which the customer will not be charged, must be accounted for on the customer order.
      1. The quantity entered MUST reflect the hours worked.
      2. The price can only be adjusted to a 99% discount.
    2. The District Manager must approve all non-warranty repairs for which the customer will not be charged labor.
  19. Repair parts are not to be discounted from list price.
  20. A minimum of one hour should be noted on each order for repair labor charges. This means that the quantity noted for labor must be at least 1.0.
    1. Quantities noted on the order for labor hours should be rounded to the nearest quarter hour. For example, if two hours and twenty-five minutes were spent on the unit, this should be rounded to two and a half hours and the quantity noted in the order detail as 2.5.
  21. A safety check per manufacturer's instructions should be completed on all equipment before releasing to the customer.
  22. Stores should be aware of the following policies regarding all warranty claims:
    1. Use only OEM parts for warranty repairs. 
    2. Verify warranty coverage on necessary repairs.
    3. Abusive use or misuse damages are not covered by warranty.
    4. When completing the chargeback to the vendor, include the following charges:
      1. An $8.00 shop charge to help offset the cost of shop materials used during the repair and the cost of handling waste generated during the repair. Use Sales Number 163-2157. 
      2. Labor Charges as follows:
        1. Standard Spray Units (non-handheld) should be charged repair labor to the vendor at a rate of $70.00 per hour, with a 2-hour minimum using sales number 163-2116.
        2. Graco Handheld Units (ProShot, ProShot Fine Finish, TrueCoat, TrueCoat Plus) should be charged labor at a rate of $70.00 per hour using the time noted below and sales number 163-2116.

  23. For standard spray units (non-handheld) only, calculate the cost of parts used for the Warranty Repairs at a 30% gross margin over your delivered store cost (product + inbound freight). Enter this calculated cost on the chargeback. This applies whether you have to order the part or you take the part from your existing store inventory.
  24. Stores are required to follow-up with their District Manager on all completed repairs left at the facility for more than 90 days. Stores should document all customer contact regarding the repair.
