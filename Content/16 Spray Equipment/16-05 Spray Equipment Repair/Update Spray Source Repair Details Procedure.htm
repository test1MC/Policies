Update Spray Source Repair Details Procedure

Upon receipt of a rental repair order, the Spray Center must keep track of hours worked and components used in the repair of the unit.

Step

Action

1

Access the customer order.

If

Then

Your location both created the order and is performing the repair.

From POS desktop, select Store -> Customer Order Entry -> Select Update/Review Orders from Customer Order Entry. Use the Order Lookup to search from your order.

You are a Spray Source Center.

Refer to Process Spray Source Transfer Repair Procedure  to access a repair order, which has been transferred to your location. Then, proceed with the following instructions.

Note: As a repair is being completed on a unit, the customer order should be updated continually to reflect work performed and charges accrued.

2

Once an employee has been assigned to repair a unit, that employee should update the customer order to show that employee in the Repair Employee Field in the Repair Window.

3

Update Problem Description to include the diagnosis of the unit.

Note: Do not forget that once a unit has been diagnosed, an estimate should be prepared and presented to the customer before work actually begins on the unit.

4

Did the customer accept the proposed work and estimate?

If Then
Yes Proceed to Step 5.
No Update the line items of the order to include charges for Shop Charge, cleaning, and diagnosis. Make arrangements to return the unit to the customer and charge the customer for the above services.

5

Prepare estimate for the customer.

6

Did the customer accept the estimate?

If Then
Yes Once repairs have commenced on the unit, update Corrective Action to include the work performed on the unit.
No Update the line items of the order to include charges for Shop Charge, cleaning, and diagnosis. Make arrangements to return the unit to the customer and charge the customer for the above services.

7

Include all parts and charges for the repair in the detail area of the order.

Note: Do not forget that repair orders must have the following service charges included: Cleaning Charge, Shop Charge, and Labor Charge.

8

Is the part covered by warranty and the customer will not be billed?

If Then
Yes

Set the Warranty Flag to W. Enter the Vendor Number or press LOOKUP [F3] for a listing of vendors.

Note: By setting this flag, the part will appear on the customer order but will not be pulled into the sale transaction for the customer.
No Proceed to Step 9.

9

Is the customer being charged for labor?

If Then
Yes Proceed to Step 11.
No Use the standard repair sales numbers.

10

Ensure that the quantity accurately reflects the labor hours performed for the repair. 

  • Adjust the price. 
  • You will not be able to discount the labor to $0.00. You will be able to discount the labor by 99%.
  • Press DISCOUNT [F11]. Select Other as the Discount Type. Enter the Percent as 99.00.

11

Did the repair involve a warranty claim?

If Then
Yes

Process a chargeback to the vendor to receive credit from the vendor.

  • Select Yes when prompted to create a Spray Warranty Claim. You will be required to enter information about the unit, customer, and original purchase. Do not forget to include labor charges and shop charges in your warranty claim chargeback.
  • Process a Store Expense Transaction for any parts used in the warranty repair that will not be reimbursed by the vendor using prime-sub 7402-104 (Spray Repair Misc).
No Refer to Spray Source Repair Order Billing Overview.
