Spray Source Standard Practices Policy

  1. Spray Source Repair Technicians should bill out all hours worked completely and accurately based on the time needed to perform all functions related to the repair.
    1. Billable hours are to be logged in Customer Order Entry using the standard repair labor sales numbers. Hours should be noted as the Qty for the line item. For example, 1 1/2 hours of repair would be a quantity of 1.5.
    2. Billable hours may include but are not limited to: initial diagnostics, research to determine needed parts, looking up parts and costs, checking stock, generating purchase orders, receiving purchase orders, updating the customer order in POS, completing the physical repair, cleaning, testing, and customer service tasks (initial repair paperwork in COE, customer phone calls, customer follow-up).
  2. Spray Source Repair Centers should optimize inventory:
    1. Stock a maximum of one hundred (100) repair parts.
    2. Do not stock parts which can be received second day from vendors.
    3. Stock no more than a two (2) month supply of high volume parts.
    4. Parts should be ordered as follows:
      1. Always use OEM parts for warranty repairs to maintain the warranty.
      2. For non-warranty repairs, you have the option of using OEM repair parts or repair parts from an alternate supplier. All repair parts should be sold at the list prices shown in the system and should not be discounted.
